## PART 1: What Was Spent in May 2026?

### The Event

- **Date:** April 30 – May 2, 2026 (3 consecutive business days)
- **Amount:** ¥5.4 trillion (~$34.5 billion)
- **Method:** MoF sold USD from FEFSA, bought yen in spot market
- **Trigger:** USD/JPY broke ¥160 "red line"
- **Official Count:** Counts as **ONE operation** under IMF rules (3-day intervention = 1 event)

### In Context

```
May 2026 single event:  $34.5B
Is larger than:
  ✓ 1998 record single-day: $26B
  ✓ Average 2024 per-occasion: $15.6B (¥2.4–4T each)
  
Is smaller than:
  ✗ 2024 total 4-week campaign: $62.3B
  ✗ 2022 October campaign: $42.8B
```

## PART 2: Historical Comparison — The Context Matters

### 1. The 1998 "Asian Crisis" Intervention

```
Date: April 10, 1998
Amount: ¥2,620 billion ($26 billion @ 100 yen/$)
Context: Massive selling of ¥ against USD
         (Yen strengthening was hurting exports)
         South Korean won collapsed
         Thai baht crashed
         Japan was protecting yen strength, not weakness

Type: OFFENSIVE (weaken yen to help exports)
Effectiveness: Moderate
   - Yen strengthened anyway (risk-off demand)
   - But single-day record held for 24 years
```

### 2. The 2003–2004 "Yen Weakening Period" (LARGEST EVER)

```
Period: 2003–2004 (15 months)
Total Amount: ¥35 trillion ($340 billion) 
% of GDP: ~7% (MASSIVE)
Monthly average: ¥2.3 trillion/month
Peak month (Q1 2024): ¥14.8 trillion sold in 3 months

Context: 
  - BOJ rates at zero (same as now)
  - Global carry trade starting to build
  - USD rising (Fed tightening after Iraq War)
  - Japan selling yen aggressively (OPPOSITE of 2022+)
  
Direction: MoF was SELLING YEN (buying USD)
  Why? To weaken yen and support exports
  Who was doing the trade? Japanese institutions 
         repatriating USD (not speculators shorting yen)

Effectiveness: FAILED
  - Yen still appreciated from ¥135 to ¥105 by 2007
  - Massive USD accumulation (reserves doubled)
  - But the trade itself was never profitable
```

### 3. The 2022–2024 "Yen Defending Period" (Current)

```
October 2022: ¥6.3 trillion ($42.8B) in 4 days
  Context: Yen weakened to ¥150+ for first time in decades
  Carry trade emerging (Fed @ 3.5%, BOJ @ 0%)
  Type: BUY YEN (defend, not attack)
  
May 2024: ¥9.8 trillion ($62.3B) over 4 weeks
  Context: Yen hit ¥160 again
  Carry trade in full swing (Fed @ 5%+, BOJ @ 0%)
  Type: BUY YEN (intensive campaign)
  4 separate occasions, ¥2.4–3.5T each
  
May 2026: ¥5.4 trillion ($34.5B) in 3 days
  Context: Yen hit ¥160 again (THIRD time)
  Iran war spiked oil prices
  Carry trade still attractive
  Type: BUY YEN (defensive)
```

## PART 3: Is $34.5B a Lot? The "Fair Level" Question

### Comparison 1: As % of FEFSA Reserves

```
FEFSA Total: ~$1.2 trillion (¥180 trillion)
May 2026 spend: $34.5 billion

Percentage: 2.9% of reserves (SMALL)

Comparison:
  2024 total campaign: $62B = 5.2% of reserves
  2022 campaign: $43B = 3.6% of reserves
  
Burndown rate: If Japan intervenes 3-4 times at this scale,
               they exhaust ~10% of reserves
               IMF rules allow only 2 more sessions by Nov 2026
```

### Comparison 2: As % of Japan's GDP

```
Japan GDP: ~$4.9 trillion (¥750T)

May 2026: $34.5B = 0.7% of GDP (MODERATE)
May 2024: $62.3B = 1.3% of GDP (SIGNIFICANT)
2003–04 total: $340B = 7% of GDP (UNPRECEDENTED)

For scale:
  - US GDP: $30 trillion
  - Equivalent 0.7% intervention: $210 billion
  - US has never spent that much in one day
  
Japan's reserves allow this, but it signals urgency.
```

### Comparison 3: As % of Daily FX Volume

```
Daily global FX volume: ~$6–7 trillion (May 2026)
Japan intervention: $34.5 billion

Percentage: ~0.5% of daily volume (TINY)

This means:
  ✓ Single intervention can't permanently shift the market
  ✓ Speculators can reload positions in days
  ✓ Why MoF had to intervene in 2024 FOUR times in one month
  ✓ Why Goldman Sachs said Japan can do ~30 more interventions
        (at $1B/day burn rate, they have runway)
```

## PART 4: Historical Lesson — The 2003–2004 Parallel

### Why That Period is the Warning

```
2003–2004: Japan spent ¥35 trillion over 15 months
           = ¥2.3 trillion/month average
           = $226 billion/year rate

May 2026: Japan spent ¥5.4 trillion in 3 days
          = ¥60 trillion/year ANNUALIZED (!!!)
          = $360 billion/year rate
          
This is 1.6x FASTER than the 2003–2004 record period!
```

### What Happened After 2003–2004?

```
2003–2004 Result: FAILED
  - Yen still appreciated
  - Massive USD reserves accumulated
  - No structural fix
  - Intervention had NO lasting effect
  
Why it failed:
  1. Fundamental: China was rising, demand for yen was structural
  2. Speculative: Japanese were repatriating, not trading
  3. Global: Dollar was weakening vs all currencies
  
Lesson: You can't fight the market indefinitely.
        Eventually, fundamental forces win.
        
2022–2026 Parallel:
  - Current: Carry trade is REAL (speculators, not institutions)
  - Current: Rate gap is structural (Fed tight, BOJ loose)
  - Current: No path to "fundamental fix" without Fed cooperation
  
If Fed doesn't cut, Japan faces 2003–2004 outcome:
  Massive spending → zero lasting effect → collapse
```

## PART 5: What is the "Fair Level" for Defending the Yen?

### Theoretical Fair Price

```
Yen should be worth: Based on PPP (Purchasing Power Parity)

Current reality:
  Nominal: ¥160/$1 
  PPP fair value: ¥110–120/$1
  Overvaluation of USD: 35–45%
  
MoF implicit target: ¥145–150/$1
  (The "red line" mentality)
  
This means:
  To defend ¥150, Japan must keep the carrying trade
  unprofitable. That requires:
  
  Rate gap < 200 bps
  
  If Fed @ 5%, BOJ must be @ 3.5% minimum
  Currently BOJ is @ 0–0.5%
  
  Distance to "fair": Need 300 bps of hikes
  But only 2 more intervention windows before IMF rules bite
```

### The Sustainability Question

```
Can Japan afford ¥5.4T per intervention?

FEFSA = ¥180T total

At ¥5.4T/event:
  - 33 events before reserves exhausted
  - But IMF limits to ~2 more by Nov 2026
  - So ¥5.4T x 2 = ¥10.8T spent
  - Leaves ¥169T (~$1.1T) remaining

After Nov 2026 (if IMF rules enforced):
  - No more interventions (would signal managed float)
  - ¥ freely floats against carry trade pressure
  - Likely: ¥175–200/$ unless Fed cuts or BoJ hikes
  - Imported inflation follows
```

## PART 7: Fair Level Conclusion

### What Price is "Fair" for the Yen?

```
ECONOMIC FAIR VALUE (PPP):     ¥110–120/$1
  Based on: Relative prices of goods between countries
  Implies: Current yen is 30–40% weak

MoF INTERVENTION RED LINE:       ¥160/$1
  Based on: "Too weak, hurts importers, inflation"
  Implies: Can't let depreciation run unchecked

CARRY TRADE EQUILIBRIUM:        ¥150–155/$1
  Based on: Where speculators find it risky to short
  Requires: BOJ rate @ ~3.5% (closing the gap)

THE PROBLEM:
  Fair value (PPP) = ¥110–120/$1
  MoF tolerance = ¥160/$1
  Carry equilibrium = ¥150–155/$1
  
  These don't align!
  
  If BOJ raises to make carry unprofitable:
    → Rate hike works, ¥ appreciates
    → But overshoots to ¥100–110
    → Then imports are cheap again
    → Boom/bust cycle
    
  If BOJ doesn't raise:
    → Carry trade unlimited
    → ¥ drifts to ¥200+
    → Imported inflation spiral
```

### The Real "Fair Level" Depends On

```
Scenario 1: Fed CUTS on May 15
  → New equilibrium @ ¥130–140/$1 (higher yen)
  → Japan's $34.5B intervention was premature
  → But useful for signaling commitment
  
Scenario 2: Fed signals NO cuts
  → New equilibrium @ ¥160–175/$1 (weaker yen)
  → May 2026 intervention was effective but temporary
  → Japan will need >$50B/event for next cycle
  → Eventually exhausts reserves
  
Scenario 3: Uncontrolled yen collapse
  → ¥200+/$1
  → Imported inflation forces BOJ emergency hike
  → JGB crash, fiscal scare
  → "Fair level" becomes irrelevant (crisis pricing)
```

## Conclusion

### May 2026 Intervention: By the Numbers

**Amount:** ¥5.4 trillion ($34.5 billion) — **MEDIUM SCALE**
- Smaller than May 2024 campaign ($62B)
- Larger than 1998 single-day record ($26B)
- 2.9% of total FEFSA reserves (sustainable for now)
- 0.7% of Japan's GDP (significant but not unprecedented)

**Importance:** **CRITICAL SIGNALING EVENT**
- First intervention in ~2 years
- Signals resolve but also desperation (rate gap unresolved)
- Test case before potential escalation

**Historical Context:** **FASTER BURN RATE THAN 2003–2004**
- 2003–04 annualized: $226B/year
- May 2026 annualized: $360B/year
- Suggests urgency is HIGH
- But 2003–04 ultimately FAILED to defend yen

**Fair Level:** **IMPOSSIBLE TO ACHIEVE WITHOUT FED COOPERATION**
- PPP fair value: ¥110–120/$1
- MoF tolerance line: ¥160/$1
- Carry trade equilibrium: ¥150–155/$1
- Only Fed rate cuts narrow the gap sustainably

**Verdict:** May 2026 intervention is a **temporary holding action** that buys Japan time until May 15 Fed decision. If Fed signals cuts, Japan escapes. If Fed stays tight, Japan enters escalation phase (>$50B/event) with limited runway.
