## Extracted Official Data

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ June 2026 MPM] The Policy Board decided by a 7-1 majority vote to encourage the uncollateralized overnight call rate to remain at around 1.0 percent, effective June 17, 2026.

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ June 2026 MPM] The complementary deposit facility rate was set at 1.0 percent, and the basic loan rate under the complementary lending facility was set at 1.25 percent.

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ June 2026 MPM] Voting for the rate action were Himino Ryozo, Uchida Shinichi, Nakagawa Junko, Takata Hajime, Tamura Naoki, Koeda Junko, and Masu Kazuyuki; Asada Toichiro dissented, and Ueda Kazuo was absent.

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ June 2026 MPM] BOJ stated that the risk of underlying CPI inflation deviating upward above the 2 percent price stability target had increased because crude-oil pass-through was progressing rapidly in business-to-business transactions and medium- to long-term inflation expectations had continued to rise.

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ June 2026 MPM] BOJ stated that it would continue to raise the policy interest rate and adjust the degree of monetary accommodation in response to economic activity, prices, and financial conditions.

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ JGB Purchase Plan] BOJ decided by a 7-1 majority vote to reduce planned monthly JGB purchases by about JPY 200 billion each calendar quarter until January-March 2027, then conduct monthly JGB purchases of about JPY 2 trillion from April 2027 onward.

[RAW-OFFICIAL:BOJ JGB Purchase Plan] BOJ also stated that if long-term interest rates rise rapidly, it can increase JGB purchases, conduct fixed-rate purchase operations, and use funds-supplying operations against pooled collateral regardless of the monthly purchase schedule.

## Working Interpretation

[LLM-S] The June 2026 package combined short-rate normalization with a predictable but flexible JGB-purchase path, so the policy mix tightened the overnight rate while preserving a market-stability backstop in the JGB market.
