## Extracted Data

[WEB-2026-07-02] Japan's 10-year JGB yield climbed above 2.7% on July 2, 2026, its highest level in three weeks, as sharp yen depreciation strengthened expectations of further BOJ tightening.

[WEB-2026-07-03] Japan's 10-year JGB yield rose to 2.75% on July 3, 2026, the highest since May 2026, driven by a weak 10-year JGB auction and concerns over rising fiscal spending and government borrowing.

[WEB-2026-07:AInvest/Oxford Economics] The 10-year JGB yield has risen from approximately 2.1% in March 2026 to a July 2026 level approaching 2.8%, described as nearing its highest since October 1996, attributed to market concern about Japan's more aggressive fiscal stance.

[WEB-2026-07:Yahoo Finance/AInvest] PM Sanae Takaichi's government plans a record initial FY2026 (fiscal year starting April) budget of approximately JPY 122.3 trillion (~$786 billion), roughly 6.3% larger than the prior year's JPY 115.2 trillion, funded in part through approximately JPY 29.6 trillion in new JGB issuance.

[WEB-2026-07:AInvest] The government's long-term economic strategy targets more than JPY 370 trillion ($2.29 trillion) in combined public and private investment through fiscal 2040, aimed at strategic industries; investors interpret the plan as implying materially greater future debt issuance.

[WEB-2026-07-01:CNBC] After hedging yen exposure back into dollars, 10-year and 30-year JGBs yield approximately 5.6% and 6.8% respectively — above comparable US Treasuries — meaning dollar-based investors can be compensated for JGB exposure via the interest-rate differential embedded in the FX hedge, distinct from unhedged yen-carry positioning.

[WEB-2026-07-02:Bloomberg] Overseas investors sold the largest net amount of Japanese government bonds in three years during June 2026, after the debt's return trailed most global sovereign peers.

[WEB-2026-05-20:Bloomberg] Foreign investors recorded a net outflow from Japan's super-long-maturity government bonds in a release around May 20, 2026 — the first such foreign net outflow in that tenor bucket since 2024.

[WEB-2026-07-03:Bloomberg] Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama repeated on July 3, 2026 that Japanese authorities are "ready to act on yen at any time as needed," including during US market holidays, and confirmed ongoing contact with US authorities (Treasury Secretary Bessent) on FX matters.

[WEB-2026-06-22:Bloomberg] Reports of direct Katayama-Bessent talks on June 22, 2026 raised market perception of intervention risk.

[WEB-2026-07-05:Eastern Herald] Analysis frames Katayama's consistent refusal to name a specific intervention threshold as deliberate policy — an unnamed trigger raises the cost of the market pricing a safe window to test, unlike a confirmed level which the market can test with known consequences.
