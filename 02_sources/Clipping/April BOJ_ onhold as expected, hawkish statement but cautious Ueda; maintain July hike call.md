**BOJ kept policy rate unchanged at 0.75%,** which I had been expecting over past several months (see my note on [oil shock and BOJ](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/oil-shocks-and-boj)), but had not been market expectation till recently. Market pricing for June and July meetings didn’t change much post April meeting.

**The statement seems to be a bit hawkish but I don’t see these as implying June as BOJ’s baseline in their mind at this point, especially combined with Ueda’s comments in the presser.**

1) **There are 3 dissenters called for hike** (3:6), including two hawkish members and another relatively dovish member (Nakagawa, whose term would end in June and be replaced by another new dovish member).

In presser, Ueda explained, **while other 6 members are concerned with upside in inflation as well,** **they think there is no urgency to raise rate immediately. Rather** **they wanted to watch unfolding of risk and more confidence in central forecasts.**

2) BOJ revised FY 2026 growth significantly lower while revised FY 2026 inflation materially higher, with downside risk in growth while upside risk in inflation, and **BOJ said to in particular pay attention to upside in inflation**.

**But even with significant upward revision in inflation forecasts, BOJ’s assessment around underlying inflation is unchanged,** still judging to achieve 2% target between the second half of FY 2026 and FY 2027.

As Ueda explained, major reason is that upward revision reflects temporary view due to higher oil prices, **implying impacts on underlying inflation at this point is not significant if any (consistent with my previous analysis)**, although Ueda mentioned to be mindful of potential risk. And **Ueda reiterated while underlying inflation is approaching 2%, it cannot be said they are completely anchored.**

And **when asked which risk to prioritize** —upside in inflation or downside in growth, **Ueda said at this point it is difficult to assess and they’d like to monitor a bit more closely** on impacts of middle east situation and whether risks will change.

3) BOJ revised forward guidance a bit, **adding it will consider timing and pace of adjustment** while closely monitoring impacts of future course of the Middle East situation.

**This primarily implies hike could be delayed but not derailed, but continuing to normalize policy has been BOJ’s baseline though timing is data dependent**. This is probably a response to some very dovish voices out there (e.g., in a recent survey, 30% of firms didn’t want any hike at all, up from 17% in January).

**I think Governor Ueda was more balanced than the statement in the presser, and remained relatively cautious, especially on wanting to wait for more data to assess risk distribution and underlying inflation not seeing significant upside risk (even headline inflation up materially), and didn’t give clear hint on timing of next hike.**

In March meeting, Ueda said in April meeting they would review their baseline scenario and balance of risks (on underlying inflation) to decide.

Now, though with baseline outlook significantly revised, **at this point there remained not sufficient information for BOJ to confidently judge direction and magnitude around balance of risks. When asked for reason for not hike in April, Ueda said confidence in central forecast has declined, and he intends to get additional information to confirm** if a major economic adjustment is coming or whether there are upside risks to underlying inflation.

And **Ueda said, while they expect headline inflation to go up significantly, BOJ aims to pursue policy to anchor underlying inflation or medium to long term inflation at 2%, and at present, he lacks confidence to assert definitely that they are anchored at 2%,** and medium to long term inflation has not jumped up significantly and they don’t think it has reached that point.

And Ueda suggested, even if central forecast accuracy is low, if upside risk to underlying inflation become quite evident and risk is increasing (e.g., due to secondary effect), and also if downside risk to economy is somewhat contained, there is possibility to hike.

And when asked around upside risk to inflation before June meeting, Ueda said he doesn’t think there will be significant upward pressure on CPI before June meeting, and inflation pressure will emerge a bit further out.

**So timing of next hike to a large extent depends on how much more information they need to have stronger confidence around their forecasts and risk assessment**.

**Before June meeting (June 16-16), major data point is only April CPI. And before July meeting (July 30-31), additionally, there would be May CPI, June BOJ Tankan, BOJ regional economic report, and final round of Shunto wage, and June CPI**. In addition, how the war and oil prices will develop will be also very important, along with FX.

**BOJ released core inflation indicators for March today that BOJ monitors to gauge underlying inflation** (along with composite medium to long term inflation expectation). **As consistent with BOJ’s assessment, these underlying inflation measures (trimmed mean, mode, weighted median, global core CPI excluding food and energy) was broadly stable in March and remained below 2%, similar as inflation expectation measure.**

And as shared previously, **recent data,** including government surveys, Reuters April Tankan, **suggest significantly negative impact from Iran war on consumer confidence and activity outlook.** And downward revision in recent rounds of Shunto wage was much larger than last year which could be related to impacts from the war.

**Overall, I maintained my call for next hike in July, with BOJ having more data to assess, though with decent probability for earlier hike in June. I still think in adverse scenario, BOJ could delay hike,** as Japan growth could slow down sharply, which could weigh on wage and underlying inflation significantly, **especially as we have seen stronger impacts of output gap on wage and larger impacts of wage on inflation expectation** (s [ee my analysis here](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/oil-shocks-and-boj)).
