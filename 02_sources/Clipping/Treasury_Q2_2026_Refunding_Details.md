## Bills Strategy

- 4-week bill issuances: ~**$101B per issuance** in 2026 (up from $47B avg in 2016)
- Bills represent >20% of marketable debt — **above TBAC 15–20% target range** (consequence of extraordinary measures Jan–Apr 2026)
- Strategy: maintain current portfolio composition "more indexed towards short-term issuances"
- Forward: coupon sizes held stable ("at least a few quarters" from May 2026 guidance)
- 2027 risk: TBAC notes "a need may arise for increased coupon issuance in 2027" as debt ceiling lift-related gross borrowing flows through

## Key Policy Signals from Bessent/Brian Smith

1. **"Regular and predictable"** — TBAC doctrine maintained; no surprise size changes
2. **Bills flexibility** — Short-end used as adjustment lever; coupon sizes stable
3. **Coupon sizes frozen for "at least a few quarters"** — gives market predictability through Q3/Q4 2026
4. **2027 escalation risk** — TBAC explicitly noted potential coupon ramp in 2027; this is the forward supply pressure signal for long-end term premium
5. **Buyback program ongoing** — Tentative buyback schedule published alongside refunding; off-the-run bond buybacks improve liquidity but don't reduce total supply
