## Key Finding 1 (revised) — True H1 extremes are April 8 → April 22, driven by tax season

The denser 23-point series moves the identified extremes: **April 22, 2026** is now the true **lowest reserves reading of H1** ($2,901.8B, below the previously-identified April 29 figure of $2,918.6B) and simultaneously the **highest TGA reading of H1** ($1,007.2B). More strikingly, the two weeks **April 8 → April 22** form a tight natural experiment around the April 15 individual income tax deadline:

| | Apr 8 | Apr 22 | Δ (2 weeks) |
|--|-------|--------|--------------|
| TGA | $697.1B (H1 low) | $1,007.2B (H1 high) | **+$310.1B** |
| Reserves | $3,116.2B (near H1 high) | $2,901.8B (H1 low) | **-$214.4B** |
| Total Assets | $6,693.9B | $6,707.4B | +$13.5B |
| Currency | $2,401.6B | $2,402.1B | +$0.5B (flat) |
| Reverse Repo | $344.9B | $325.1B | -$19.8B |

Full identity check for this window: `ΔReserves_predicted = ΔAssets - ΔTGA - ΔRRP - ΔCurrency = 13.5 - 310.1 - (-19.8) - 0.5 = -277.3B` vs actual ΔReserves = -214.4B. Residual ("other factors," e.g., Fed capital/surplus account, gold/SDR certificates, float) = **+$62.9B**, i.e., other factors partially offset the TGA-driven drain, but TGA remains overwhelmingly the dominant single term (-310.1 of a -277.3 predicted move). This is the cleanest, tightest-window empirical confirmation of the `ΔReserves ≈ RMP_trend − ΔTGA_event` identity in the dataset — a single fiscal event (individual tax settlement) visibly moving reserves by over $200B in two weeks with no intermediating buffer (ON RRP domestic ≈ $2.3B).

## Key Finding 2 (revised) — Empirical floor is even lower than previously identified

The April 22 reserves trough ($2,901.8B) is **~$100B below** the "~$3,000B ample floor" reference figure [WEB-2026-04-11: Andreopoulos, ~9% GDP]. April 2026 SOFR-IORB averaged **-11bp** for the month (low of month -18bp equivalent) [RAW-OFFICIAL: SOFR_IORB_Spread_H1_2026.md] — no dislocation signal registered during the week of the actual trough. The tested floor in H1 2026 is therefore **at least as low as $2,901.8B without triggering payment-system stress**; the $3,000B figure is confirmed as an upper-bound estimate, not an empirically observed trigger level.

## Key Finding 3 — Reserves and TGA bands are both wider than the initial 11-point sample suggested

With full density, the reserves band widens to **$227.8B** (was $192.9B) and the TGA band widens to **$310.1B** (was $204.5B). Total assets still grew smoothly and near-monotonically (+$162.0B, RMP trend intact), but the tax-season episode shows the TGA/reserves system can move over $300B/$200B respectively inside a single two-week window — a materially larger and faster swing than the "$100-200B/week" heuristic implied. This raises the bar for what "large enough to test the floor" means: the April episode did not break the floor, so an even larger or faster future TGA event (e.g., debt-ceiling resolution rebuild, or tax season coinciding with quarter-end FBO) would be needed to observe an actual dislocation.
