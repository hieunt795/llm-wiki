## Extracted Data

[WEB-2026:Insurance Business] Japanese life insurers became net sellers of super-long JGBs (maturity >10 years) in May 2026, with a net sale of approximately JPY 201.2 billion; this is characterized as intra-JGB portfolio rotation (selling low-coupon legacy holdings, reinvesting into higher-yielding new issuance), not international repatriation.

[WEB-2026:AInvest/CFR] With rising domestic JGB yields and persistently high FX hedging costs, hedged foreign-bond positions have become less attractive to Japanese institutional investors; for currency-unhedged foreign bonds, many insurers plan to hold steady or modestly decrease holdings rather than materially expand them.

[WEB-2026:CFR/Lambda Finance] Japan remains the largest single foreign holder of US Treasury securities, at approximately $1.2 trillion as of December 2025 (range approximately $1.079-$1.186 trillion through 2025) — holdings characterized in commentary as structural rather than tactical, i.e., not showing a rapid repatriation-driven decline through the observed period.

[WEB-2026:Lambda Finance/TIC] All foreign investors combined added a net $587 billion of Treasury securities over the trailing 12 months to early 2026, bringing total foreign UST holdings to a record $9.49 trillion; Japan represents about 12.8% of total foreign holdings of privately-held US federal debt, ahead of the UK (~$0.9tn) and China (~$0.7tn).

[WEB-2026] Long-tenor JGB yields show a tenor-specific pattern distinct from the 10-year move: the 30-year JGB yield reached a record 3.875% on January 20, 2026, then eased to approximately 3.6% by mid-2026 — i.e., the 30Y/40Y segment peaked earlier in the year and partially retraced, while the 10-year yield continued rising through H1 to a new high (~2.75-2.8%) by early July, concentrated around a weak 10-year auction and FY2026 budget concerns rather than a uniform curve-wide fiscal repricing.
