## 1. Quarterly Refunding H1 2026

### Q1 2026 — February 4, 2026 (Brian Smith, Deputy Asst Secretary)

- **Net marketable borrowing Q1 (Jan–Mar 2026):** $574B
- **TGA target end-Q1 (March 31, 2026):** $850B
- **Refunding package Feb 15 settle:** ~$90.2B of maturing notes/bonds refunded
- **Coupon sizes:** Maintained stable; no major changes announced
- Source: [home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0010] (timed out — data from search summary)

### Q2 2026 — May 6, 2026 (Brian Smith, Deputy Asst Secretary)

- **Net marketable borrowing Q2 (Apr–Jun 2026):** $109B
- **TGA target end-Q2 (June 30, 2026):** $900B
- **Refunding package:** $125B total
  - 3-year note: **$58B**
  - 10-year note: **$42B**
  - 30-year bond: **$25B**
  - New cash raised: ~$41.7B
- **Policy decision:** Nominal coupon and FRN sizes **UNCHANGED** — "at least the next several quarters"
- **Buyback:** Cash management buyback bucket (1M–2Y sector) trimmed from $75B → $25B
- **Settlement change:** 20-year bond reopenings → Friday-of-auction-week settlement starting June 16
- **Staging context:** All primary dealers expected no changes to nominal coupon, TIPS, or FRN issuance sizes
- Source: [home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0305]; [mariemontcapital.com/treasury-quarterly-refunding-may-2026-stagflation/]

## 5. OBBBA Fiscal Impact

*Source: CBO score; CRFB; Bruegel; BudgetLab Yale*

- **Debt ceiling raised by $5T** (from ~$36.1T to $41.1T)
- **CBO 10-year cost:** ~$3T to $3.9T added to debt (Senate version — higher than House)
- **With interest (if provisions permanent):** ~$5T
- **Gross debt crossed $39T:** March 17, 2026
- Main provisions: TCJA extension + increased defense/immigration spending + Medicaid cuts offsetting partially
