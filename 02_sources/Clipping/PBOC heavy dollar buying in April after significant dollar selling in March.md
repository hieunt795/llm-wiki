After being one of best performing currencies in Asia since last year, **RMB has continued to outperform since the Iran war and appreciated against dollar by 0.6%** — **it depreciated the least** and mildly **before the ceasefire in early April and has appreciated afterward though not the most.**

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k5zO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f89db03-b6f9-4058-9077-6d309ef5670f_757x388.png)

**Fundamental factors** like low beta to dollar, and smaller impact from term of trade shock **have contributed. Another major factor seems to be heavy intervention by PBOC** (and likely banks as well)—it helped mitigate depreciation in March but also slowed appreciation pace in April (along with significant counter-cyclical factor in daily fixing).

Based on my estimation, **FX reserve, adjusted for FX valuation effect, was down significantly** by USD 64bn **in March (i.e. selling dollar), and then up materially** by USD 51bn **in April (buying dollar), both the largest in recent years** (and apparently these cannot be explained by valuation from bond prices). Similar message was suggested for banks by FX settlement and banks’ foreign assets data in March (April data released later).

In addition to direct intervention, **counter-cyclical factor has been used heavily as well in April to mitigate appreciation pressure** (like January/February).

Overall, **these have reaffirmed PBOC’s conservative two-way FX approach** I elaborated [in previous note](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/rmb-part-2-pboc-unlikely-to-allow), and **recently it has turned more conservative (reflected in divergence between changes in daily fixing and contribution from market forces). As a result, we’ve again observed a combination of stronger RMB against dollar while weaker RMB against basket.**

**Another thing is it seems FX market behavior has shown some sign of increasingly counter-cyclical recently,** which is important to PBOC and FX market stability. As mentioned in previous note, China FX market had been broadly counter-cyclical since around 2018, but **this behavior has weakened since around 2024**, with FX sales ratio down to low levels amid a weak RMB environment. Over past several months, amid stronger RMB, FX sale ratio has picked up, but important thing to FX market stability is normalization in FX market behavior. **We may need more data to confirm if positive sign we observed recently could sustain. Of course, PBOC always monitors closely if it turns significantly pro-cyclical and would take measures if so.**

**With de-escalation in middle east conflict (hopefully), mildly stronger RMB against dollar this year driven by fundamental factors (dollar and BBOP) and PBOC’s continued conservative FX approach that I argued in previous notes (see [here](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/rmb-part-1-fundamentals-point-to) and [here](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/rmb-part-2-pboc-unlikely-to-allow)) is still intact.**
