RMB has been one of best performing currencies within Asia since H2 last year, with an acceleration in appreciation pace since late December, supported by strong seasonality in dollar supply before CNY holiday. Recently there have been some arguing for RMB to appreciate significantly, with RMB being materially undervalued cited as one major reason.

**To market, from forecasting perspective, key questions are 1) is valuation a good indicator to forecast direction/magnitude of FX change (say within one year which could be most relevant); 2) would fundamentals support significant appreciation in RMB; 3) would PBOC tolerate a significant appreciation in short period.**

Short answer is no to all those three questions. But in order to not get readers overloaded with a lengthy note, here I would focus on first two questions, and follow with another note on third question focusing on PBOC’s FX management approach.

**1) is valuation a good indicator for short-term FX forecast?**

I would not get into debate on how to appropriately assess if/how much FX is over/undervalued. Simply looking at IMF annual external assessment, REER of RMB was undervalued by 8.5% with a wide range of +/- 4.6% in 2024, with current account surplus above desirable level by 1.2pp (“moderately stronger “ per IMF, following several years of “broadly in line”). Magnitude of undervaluation could be larger in 2025 with higher current account surplus.

**However, empirically, valuation is a very poor indicator for FX forecasting**. One simple exercise is to look at if there is decent correlation between how much FX is under/over-valued (I use IMF estimates from external assessment reports for past five years) and how FX performs (say one year ahead). Basically no correlation is detected. This is probably not a surprise, but it is a surprise some use valuation as a major argument for short-term FX forecast even though it doesn’t work quantitatively.

**2) would fundamentals support significant appreciation in RMB?**

As a benchmark, **I run a simple regression of USDCNY on two major fundamental variables--basic balance of payment or BBOP** (current account+ direct investment +portfolio investment) **and broad dollar,** and **the model explains change in USDCNY pretty well.**

**In 2025, CNY appreciated against dollar by around 4%** with magnitude comparable to historical average for annual change since 2015, **on back of one of sharpest depreciation in dollar (around 10%), and modest increase in BBOP.**

**Current account surplus,** which market primarily focus on, **has surged to record high in 2025, but BBOP was up much less,** as capital account has been increasingly important to RMB in recent years, **with outflow in portfolio investment up to record high as well in 2025 (and outflow in direct investment still decent).**

For Q1-Q3 2025 (data we have), current account surplus was up sharply to USD 490bn vs 260bn Q1-Q3 2024 primarily due to surge in trade surplus (full year could be around USD 700bn in 2025 vs 420bn in 2024), but **outflow in portfolio investment** was up materially as well to 300bn vs 40bn, and this was **primarily due to record high China residents accumulation of foreign assets (both equity and bonds) and decrease in foreign inflow to China bond market. Foreign inflow to China equity remained subdued and only improved modestly (though with surge in equity market) and has been much smaller than other items (it is a misperception that equity market rally would lead to stronger RMB).** While FDI has seemed to be bottoming out recently following sharp drop in 2022-2024, it remained much lower than outbound investment, resulting in continued net outflow in direct investment (though less, 80bn for Q1-Q3 2025 vs 160bn). Combining these together, BBOP increased to 104bn vs 64bn.

**For 2026, while it is possible for dollar to weaken further, I would expect magnitude much smaller than 2025, and I am less bearish on dollar.** While dollar weakened significantly in 2025, it primarily happened in H1, with a modest appreciation in H2 (and most Asia currencies, which market had been bullish, has depreciated against dollar since H2). **Several key pillars for dollar bearish view last year, for instance, significant rise in hedging, exporters selling dollar deposit, [reallocation of assets out of US](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/data-didnt-support-selling-us-assets), basically haven’t not really happened or not as much as market had expected.** Question would be if this year could be different, with shallower dollar depreciation, probably narrower underperformance of US equity, of course with still elevated policy uncertainty. **At least we may again need a bit more data to examine.**

**For BBOP, it could stay decent on back of still strong current account surplus partially offset by continued portfolio outflow** amid efforts for capital account liberalization (which is important to RMB internationalization) to give residents more room to accumulate foreign assets (so called “藏汇于民”, especially amid strong RMB environment).

**Based on my benchmark model, fundamental factors suggest RMB could appreciate, but magnitude could be smaller than last year, unless BBOP increases materially or dollar sees another year of sharp depreciation.**

**Another fundamental variable that could particularly matter for RMB move in short term is market behavior in selling/purchasing dollar—whether it is procyclical or countercyclical** in a sense that whether market participants (e.g., exporters) would sell more dollar when dollar weakens (pro-cyclical).

**China FX market has been broadly counter-cyclical since around 2018** —when Rmb is weaker, FX conversion gets stronger so that market is self-stabilizing, and **this behavior has weakened since around 2024, with FX sales ratio down to low levels amid a weak RMB environment. Over past several months, amid stronger RMB, FX sale ratio has increased, but still relatively mild,** and more striking is significant drop in FX purchase ratio. If pace of RMB appreciation/dollar depreciation slows, even if procyclical behavior continues, **I don’t expect FX sales ratio to spike (and room for FX purchase ratio to drop further is also limited as it is already at low levels). And also it is possible market behavior could normalize back to be counter-cyclical.** And importantly, **PBOC is very mindful of significant accumulation of procyclical/herding behavior in market, and if they perceive this, they could take measures to guide expectation and mitigate.**
