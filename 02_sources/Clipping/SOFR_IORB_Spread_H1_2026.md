## Key Findings

**1. No SRF breach in H1 2026**
Max SOFR = 3.75% (Jan) = IORB exactly. Did not exceed SRF ceiling of 3.85%. Copeland-Duffie-Yang threshold for payment throttling impact: +1SD payment delay = +7bp SOFR-IORB → no clear cascade event in H1.

**2. January was the tightest month**
SOFR hit 3.75% = IORB in early January. This is consistent with year-end/quarter-start seasonality (FBO window-dressing reversal, new reserve requirements). Reserves at that time were already at ~$3T but swing into the new year from Q4-end FBO ($200B+) reduced effective supply temporarily.

**3. May anomaly: SOFR dropped to 3.50%**
SOFR fell 25bp below IORB in May → very ample reserves. Consistent with: (a) post-debt-ceiling TGA being rebuilt more slowly than expected, or (b) Treasury extending bill maturities → fewer near-term settlement pressures → reserves stayed high.

**4. Q2-end (June 30, 2026) risk pending**
As of June 25, SOFR-IORB at -11bp. Q2-end FBO window-dressing historically = ≥$200B reserve swing (Duffie, citing Crandall). With ON RRP domestic = ~$2.3B (no buffer), any large FBO demand could push SOFR toward IORB or higher on June 30.
