This is second part of my RMB outlook. [In my previous note,](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/rmb-part-1-fundamentals-point-to) I focused on fundamentals, which, based on my model, support RMB to appreciate this year, but milder than last year. In this note, I would focus on PBOC’s FX management approach--another key factor determining RMB moves.

**There has been a change in the way PBOC manages RMB since late 2023 towards being more conservative** (when Pan Gongsheng became PBOC governor), and it is not a coincidence that since 2022 maintaining FX stability has been mentioned in central economic working conference for four consecutive years (including in 2025).

Basically **anchor role of daily fixing (for spot) has increased significantly as PBOC has been heavily using counter-cyclical factor (CCF) so that daily fixing moves similar to a piecewise constant function or a linear function (a bit like crawling peg to dollar).** **Under this approach, slope of fixing is a key FX policy parameter (**with spot moving around -/+2% band).

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JY7_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fff545d9b-6e79-4ef1-834e-470d7a26d436_799x435.png)

**Slope or change in USDCNY fixing=deviation of spot from fixing in t-1+ change due to overnight dollar move+ CCF**

**CCF in theory is calculated by quoting banks based on first two market factors multiplied with CCF coefficient.** So CCF coefficient could be estimated as ratio of CCF to first two factors to gauge how strong PBOC’s intention in correcting market pressure. In general, when deviation of closing from fixing is small, CCF typically is muted.

So **slope of fixing depends on two factors--market forces and CCF coefficient.** If CCF coefficient is maintained unchanged, direction wise USDCNY fixing would move consistent with market forces, but with a discount reflected in CCF coefficient. And PBOC could affect slope through adjusting CCF coefficient, for instance, they could slow pace of appreciation by adjusting CCF coefficient higher, with same pace of market appreciation pressure.

Since late 2023 (before last year), USDCNY spot had persistently been weaker than fixing, amid deterioration in FX flow suggested by FX settlement data (turning significant and persistent deficit/outflow), and [FX sales ratio had dropped](https://zhennanli.substack.com/p/rmb-part-1-fundamentals-point-to) notably amid such weak RMB environment (and market turning a bit pro-cyclical). Daily fixing had been basically like a piecewise constant function with CCF offsetting market pressure.

**Early last year (Jan-April) dollar was down sharply by around 9%, but USDCNY fixing was roughly flat instead of being adjusted lower** (ie strong RMB), again through heavily using CCF. Then **since around May,** daily fixing started to appreciate persistently, and deviation of spot from fixing has also narrowed significantly (while dollar has been up and down), amid improving dollar supply (FX flow turning decent inflow/surplus). **In some sense, daily fixing has been catching up in recent months for the gap left early this year.**

**Since December, appreciation pace of daily fixing has accelerated, and broadly consistent with market factors** amid stronger seasonality in dollar supply and weaker dollar, and spot has been persistently stronger than fixing. And **PBOC has been using CCF to mitigate appreciation pressure (and CCF coefficient up a bit, reflecting PBOC intention to slow the pace).**

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vzwP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1e4c99ec-0d40-49c2-a801-15cc4f6744b0_771x426.png)

**In addition to CCF, PBOC could also affect FX market through other tools.** Direct intervention through FX reserve has decreased significantly in recent years, amid push for a more flexible RMB regime, but sometimes, PBOC may still do that based on my estimation of valuation adjusted FX reserve change (PBOC doesn’t disclose intervention). And also, PBOC could ask SOE banks as agents to intervene as well, which could be captured by monthly FX settlement data.

For instance, **if looking at FX valuation adjusted reserve change (which doesn’t capture valuation effect due to bond yields change), and compared it to change in treasury yields, there was a notable divergence in January this year and also October last yea** r and **PBOC accumulated FX reserve in these two months significantly, which may suggest PBOC has tried to mitigate appreciation pressure**. And **FX settlement data suggest banks helped in December to absorb dollar as well** with significant FX settlement surplus (though FX reserve didn’t change much).

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AxaQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6cd55586-7397-43bb-8e9a-3e1bbc702ed4_878x476.png)

**One consequence of shift to conservative FX management approach is correlation between CFETS and USDCNY has turned positive since late 2023** from negative for many years-- **the weaker RMB against dollar, the stronger RMB against basket. Sometimes it could still turn negative**, like last year for certain period, probably to have a bit more flexibility, **but not sustain**. This could hold if conservative approach continues, and could also serve as a check if FX approach turns more flexible in sustainable way. From macro perspective, since NEER matters more for trade growth, with such approach, so-called automatic stabilizer role of FX for growth could be weakened.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BORO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F953d6db6-67a9-48dc-ad36-c4f31c77075f_788x469.png)

**Overall, both CCF and FX reserve/settlement data (I construct a composite PBOC intervention proxy) suggest PBOC has tried hard in recent months to mitigate appreciation pressure,** consistent with conservative approach in recent years (and other tools could be also used if necessary), and **I think this may continue in coming months,** even though a bit flexibility could be added. **Along with fundamentals I examined in previous note, I think RMB could appreciate, but pace could be milder than last year.**
