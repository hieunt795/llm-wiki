## Thesis

Alexander Düring proposes a radical re-evaluation of personal wealth: by treating unconditional social claims (healthcare, welfare, education) as fixed income annuities, we can quantify the massive "invisible assets" held by the average citizen. This perspective challenges standard wealth disparity studies by recognizing that a person outside these systems would need to accumulate substantial private capital to purchase the same income/service stream.

## Thesis

The mathematical core of this perspective is that an income stream (annuity) is capitalizable into an asset value.

### 1. Literary Evidence: The Annuity View of Wealth
In historical literature, wealth was defined by its generated income, not its current market value:
- **Mr. Darcy (*Pride and Prejudice*):** Described as having £10,000 a year.
- **Sir Andrew Aguecheek (*Twelfth Night*):** Described as having three thousand ducats a year.
- **Insight:** Describing wealth through the annuity it generates provides a more accurate picture of a person's sustained social standing than a volatile market price. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=6]]

### 2. Case Study: The NHS as a £41,400 Asset
Düring analyzes the UK's National Health Service (NHS) using an annuity formula:
- **Annual Value:** The NHS budget per head in 2015/16 was approximately **£1,800/year**.
- **Assumptions:** A 30-year remaining life span and a 1.8% discount rate (the long-term yield at that time).
- **Asset Calculation:** Using the annuity formula (Eq 16.1), this stream is equivalent to an asset worth **£41,400**.
- **Conclusion:** By simply registering with the NHS, an average UK resident effectively acquires a fixed income asset of £41,400. This asset is only valuable if illness occurs, but its existence removes the need for private capital accumulation to buy such services. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=6]]

### 3. Case Study: German Welfare as a €127,000 Asset
- **Value:** A monthly stipend of €409 (2017) provided to single people with no dependents.
- **Asset Calculation:** A 30-year annuity of this magnitude has a present value of **€127,000**.
- **Insight:** A person with €127,000 in savings would be considered well-off, yet few consider the welfare system as a "wealth asset" of the recipient. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=6]]

## Mechanism

### The Creditor-Debtor Reversal
These social assets have a physical counterpart on the **National Balance Sheet**:
- **Claimants:** Function as virtual creditors, holding claims on fixed income streams from society.
- **Taxpayers:** Function as the ultimate contingent debtors, as these liabilities must be backed by corresponding tax assets or government debt. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=7]]

### Optimal Redistribution and the Laffer Curve
Düring notes that this relationship has no obvious welfare optimum.
- **Under-redistribution:** Immoral and ruinous as it destroys potential consumers.
- **Over-redistribution:** Discourages producers from earning taxable income. The optimal point depends on the form of redistribution, not just the magnitude. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=7]]
