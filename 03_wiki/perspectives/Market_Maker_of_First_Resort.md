## Thesis

Traditional central banking focuses on being the **Lender of Last Resort (LoLR)** to individual banks. Post-GFC, the focus has shifted to the entire **Payment and Settlement System**. If the "plumbing" stops, even solvent banks fail. The central bank acts as the ultimate liquidity guarantor for the system as a whole.

## Mechanism: Preventing Gridlocks

- **Intraday Liquidity:** RTGS systems require massive amounts of liquidity to settle payments throughout the day. The central bank provides this liquidity (often interest-free if returned by EOD) to prevent a "Settlement Gridlock."
- **Asset Purchase during Stress:** When interbank markets freeze, the CB may step in to buy high-quality assets (repo or outright) to provide the market with the "Outside Money" it desperately needs.

## Boundaries / Conditions

### Moral Hazard
By acting as the MMFR, the central bank risk-protects market participants. If banks know the CB will always ensure liquidity, they may under-invest in their own private liquidity buffers (LCR/NSFR).
