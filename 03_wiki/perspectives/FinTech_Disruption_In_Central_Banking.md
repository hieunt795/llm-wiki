## Thesis

The rapid emergence of FinTech (e.g., P2P lending, e-money, digital banking) is a "Double-Edged Sword." While it supports financial deepening, especially for MSMEs, it challenges the central bank's ability to monitor the full financial cycle and protect monetary transmission channels.

## The Dual Impact

### 1. Benefits (Financial Deepening)
- **Outreach:** Technologies directly present services to consumers, bypassing traditional bank friction.
- **Efficiency:** Automation of processes and administration.
- **Inclusion:** Facilitating credit to micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSME) through P2P platforms.

### 2. Risks (Systemic Fragmentation)
- **Blind Spots:** Innovation occurring *outside* formal financial institutions makes it harder for central banks to track leverage and credit growth.
- **Contagion:** A failure of a large unregulated FinTech participant could spill over into the formal banking system (Market Liquidity Risk).
- **Payment Security:** Increased reliance on mobile/internet banking necessitates higher standards for cyber-resilience and public confidence.

## The Central Bank's Response
Central banks must expand their "Vigilance" and develop "Prudential Regulation" specifically for the FinTech sector to ensure that digital innovation does not compromise financial system stability.
