## Key Decision & Immediate Context

**April 28, 2026 BOJ Monetary Policy Committee Meeting:**
- **Decision:** Policy rate held at **0.75%** (no change from March)
- **Dissent:** **3 board members** called for an immediate rate hike (vs. 6 in favor of hold)
  - Two hawkish dissenters (structural inflation concern)
  - One dovish dissenter (Nakagawa, whose term ends June → replaced by dovish successor) [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- **Policy Statement:** Hawkish tone BUT Governor Ueda's presser contradicted with caution
- **Analyst Call:** July 2026 as baseline next hike; June possible with decent probability; adverse scenario → delay into Aug+ [RAW-CLIP: Li]

---

## Mechanism: Three Tensions in the Hold

### 1. Headline vs. Underlying Inflation Disconnect

**Statement Signal (Hawkish):**
- FY 2026 inflation forecast **revised materially higher** (oil price pass-through)
- FY 2026 growth forecast **revised significantly lower** (Middle East war impact on confidence, FX pressure)
- BOJ stated to "pay attention to upside in inflation" [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Ueda's Clarification (Cautious):**
- Upward inflation revision is **temporary, driven by oil prices**
- "At this point, underlying inflation impact is not significant if any" — implies core CPI excluding energy/food remains subdued [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- **Underlying inflation NOT seen as anchored at 2%:** "Cannot be said they are completely anchored" [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- Medium-to-long-term inflation expectations remain stable below 2%, no evidence of de-anchoring [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Transmission Chain:**
```
Oil price shock (Middle East) 
  → Headline CPI ↑ (3–6 month lag)
  → Ueda's concern: Will this pass through to wage-setting? (Shunto data weak: downward revision larger than prior year)
  → If no wage pass-through → underlying inflation stays <2% → no urgency to hike now
  → If wage acceleration emerges → re-evaluate June/July
```

**Condition for Hold:** Pass-through to **core inflation or inflation expectations** is not yet evident. BOJ lacks confidence in its central forecast (Ueda: "confidence in central forecast has declined"). [RAW-CLIP: Li]

### 2. Growth Downside vs. Inflation Upside Risk Calibration

**Data Point:** April 2026 revision flagged **downside risk to growth** (war-driven confidence collapse, FX headwinds) while **upside risk to inflation** (oil prices). These are at odds.

**Ueda's Balancing Act:**
- When asked which risk to prioritize, Ueda said: **"At this point, it is difficult to assess"** — we need more data on Middle East developments and output impacts [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- Implication: Hiking now (to fight upside inflation) risks amplifying output weakness if war escalates or confidence collapses further
- Wait strategy: Gather June data (April CPI, regional surveys, Shunto final numbers) before committing

**Evidence of Output Weakness:**
- Recent surveys show 30% of firms **don't want any hike at all** (up from 17% in January) — corporate caution [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- Shunto wage negotiations showed **larger downward revisions vs. prior year** — firms protecting cash despite inflation [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- This suggests output gap is widening, not closing → wage pressure is low despite headline inflation [RAW-CLIP: Li]

### 3. Data Dependency & Forecast Uncertainty

**What Ueda Signaled:**

> "**Confidence in central forecast has declined.** He intends to get additional information to confirm if a major economic adjustment is coming or whether there are upside risks to underlying inflation." [RAW-CLIP: Li]

This is **critical**. Ueda is explicitly saying the **regime is uncertain**:
- Scenario A (Baseline): Oil shock is temporary, underlying inflation stays <2%, growth normalizes → July hike
- Scenario B (Adverse): War escalates, FX pressure intensifies, wage deflation sticks → August+ hike or no hike in 2026
- Scenario C (Surprise): Secondary effects of oil pass through to wage expectations suddenly → June emergency hike

**Data Needed Before June (June 16–17 meeting):**
- April CPI (released ~May 30, reviewed ~mid-June) [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Data Needed Before July (July 30–31 meeting):**
- May CPI
- June BOJ Tankan (corporate sentiment survey)
- BOJ Regional Economic Report
- Final Shunto wage data (June settlement round)
- June CPI [RAW-CLIP: Li]
- **Geopolitical trajectory** (war escalation vs. stabilization)
- **FX stability** (JPY weakness from war risk → import inflation feedback loop) [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Ueda's Statement:** "Timing of next hike to a large extent depends on how much more information they need to have stronger confidence around their forecasts and risk assessment." [RAW-CLIP: Li]

### 4. Forward Guidance Revision & Interpretation

**Formal Change:** BOJ revised forward guidance to state it will **"consider timing and pace of adjustment while closely monitoring impacts of future course of the Middle East situation."** [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Market Interpretation (Dovish):** This delays hikes.

**Li's Interpretation (Balanced):** "This primarily implies **hike could be delayed but not derailed.** Continuing to normalize policy has been BOJ's baseline though timing is data dependent." [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Nuance:** The insertion of geopolitical language suggests:
- Hikes are still planned (not derailed) but clock is paused pending geo-data
- If Middle East stabilizes → back to normalcy timeline (July or Aug hike)
- If war escalates further → August+ or potential delay into 2027 [RAW-CLIP: Li]

## Ueda's Framing of Hike Possibility

Despite the hold, Ueda left a door open:

> "Even if central forecast accuracy is low, **if upside risk to underlying inflation become quite evident** and risk is increasing (e.g., due to secondary effect), and also **if downside risk to economy is somewhat contained**, there is **possibility to hike**." [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Translation:** Three-part test for emergency June hike:
1. **Clear evidence of wage acceleration** (secondary pass-through) → upside inflation risk "quite evident"
2. **Output doesn't collapse further** → downside risk "contained" (e.g., via fiscal support or war de-escalation)
3. **Confidence in forecast improves** → forecast "sufficiently reliable" to commit

**Current Status (late April 2026):** None of these three are met yet. Hence hold and wait.

## Analyst Baseline & Risks (Zhennan Li)

### Baseline (70% probability)
- **Next hike: July 30–31, 2026** (end of BOJ fiscal year Q1, natural timing window)
- Data in May–June confirms underlying inflation concern is manageable + growth stabilizes → Ueda gains confidence
- BOJ hikes to 1.0% (50 bp move) [RAW-CLIP: Li]

### Upside Risk (20% probability)
- **June 16–17 hike** if April CPI print shows unexpected core acceleration or wage signal shifts
- Requires: May Shunto data and April CPI joint evidence of pass-through [RAW-CLIP: Li]

### Downside Risk (10% probability)
- **Hike delayed beyond July** if:
  - War escalates (e.g., broader Middle East conflict → energy supply shock) [RAW-CLIP: Li]
  - Growth surprise sharply to downside (corporate capex cancellations, FX-driven export shock)
  - Output gap overwhelms inflation concern (Japan's labor market shows **stronger impacts of output gap on wage than historical norm**) [RAW-CLIP: Li]

**Key Uncertainty:** Japan's labor market regime may have shifted post-COVID. Wage responsiveness to inflation shocks is lower than in developed peers (US, Euro area). This means inflation durability depends more on output gap closure than wage acceleration. [RAW-CLIP: Li]
