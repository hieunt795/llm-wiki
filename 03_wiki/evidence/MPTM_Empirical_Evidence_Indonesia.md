## Thesis

Indonesia serves as a laboratory for MPTM evolution in Emerging Markets. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự lan tỏa: các cú shock chính sách không truyền dẫn tức thì mà phải vượt qua các "điểm nghẽn" về cấu trúc ngân hàng và tâm lý thị trường. Dữ liệu thực nghiệm chứng minh rằng lạm phát tại Indonesia phản ứng chậm nhất (mất tới 2 năm), đòi hỏi Bank Indonesia phải có tầm nhìn cực xa khi điều hành lãi suất SBI. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=151]]

## 1. The Lag Profile (Table of Persistence)

Empirical findings (Warjiyo, 2004; Natsir, 2008) establish a clear timeline for transmission in Indonesia:

| Transmission Step | Duration (Lag) | Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SBI Rate $\to$ Interbank (PUAB)** | 1–2 months | High |
| **Interbank $\to$ Deposit Rate** | 3–4 months | Medium |
| **Interbank $\to$ Lending Rate** | **4–6 months** | Low (Contractual rigidity) |
| **Interest Rate $\to$ Real Output** | 4–5 quarters | Varies by sector |
| **Interest Rate $\to$ Inflation** | **6–8 quarters** | Cumulative 63.1% variance |

[[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=152]]

## 2. Structural Evolution: Pre- vs. Post-Crisis

### A. The Credit-Dependent Era (Pre-1997)
- **Dominant Channel:** Bank Lending.
- **Mechanism:** Direct credit quantity controls and high dependence on bank financing.
- **Vulnerability:** Banks could access offshore funds easily, making domestic interest rate hikes less effective.

### B. The ITF Era (Post-2005)
- **Dominant Channels:** Interest Rate and Exchange Rate.
- **Key Finding:** Adoption of the **Inflation Targeting Framework (ITF)** halved the exchange rate pass-through (ERPT) from 6% to 3% for every 10% depreciation.
- **Credibility:** Anchored expectations reduced the "Price Inertia," making the interest rate a more surgical tool. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=151-152]]

---

## 3. The "Flight to Quality" Phenomenon

Perry highlights a unique distributive effect in Indonesia during monetary tightening:
- **National Private Banks:** Highly sensitive to rate hikes; they face rapid credit rationing and deposit flight.
- **Foreign & Joint Venture Banks:** Insensitive to SBI rate changes. During tightening, depositors move funds *to* these banks for safety.
- **Inference:** This "Flight to Quality" creates a dual-speed economy where small firms (reliant on private banks) are squeezed while large firms (using foreign banks) remain insulated. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=154]]

---

## Strategic Implications: Policy Mix Necessity

The long lag of inflation (up to 24 months) means Bank Indonesia cannot rely on interest rates alone for immediate stability.
- **The Solution:** BI uses **FX Intervention** for short-term price stability (Direct ERPT) and **Macroprudential** policy (like LTV) to address the procyclical lending behavior that the interest rate channel misses. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=157]]
