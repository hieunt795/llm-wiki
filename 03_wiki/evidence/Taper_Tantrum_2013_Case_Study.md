## Thesis

Perry Warjiyo uses the **Taper Tantrum** as the opening example of the book to illustrate the "New Normal" for central bankers in Emerging Markets: a world where domestic stability is constantly threatened by external policy shifts from advanced economies.

## The Event (May 2013)

- **Trigger:** Fed Chair Ben Bernanke hinted that the Fed might begin "tapering" (reducing) its ultra-loose bond-buying programme (QE) adopted after the 2008 GFC.
- **Reaction:** Global investors, anticipating higher US yields, rapidly pulled capital out of EMEs (The "Flight to Quality").
- **Impact on Indonesia:** Massive depreciation of the Rupiah, spikes in government bond yields (SUN), and a sharp drop in stock prices.

## Central Bank Response (Bank Indonesia)

The tantrum forced a paradigm shift in BI's policy:
1. **Rate Hikes:** BI aggressively raised the policy rate (BI Rate) to defend the currency and anchor inflation expectations, despite stable domestic macro conditions.
2. **Policy Mix Initiation:** Recognition that interest rates alone could not manage the shock. BI began coordinating interest rates with FX intervention and macroprudential measures (like adjusting LTV ratios) to stabilize the system.

## Invaluable Lessons

- **Price Stability is Not Enough:** Even if domestic inflation is low, external shocks to the exchange rate can disrupt financial system stability.
- **The Trilemma in Practice:** Demonstrates the difficulty of maintaining an independent monetary policy when capital flows are perfectly mobile.
