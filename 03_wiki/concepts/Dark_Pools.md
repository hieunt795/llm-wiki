## Thesis

Dark pools serve as a structural alternative to public exchanges by removing **pre-trade transparency**. They allow market participants, particularly institutional investors and dealers, to match orders anonymously. This is crucial for executing large volumes without alerting the wider market and incurring adverse price movements (information leakage). [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]

## Definition

- **Mechanism:** A matching structure that does not publish an order book. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]
- **Function:** Enables trading but intentionally does **not** serve the purpose of public price discovery.
- **Ownership:** Often operated by exchanges as an alternative tier or by large dealers as internal match-making services.

## Mechanism

- **Conflict with Transparency:** Regulators often view dark pools with skepticism as they can obscure the true depth and price of the market.
- **Dealer Use:** Market makers use dark pools to find matches within their own client base before committing their own balance sheet to an OTC trade.
