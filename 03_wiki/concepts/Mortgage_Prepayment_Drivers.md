## Thesis

Prepayment is the "monster" of mortgage valuation. Alexander Düring explains that while interest rate models can project yields, they struggle to capture human behavior. Prepayment risk is the uncertainty of when a borrower will exercise their "Short Call Option" (the right to repay at Par). This behavior is driven by four distinct categories of factors. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch28.md#page=290]]

## Taxonomy of Drivers

### 1. Economic Prepayments (The Refinancing Incentive)
- **Mechanism:** Borrowers refinance when market rates are lower than their existing coupon.
- **Fixed Costs:** Refinancing involves notary and bank fees. Because these are often fixed, **larger loans** tend to prepay faster than smaller ones (as the interest saving justifies the fee earlier). [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch28.md#page=290]]

### 2. Non-Economic Prepayments (Attrition/Hao mòn)
Early redemptions without an economic profit motive.
- **Drivers:** Selling a home (moving for a job or family size), divorce, fire damage, or death of the borrower.
- **Seasonality:** Voluntary moves (and even mortality) have clear seasonal patterns, making prepayments higher in summer months in most climate zones. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch28.md#page=290]]

### 3. Behavioral Phenomena

#### The Burn-out Effect
- **The Observation:** After a prolonged period of low rates, the speed of prepayments declines even if rates stay low.
- **The Reason:** The pool is "burned out"—cost-sensitive and credit-worthy borrowers have already refinanced and left the pool. Those remaining are either uninterested or structurally unable to refinance (e.g., declining property values or poor credit scores). [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch28.md#page=291]]

#### The Newspaper Effect (Cú hích truyền thông)
- **The Observation:** A spike in refinancing occurs just as interest rates appear to be on the verge of a **rise**.
- **The Reason:** Media coverage warns of the imminent loss of attractive refinancing options. This "call to action" triggers borrowers who were previously passive to refinance before the window closes. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch28.md#page=291]]

### 4. Non-Economic Non-Prepayments
Situations where an incentive exists but the borrower *cannot* act.
- **Credit Constraint:** Declining FICO scores or property values (negative equity) make new loans unavailable.
- **Result:** Refinancing rates never reach 100%, even in the most advantageous circumstances. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch28.md#page=291]]
