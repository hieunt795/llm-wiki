## Summary

[RAW-BOOK:5] Market risk = sensitivity to changes in market prices (interest rates, FX, equity, volatility). Two types of financial risk: credit risk (inability/unwillingness to repay) and market risk. Key insight: risk quality ≠ risk magnitude. High-risk trade good if returns substantial (e.g., OTM options with 10× payoff). Low-risk trade bad if returns minimal. Portfolio context critical: trade value depends on marginal contribution, not standalone payoff. Buy puts to hedge equities = smart move despite expected premium loss (portfolio diversification benefit).
