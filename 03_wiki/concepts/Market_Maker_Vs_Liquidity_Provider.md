## Thesis

Market makers and liquidity providers are the backbone of OTC markets, but their roles differ in formality and commitment. A **market maker** explicitly advertises their willingness to provide prices, while a **liquidity provider** is any participant who is generally willing to trade in response to price movements. Crucially, the latter role is often less formal and can shift from providing to consuming liquidity during market stress. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]

## Definition

### 1. Market Maker
- **Role:** Advertises a willingness to provide prices for specific instruments on a continuous basis. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]
- **Mechanism:** Typically provides hai mức giá (Two-way quotes): **Bid** (giá sẵn sàng mua) và **Ask/Offer** (giá sẵn sàng bán).
- **The Law of Two Prices:** [RAW] Neftci nhấn mạnh chênh lệch Bid-Ask là nguồn thu nhập cốt lõi để bù đắp chi phí lưu kho (inventories) và rủi ro nắm giữ tài sản.
- **Designated Market Maker (DMM):** [RAW] Hệ thống tại NYSE (trước đây là Specialists), nơi một cá nhân/đơn vị chịu trách nhiệm chính trong việc duy trì thị trường công bằng và trật tự, phải đứng ra mua/bán khi có sự mất cân đối tạm thời giữa lệnh mua và bán của khách hàng.

### 2. Liquidity Provider
- **Role:** Any participant willing to trade in response to price movements. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]
- **Informal Nature:** May not advertise or have a formal obligation to provide quotes.
- **Crisis Behavior:** Some participants act as liquidity providers in normal environments but become **liquidity consumers** (seeking to exit positions) during crises. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]

### Boundaries / Conditions

- **Client Selection:** Market makers typically provide firm prices only to known clients to protect against adverse selection and information leakage. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=86]]
- **Institutional Incentives:** Exchanges and issuers may employ financial incentives to ensure that market makers maintain a presence in the order book. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=87]]
