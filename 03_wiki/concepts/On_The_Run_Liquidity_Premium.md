## Thesis

In many bond markets, liquidity is not distributed evenly across the yield curve but is "lumped" into specific securities. Alexander Düring explains that the **On-the-run Premium** measures this concentration. For an investor with a short holding period, transaction costs are the primary concern, making them willing to accept a lower yield (higher price) for the most liquid benchmark bond. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch19.md#page=185]]

## Mechanism: The Benchmark Anchor

### 1. The US Treasury Model
Traders and hedgers primarily use the **On-the-run (OTR)** bond (the most recently issued bond of a certain tenor) to express their views on data releases.
- **Concentration:** Because everyone else is trading the OTR, it has the deepest market and lowest bid-ask spread.
- **The Premium:** This "winner-take-all" liquidity leads to the OTR trading at a significantly lower yield than the **Off-the-run** bonds issued just months prior. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch19.md#page=186]]

### 2. The European/Japanese Contrast
Interestingly, German (*Bunds*) and Japanese (*JGB*) markets do not exhibit a persistent OTR premium of the same magnitude.
- **The Reason:** In these markets, price discovery and duration hedging take place predominantly in **Bond Futures** rather than the physical cash bonds.
- **The Result:** The physical bonds are essentially "deliverables" for the futures contracts, and liquidity is distributed across the **Cheapest-to-Deliver (CTD)** basket rather than being anchored to the newest issuance. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch19.md#page=187]]

## Strategic Implications: Measuring Liquidity

The OTR premium is itself a real-time indicator of market health:
- **Calm Times:** If investors do not expect a need for rapid portfolio adjustments, the premium shrinks as they prioritize income (yield) over liquidity.
- **Crisis Times:** In periods of uncertainty (e.g., the 2019 repo squeeze or the 2020 COVID outbreak), the OTR premium spikes as investors scramble for the absolute safety of the benchmark "anchor." [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch19.md#page=186]]
