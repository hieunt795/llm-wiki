## Thesis

Why do EMEs like Indonesia take years to lower inflation while advanced countries do it in months? The answer is **Imperfect Credibility**. If a central bank is not fully trusted, agents remain backward-looking, and a sudden rate hike to kill inflation will only result in a massive recession rather than a shift in expectations.

## Strategic Choices

### 1. Cold Turkey (High Credibility)
- **Approach:** Rapid, aggressive reduction in inflation.
- **Context:** Advanced economies where expectations are already anchored to low inflation.
- **Result:** Minimal output loss because agents immediately believe and adapt to the new target.

### 2. Gradualism (Low/Developing Credibility)
- **Approach:** Incremental reduction (e.g., Indonesia's -0.5% per annum path).
- **Context:** Emerging markets with a history of double-digit inflation.
- **Result:** Provides a "Learning Period" for economic agents. It accepts a longer duration of inflation to keep the **Sacrifice Ratio** at a socially tolerable level.

## Research Insight: Indonesia's Path
Indonesia chose **Gradualism** post-2005. The disinflation rate of -0.5% was slightly lower than the EME average of -0.7%, reflecting the time needed for the public to "convince themselves" that BI was serious about the new ITF regime.
