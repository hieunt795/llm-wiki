## Thesis

Covered Bond ratings rely explicitly fundamentally on a simple dynamic buffer: the **Cover Pool** mathematically must absolutely physically hold completely more physical loan value than the absolute sum of physical Covered Bonds outstanding. This excess buffer is known broadly as **Overcollateralization (OC)**. 

While OC mathematically flawlessly insulates the covered bondholder against loan deterioration, it introduces a severe zero-sum structural tension directly into the absolute core of the banking system. The physical OC buffer physically absolutely has to be explicitly funded by something. Because it is natively entirely in excess of the Covered Bond liability, the OC is forcefully strictly funded completely by the explicit collective deposits of ordinary citizens, senior unsecured creditors, and shareholder equity.

## Mechanism

### 1. The Dynamic Replenishment Trap
The safety of the Covered Bond demands strict continual preservation. If explicitly $5\%$ of the physical mortgages specifically inside the locked Cover Pool fall rapidly into arrears (default), the specific Bank is physically legally required absolutely immediately to aggressively physically rip the specific bad loans entirely out of the Cover Pool violently, and aggressively replace them perfectly strictly with fresh, fully performing physical loans aggressively pulled from the general open balance sheet.
- *The Consequence:* The general, totally un-pledged aggregate balance sheet physically entirely absorbs $100\%$ of the absolute localized toxic credit rot, explicitly synthetically transferring the highest-grade performing baseline assets exclusively dynamically into the locked ring-fenced Covered system. 

### 2. Asset Encumbrance and Depositor Ruin
When the bank entirely fundamentally eventually suffers wholesale catastrophic insolvency, the Covered Bond holders are perfectly entirely whole due specifically to their ring-fenced $110\%$ OC lock. The explicit Unsecured Depositors (retail accounts, businesses, and standard bondholders) strictly inherit absolutely exclusively the exact remaining "residual" unencumbered aggregate balance sheet. 
- *The Void:* By definition, because the absolute most flawless ultra-high-quality physical performing mortgage loans were systematically methodically stripped and permanently pledged exclusively to the Covered Bond pool, the residual aggregate unencumbered balance sheet is physically a toxic, heavily deteriorated wasteland. 
- *The Regulatory Squeeze:* Regulators view high covered bond issuance with extreme alarm precisely due to this **Asset Encumbrance** effect. The more covered bonds a specific bank structures over time, the fundamentally structurally worse the physical systemic recovery rate becomes strictly for the sovereign FDIC/taxpayer deposit-guarantee insurance funds upon failure. 

### The Economic Cap on OC

Because OC is structurally completely mathematically funded specifically by more wildly expensive generic Unsecured Debt (or Equity), the bank precisely faces an arithmetic limit. Plunging $200\%$ OC into a pool makes the covered bonds insanely cheap to fund, but mathematically wildly dramatically skyrockets the explicit necessary risk premium demanded aggressively by the bank's Unsecured Bondholders, who logically instantly explicitly see they are being structurally massively legally hollowed out. Total integrated holistic funding costs rigidly physically constrain total OC caps.
