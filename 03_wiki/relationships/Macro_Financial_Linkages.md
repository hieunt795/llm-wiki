## Thesis

The GFC exposed a massive "Analytical Gap." Governments were looking at macro indicators (Inflation/Fiscal), and supervisors were looking at individual banks, but no one was looking at the **Linkages**. These linkages turn a small shock into a systemic collapse.

## The Four Dimensions of Linkage (Figure 14.1)

1. **Microfinancial Sector:** Soundness of individual banks (Capital, NPLs).
2. **Macronational:** Domestic stability (Inflation, Fiscal deficit).
3. **Macrointernational:** External resilience (Capital flows, FX reserves).
4. **Macro-Financial Linkages:** The interaction between the three above. This includes:
    - **Procyclicality:** How GDP growth fuels credit booms.
    - **Contagion:** How a currency shock (International) triggers a banking crisis (Micro).
    - **Herding:** How information flows across dimensions to trigger panic.

## Transmission Channels of Instability

- **The Price Channel:** Asset bubbles (Property/Stocks) inflate collateral values, encouraging macro-overheating.
- **The Quantity Channel:** Credit booms provide the "fuel" for macro-imbalances.
- **The Liquidity Channel:** A freeze in the interbank market (Micro) halts the payment system (Infrastructure) and stops economic trade (Macro).
