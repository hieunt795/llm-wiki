## Thesis

The standard **Monetary Trilemma** suggests that if you have a floating exchange rate, you can have both free capital movement and independent monetary policy. Maurice Obstfeld (2015) and Helene Rey (2013), as cited by Perry, argue this is a myth. The reality is a **Financial Trilemma**.

## The Evolution

### 1. Standard Monetary Trilemma (The Triangle)
- **Choice:** Pick 2 of 3 (Fixed Rate, Free Capital, Independent Policy).
- **Key Assumption:** Flexible exchange rates act as a "shock absorber" that insulates the domestic economy.

### 2. The Financial Trilemma (The Square/Quadrilemma)
- **Argument:** Global spillovers through long-term interest rates and risk premiums are so strong that they "bypass" the exchange rate buffer. 
- **The Dilemma:** Even with a floating rate, an EME central bank may lose its independence because its domestic financial conditions are dominated by the **Global Financial Cycle** (driven by the Fed).
- **New Objective:** **Financial Stability**. 

## Practical Implications for EMEs

Perry argues that to solve the Financial Trilemma, central banks must move beyond the "Triangle" and implement a **Policy Mix**:
- **FX Intervention:** To manage extreme volatility that exchange rate flexibility cannot handle.
- **Capital Flow Management (CFM):** To break the direct link between global leverage and domestic credit growth.
- **Macroprudential Policy:** To protect the domestic banking system from the procyclicality of global risk premia.
