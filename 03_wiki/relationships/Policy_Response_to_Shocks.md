## Thesis
Effective policy response to macroeconomic shocks requires a diagnostic framework that distinguishes between **transitory vs. permanent** shocks and **domestic vs. foreign** origins, utilizing the National Accounts identities to align fiscal and monetary tools.

### ① REGIME LOCK
The appropriate policy mix depends on the **Exchange Rate Regime**:
*   **Flexible Regime:** Acts as a primary "shock absorber" through self-correcting relative price adjustments.
*   **Fixed/Managed Regime:** Requires active policy intervention (fiscal/monetary) to simulate the adjustment that the market exchange rate would otherwise perform.

### ② CAUSAL CHAIN
[LENSES: Lipschitz & Schadler — Decision Tree Logic; Perry Warjiyo — Policy Mix Framework; Ulrich Bindseil — Liquidity/Interest Rate Control]

**Diagnostic Chain:**
Identify Shock $\rightarrow$ [Longevity Test] $\rightarrow$ [Sectoral Test] $\rightarrow$ Tool Selection.

1.  **Longevity Test (Transitory vs. Permanent):**
    *   **Transitory:** Finance the deficit (borrowing or using reserves). Avoid structural changes to avoid "whipsawing" the economy.
    *   **Permanent:** Adjust the economy (reduce absorption or reallocate resources). Financing a permanent shock leads to an eventual balance of payments crisis.

2.  **Sectoral Test (Absorption Origin):**
    *   **Fiscal Expansion Origin:** Response: **Fiscal Tightening**. Reversing government expenditure ($G \downarrow$) or increasing taxes ($T \uparrow$) to restore $S_g - I_g$ balance.
    *   **Private Sector Boom Origin:** Response: **Monetary Tightening**. Raising interest rates ($r \uparrow$) to increase the cost of borrowing, incentivizing private saving ($S_p \uparrow$) and reducing private investment/consumption ($I_p, C \downarrow$).

**DENSE — Absorption Approach Unpacked ($CAD = ABS - GNDI$):**
*   **INTENT:** To shift focus from trade flows to the underlying gap between national income and national spending.
*   **MECHANISM:** Policy must either raise $GNDI$ (supply-side, long-term) or lower $ABS$ (demand-side, short-term).
*   **INTERACTIONS:** Tightening monetary policy reduces $ABS$ via the wealth and credit channels; tightening fiscal policy reduces $ABS$ via the income channel.
*   **BOUNDARY:** Fails if the economy is in a liquidity trap (ZLB), where interest rate increases have diminishing returns on $ABS$.

### ③ FEEDBACK TOPOLOGY
*   **Pro-cyclical Trap:** Failing to tighten during a boom $\rightarrow$ widening CAD $\rightarrow$ loss of confidence $\rightarrow$ capital flight $\rightarrow$ forced tightening during a recession.
*   **Stabilization Loop:** Counter-cyclical policy $\rightarrow$ narrowed CAD $\rightarrow$ stable reserves $\rightarrow$ sustained creditworthiness $\rightarrow$ lower cost of capital.

### ④ CHANNEL INTERACTION
*   **The Policy Mix:** In developing contexts (Perry Warjiyo), monetary policy often handles external stability (exchange rate) while fiscal policy handles domestic absorption ($ABS$), though they must be coordinated to avoid conflicting signals.

## Boundary Conditions
*   **Fiscal Space:** If government debt is already high, fiscal tightening is a mandate, not an option, regardless of the shock's origin.
*   **Social Thresholds:** Drastic cuts in absorption (Austerity) can trigger social instability, breaking the political feasibility of the adjustment mechanism.
