## Thesis

Perry Warjiyo highlights that central bank policy is not just a technical exercise but is deeply embedded in **Political Economy**. The legitimacy of a central bank depends on its ability to bridge the gap between "Philosophical Conceptual Theories" and the "Messy Reality" of innovative policymaking during crises.

## The Feedback Loop

### 1. Academia to Central Bank
Academia contributes quantitative methods and empirical studies based on assumptions that simplify complex behaviors. These provide the initial "Reference" for policymakers to identify best practices and prevent known problems.

### 2. Central Bank to Academia
During crises (like the GFC or Taper Tantrum), the central bank often makes **Breakthroughs** in conceptual ideas—policy innovations that have no prior academic blueprint (e.g., the Policy Mix or NIRP). These innovations then "Encourage the academic world to conduct theoretical studies" to find the underlying evidence.

## The Challenges of the Bridge

- **Sorting Relevant Theories:** One of the largest challenges for practitioners is sorting through the "Panoply of practices" that do not yet appear in academic textbooks.
- **Democratic Demand:** As governments become more market-based and democratically elected, there is an increasing demand for **Accountability and Transparency**, forcing the central bank to communicate its philosophical rationale more clearly.
