# [[Monetary_Policy_Trilemma]]

## Thesis
[WIKI/LLM] The Trilemma is the "Iron Law" of international macroeconomics. While the traditional Mundell-Fleming model suggests floating rates provide autonomy, modern financialization has created a **Global Financial Cycle** that forces a collapse into a "Dilemma" for many Emerging Market Economies (EMEs). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=81]]

## The Three Pillars (The Trinity)
[RAW] An authority can achieve only two of the following goals at any given time:
1.  **Exchange Rate Stability:** Reducing volatility to support trade and prevent currency mismatches.
2.  **Free Capital Mobility:** Allowing non-resident investors to move funds freely (Financial Liberalization).
3.  **Monetary Policy Autonomy:** The ability to set domestic interest rates independent of external shocks. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=82]]

## Evolution: From Trilemma to "Dilemma"

### 1. The Global Financial Cycle (Helene Rey)
[WIKI] Modern empirical evidence (Rey, 2013) suggests that global capital flows and leverage are driven primarily by Fed policy and the **VIX**, making exchange rate regimes less effective as shock absorbers.
- **The Dilemma:** The choice effectively becomes: **Open Capital (No Independence) vs. Managed Capital (Independence)**, regardless of whether the FX rate floats.

### 2. The Lucas Paradox
[RAW] Capital does not always flow from rich to poor countries as predicted. It often flows "uphill" due to institutional quality gaps, complicating the trilemma for EMEs that need investment but fear the volatility of sudden reversals.

## Strategic Trade-offs (Traditional Models)

### A. Fixed-Rate / Open-Capital (Hong Kong Model)
- **Sacrifice:** Monetary Autonomy. Money supply fluctuates passively to maintain the peg.

### B. Open-Capital / Autonomous-Policy (US/Eurozone Model)
- **Sacrifice:** Exchange Rate Stability. Rates are set for domestic needs; FX floats freely.

### C. Autonomous-Policy / Fixed-Rate (Historical/Closed Models)
- **Sacrifice:** Free Capital Mobility. Uses **Capital Controls** to break the link between domestic and international rates. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=82]]

## EME Adaptation: The Policy Mix
[RAW] Perry Warjiyo argues that for EMEs, pure choices are often catastrophic. Instead, they adopt a **Flexible Regime with Intervention**. By using FX Intervention (FXI) and Capital Flow Management (CFM), they "blur" the corners of the triangle to maintain partial stability in all three pillars. This is the foundation of the [[Central_Bank_Policy_Mix_Paradigm.md]]. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=82]]
