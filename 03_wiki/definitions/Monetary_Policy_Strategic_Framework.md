## Thesis

A strategic framework is more than just a target; it is a commitment mechanism. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự lựa chọn: NHTW không thể làm hài lòng tất cả mọi người cùng một lúc. Khung chiến lược là cách NHTW trả lời các câu hỏi về sự ưu tiên: lạm phát hay tăng trưởng? Lãi suất hay cung tiền? Quy tắc cứng nhắc hay sự linh hoạt tùy nghi? ## 1. The Three Fundamental Dualities

Perry identifies three core questions that define every monetary regime:

| Duality | The Choice | Modern Consensus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Target Priority** | Output Stability vs. Price Stability | Long-term focus on Price Stability. |
| **Operational Target** | Quantity (M) vs. Price (i) | Shift toward Price (Interest Rates). |
| **Response Style** | Rules vs. Discretion | Rules-based with "constrained discretion." |

## 2. The Social Welfare Logic (Loss Function)

To evaluate if a policy is "optimal," central banks use a **Quadratic Loss Function** ($L$) to minimize economic pain:
$$L = \frac{1}{2} [\omega_p (\pi - \pi^*)^2 + \omega_y (y - y^*)^2]$$
- **$\omega_p, \omega_y$:** Weights reflecting the central bank's preference.
- **The Quadratic Nature:** Implies that any deviation (positive or negative) from the target is equally harmful, with larger deviations penalized exponentially.
- **Strict vs. Flexible:** If $\omega_y = 0$, the bank is an "Inflation Nutter" (Strict ITF). If $\omega_y > 0$, it is a Flexible ITF bank that considers the output gap. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=162]]

---

## 3. The Nominal Anchor: The Anti-Inflation Constraint

A **Nominal Anchor** is a variable (like the exchange rate, money supply, or inflation forecast) used to pin down the price level.
- **Function:** It limits "Discretion" and prevents **Inflation Bias**—the tendency of politicians to boost the economy in the short term at the cost of long-term hyperinflation.
- **Criteria for a Good Anchor:**
    1. **Optimization:** Best trade-off between P and Y.
    2. **Stability:** Proof against unforeseen shocks.
    3. **Simplicity:** Transparent and easy for the public to understand. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=167]]

---

## Strategic Implications: The Long-term Neutrality

Perry Warjiyo highlights that while there is a short-term trade-off (Phillips Curve), in the long term, money is neutral.
- **Feasible Goal:** Because monetary policy cannot permanently raise output growth, **Price Stability** is the only feasible long-term target.
- **Short-term Flexibility:** Nominal income targeting could be a compromise during supply-side shocks, but it is rarely practiced due to complexity. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=163]]
