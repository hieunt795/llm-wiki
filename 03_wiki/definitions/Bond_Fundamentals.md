## Summary

[RAW-BOOK:5] Bond = IOU promising periodic interest (coupons) and principal repayment (face value) at maturity. Coupon amount fixed at issuance; paid regularly (semi-annually typical). Bonds trade on price basis (% of 100 face value), not par. Price determination: if coupon < market returns (equivalent maturity/risk), bond discounted below 100; if coupon > market returns, bond trades at premium above 100. Investor earning capital gain if purchased at discount; capital loss if purchased at premium. Yield concept captures both coupon income + capital gain/loss, expressed as annualized % return.

## Key Concepts

[RAW-BOOK:5] **Coupon**: Fixed periodic interest payment, historical origin from physical certificates where investor detached coupon to claim interest. **Face/Par Value**: Amount repaid at maturity (typically 100 per 100 nominal). **Maturity**: Date when principal repaid. **Price vs. Par**: Bonds rarely trade at exactly par. Price driven by coupon attractiveness vs. current market rates and risk. **Capital Gain/Loss**: Difference between purchase price and par value (realized at maturity or sale). **Yield**: Market's measure combining coupon income + capital appreciation into single return metric (explored in Chapter 2).
