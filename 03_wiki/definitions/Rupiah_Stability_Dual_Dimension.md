## Thesis

Unlike the ECB (Strict Price Stability) or the Fed (Price + Employment), BI's mandate is explicitly **Currency-centric**. This reflects Indonesia's vulnerability to exchange rate shocks, which historically have been the primary drivers of domestic inflation.

## The Dual Dimensions

1. **Internal Stability (Price Stability):** Maintaining a low and stable inflation rate for goods and services, measured by the CPI.
2. **External Stability (Exchange Rate Stability):** Managing the Rupiah's value against other currencies (primarily the USD) to prevent systemic shocks to the balance of payments.

## Operational Strategy: Managed Floating
BI does not target a *specific level* for the Rupiah. Instead, it aims to:
- **Minimize Volatility:** Intervening when the rate moves too fast for the economy to adjust.
- **Align with Fundamentals:** Ensuring the rate reflects long-term economic health (REER).

## Boundaries / Conditions

### The Understanding Gap
Perry notes that the public often misinterprets "Stability" as "Fixity." This creates political pressure on BI to defend the Rupiah even when a depreciation is fundamentally necessary, potentially leading to a drain on reserves.
