## Thesis

Post-1997, Bank Indonesia (BI) initially operated under an IMF-mandated monetary targeting regime. However, the seismic structural changes in the economy made the **Monetary Multiplier** unpredictable. The transition to ITF was not just a change in targets, but a 5-year process of rebuilding institutional credibility and technical capacity.

## Key Phases of Transition

### 1. The Monetary Targeting Era (pre-2005)
- **Target:** Base Money (M0).
- **Tool:** SBI (Sertifikat Bank Indonesia) auctions.
- **Problem:** Frequent overshooting of targets due to seasonal demand (Eid, Christmas) and a fading correlation between money supply and inflation.

### 2. Strategic Housekeeping (2000-2004)
- **Modelling:** BI developed its first structural models (SSM, SOFIE) to replace simple money demand functions.
- **Legal Reform:** The 2004 amendment to the BI Act shifted the inflation target setting from BI to the **Government (in consultation with BI)** to ensure national "ownership" of the target.

### 3. Full ITF Launch (July 1, 2005)
- **Operational Target:** The **BI Rate** (1-month SBI rate) replaced Base Money.
- **Signalling:** BI began using the interest rate to anchor expectations directly, focusing on the 6-8 quarter inflation forecast.

## Research Insight: The IMF Constraint
Indonesia's inclusion in the IMF program initially restricted the move to ITF because the IMF framework was rigidly quantity-based. BI had to conduct parallel research to prove that an interest-rate-based framework would be more stable for the recovering economy.
