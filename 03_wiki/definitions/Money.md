## Thesis
Money decouples the act of trade from direct barter by providing a standardized abstract commodity that represents a social obligation.

## Definition
According to the functional definition, any asset that fulfills the following three properties is money:
1. **Means of Exchange:** Standardized medium to facilitate trade.
2. **Unit of Account:** Measure of value for goods and services.
3. **Store of Value:** Mechanism to transfer purchasing power into the future.

## Monetary Aggregates (M0 to M3)
Central banks classify money into "Aggregates" based on a spectrum of **liquidity** (how easily it can be used for transactions without losing value):

- **M0 (Base Money):** Physical currency in circulation + Commercial bank reserves held at the central bank. This is "Outside Money" created directly by the central bank.
- **M1 (Narrow Money):** Physical currency + Demand deposits (checking accounts). These are highly liquid assets that can be spent immediately.
- **M2:** M1 + Savings deposits + Small time deposits. Slightly less liquid but easily convertible to cash.
- **M3 (Broad Money):** M2 + Large time deposits + Institutional money market funds + Short-term repurchase agreements. This represents the total purchasing power in the broader financial system. Monetarist frameworks (like the ECB's First Pillar) often target M3 growth to forecast long-term inflation.
