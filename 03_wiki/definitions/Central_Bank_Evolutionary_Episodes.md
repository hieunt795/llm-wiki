## Thesis

The pendulum of central banking focus swings between the state, the market, and the price level. Perry Warjiyo (citing Goodhart, 2010) systematizes these swings into three distinct "Episodes." Understanding these episodes is vital to realize that modern frameworks like ITF are not the "end of history," but rather a response to the failures of previous eras. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=22]]

---

## 1. Episode 1: The Gold Standard & Real Bills (1840–1914)

- **Primary Goal:** Price and Exchange Rate stability via gold convertibility.
- **Operational Logic:** **The Real Bills Doctrine**. Central banks only purchased commercial papers linked to real production and trade.
- **The Synergy:** Monetary and financial stability were integrated; money supply was naturally constrained by real output.
- **The Failure:** World War I forced governments to abandon gold to finance war deficits, ending the era of automatic discipline. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=22-23]]

## 2. Episode 2: Government Control (1940–1979)

- **Primary Goal:** Economic growth, full employment, and post-war recovery.
- **Key Characteristic:** Central banks were subordinated to the Executive. They acted as "printing presses" to buy government debt.
- **Mechanism:** **Financial Repression**. Interest rate caps and credit rationing were used to manage the resulting money growth.
- **The Failure:** The "Great Inflation" of the 1970s and the collapse of the Bretton Woods system proved that political control over money leads to stagflation and loss of credibility. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=25]]

## 3. Episode 3: Independence & ITF (1980–2007)

- **Primary Goal:** Price Stability (Inflation Targeting).
- **Key Characteristic:** Central bank independence was codified. Policy focused on short-term interest rates and market mechanisms.
- **The Era:** Known as the **Great Moderation**—low inflation and stable growth.
- **The Failure:** The GFC (2008) revealed a fatal flaw: "Inflation success" encouraged excessive risk-taking and asset price bubbles (Finance Bills), proving that price stability is insufficient for macro-stability. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=27-28]]

---

## 4. Post-GFC Era: The "New Original" Mandate (2008–Present)

Perry Warjiyo argues that we are now in a "hybrid" era:
- **The Return:** Central banks have returned to their original role as the active guardians of financial stability.
- **The Toolset:** Monetary policy must now coordinate with **Macroprudential** policy to "lean against the wind" of credit cycles.
- **The Shift:** LoLR function expanded to include non-bank financial institutions (QE, TAF). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=28-29]]
