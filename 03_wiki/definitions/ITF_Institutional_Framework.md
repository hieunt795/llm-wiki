## Thesis

Legislation is the "Shield" of the ITF. Without a strong legal mandate that proscribes the central bank from financing fiscal deficits and grants it absolute control over its instruments, an inflation target is merely a "forecast" rather than a commitment.

## Core Components

### 1. Central Banking Laws
- **Mandate:** Explicitly prioritizing price/currency stability.
- **Instrument Independence:** Freedom to set rates/OMOs without political veto.
- **Fiscal Proscription:** Banning the purchase of government debt on the primary market to prevent hyperinflationary "printing."

### 2. Target Formulation Parameters
- **Price Index:** Most EMEs prefer **CPI (Headline)** for its public clarity and credibility, despite its inclusion of non-monetary factors like energy prices.
- **Size:** A **Target Point** (e.g., UK 2%) vs. a **Target Range** (e.g., Indonesia 3.5% ± 1%). A range allows for more flexibility during supply shocks.
- **Horizon:** Typically **12-24 months** to match the transmission lag of monetary policy.

### 3. Escape Clauses
Specific requirements that state under which conditions the inflation target may be missed (e.g., natural disasters, massive oil shocks).
- **Function:** Protects the central bank's credibility during "Acts of God."
- **Condition:** The bank must publicly explain the deviation and provide a timeline for returning to the target.
