## Thesis

The exchange rate is the single most important relative price in an open economy. Perry Warjiyo systematizes the evolution of FX theory into three distinct paradigms. Understanding which paradigm is dominant in a given period is essential for central banks to manage the trade-offs between external competitiveness and domestic price stability. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=82]]

---

## 1. The Trade-Based Paradigm: PPP (Purchasing Power Parity)

Based on the **Law of One Price**, PPP view exchange rates as being determined by the flow of goods and services in the trade balance.

- **Absolute PPP:** $P = P^* + S$ (Domestic price = Foreign price adjusted by exchange rate).
- **Relative PPP:** $\Delta S = \pi - \pi^*$ (Changes in FX reflect the inflation differential).
- **Metric - REER (Real Effective Exchange Rate):** $\text{REER} = \sum \omega_j \pi^*_j / \pi$. Used as a measure of **Currency Misalignment** and competitiveness. A rising REER indicates a loss of export competitiveness. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=83-84]]

## 2. The Capital-Based Paradigm: IRP (Interest Rate Parity)

As global financial markets developed, focus shifted to the **Capital Account**. FX is seen as the price that equilibrates cross-border investment returns.

- **UIRP (Uncovered):** $r = r^* + [E(S) - S]/S$. Domestic rates equal foreign rates plus expected depreciation.
- **CIRP (Covered):** $r = r^* + (F - S)/S$. Includes a forward contract $(F)$ to hedge currency risk, ensuring no-arbitrage between currencies. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=84]]

## 3. The Modern Asset-Market Approach

Modern theory treats the exchange rate as an **Asset Price** on international financial markets, emphasizing three specialized models:

| Model | Focus | Key Characteristic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Monetary Model** | Money Supply | Assumes money is only used domestically. $S$ is determined by relative money demand/supply. |
| **Money Substitution** | Currency Holdings | Acknowledges the "Dollarization" effect where residents hold foreign assets. |
| **Portfolio Balance (PBA)** | Diversification | Assumes domestic and foreign bonds are *not* perfect substitutes. Risk premia drive FX moves. |

[[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=82-85]]

---

## Strategic Implications: The Real Effect

Perry Warjiyo highlights that while long-term FX moves follow PPP, short-term volatility is driven by the **Asset Approach** and market microstructure. This creates the necessity for central banks to intervene in FX markets when "Asset-based" volatility threatens the "Trade-based" fundamentals of the real economy. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=83]]
