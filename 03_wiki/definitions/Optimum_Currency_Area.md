## Thesis
An Optimum Currency Area (OCA) is a geographical region where it is economically beneficial to share a single currency or a common peg, based on the degree of economic integration and shock symmetry. [extracted]

## Definition
The OCA theory, pioneered by Mundell (1961) and McKinnon (1963), identifies criteria for when the benefits of joining a currency union (lower transaction costs, price stability) outweigh the costs (loss of independent monetary policy and exchange rate adjustment). [extracted]

### Criteria for an OCA
1. **Labor and Capital Mobility:** High mobility allows for adjustment to asymmetric shocks without exchange rate changes.
2. **Price and Wage Flexibility:** Ensures internal adjustment to shocks.
3. **Economic Integration:** High levels of trade between member states.
4. **Shock Symmetry:** Member countries face similar and closely correlated economic shocks.
5. **Fiscal Transfer System:** A mechanism to redistribute funds to regions hit by shocks. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- If criteria are not met, the nominal exchange rate remains a vital adjustment instrument, and unification may lead to output fluctuations and unemployment. [extracted]
