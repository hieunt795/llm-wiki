## Thesis

Stone and Bhundia (2004) refined Mishkin's classification by adding the dimension of **Clarity**. They argue that modern regimes are not static; there is a "Gradation" where countries shift between regimes to find the right balance between achieving inflation targets and supporting financial stability.

## The 7-Level Taxonomy

1. **Monetary Non-autonomy:** No independent currency (e.g., Currency Board). Highest commitment, zero prudence.
2. **Exchange Rate Peg:** Fixed peg to an anchor currency. High commitment.
3. **Full-fledged Inflation Targeting:** Clear, formal, and transparent commitment to an inflation target.
4. **Implicit Price Stability Anchor:** An eclectic regime (e.g., US Fed) that achieves stability without formal rules.
5. **Inflation Targeting Lite:** Commitment is shared between inflation and other variables (Exchange rate/Money).
6. **Weak Anchor:** Regimes with persistent high inflation (>40%) and no credible yardstick.
7. **Money Anchor:** Traditional monetary targeting based on aggregates.

## Modern Trend: Converging in the Middle
Warjiyo notes a significant trend:
- "Full-fledged" targeters are increasing their **Prudence** to manage financial stability (FIT).
- "Implicit" targeters are increasing their **Commitment** (more transparency) to appear like ITF.
**Result:** Most credible central banks are converging toward a hybrid regime that balances formal targets with strategic flexibility.
