## Thesis
Macroprudential policy contains prudential regulatory instruments targeting financial system stability (FSS) as a whole, not individual institution soundness; it operates across two dimensions: time-series (dampening procyclicality) and cross-section (systemic interconnectedness).

## 1. Core Definition

[RAW] Per Perry Warjiyo: "Macroprudential policy contains prudential regulatory instruments that aim to maintain FSS as a whole, not the soundness of individual financial institutions. Analogously, microprudential policy consists of prudential regulatory instruments that aim to maintain the soundness of individual financial institutions."

The G-30 definition emphasizes: "Macroprudential policy seeks to develop, oversee and deliver appropriate policy responses to the financial system as a whole. It aims to enhance financial system resilience and dampen systemic risks that spread through the financial system."

## 2. Distinction from Microprudential Policy

[RAW] Microprudential supervision focuses on:
- Individual bank capital adequacy
- Loan loss reserves and credit risk
- Deposit insurance protection
- Single-institution insolvency prevention

[RAW] Macroprudential regulation focuses on:
- System-wide credit and liquidity cycles
- Procyclicality dampening
- Interconnectedness and network contagion
- Macrofinancial linkages between financial system and real economy

## 3. Two Operative Dimensions

[RAW] Per Perry Warjiyo (p.432-433):

**Time-Series Dimension:** Macroprudential policy dampens excessive procyclicality in the financial system by requiring financial institutions to build capital buffers during expansions and permit buffer release during contractions.

**Cross-Section Dimension:** Policy shifts focus from regulating individual institutions to regulating the financial system as a whole, recognizing that "large crises occur because of simultaneous exposure to macrofinancial imbalances by most of the financial system."

## 4. Key Policy Targets

[RAW] Per Table 14.1 (Perry), macroprudential policy addresses:
- Leverage control through risk-weighted capital requirements
- Credit boom dampening via countercyclical provisioning and loan-to-value limits
- Sector-specific risks through sectoral lending restrictions
- Liquidity adequacy through loan-to-deposit ratios and funding buffers

## 5. Central Bank Implementation

[RAW] The central bank is the most appropriate institution because:
1. Access to macroeconomic and macrofinancial surveillance capacity
2. Integration with monetary policy framework (shared models, projections)
3. Authority over payment system and interbank markets
4. Lender-of-last-resort (LOLR) function enabling liquidity provision

## 6. Post-GFC Institutional Evolution

[RAW] Before 2008/2009, policy coordination separated: Ministry of Finance (fiscal), Central Bank (monetary + exchange rate), Supervisory Agency (microprudential). The GFC exposed this fragmentation and led to integration of:
- Macroprudential policy with monetary policy
- Monetary policy with financial stability objectives
- Central bank coordination with supervisory and deposit insurance authorities
