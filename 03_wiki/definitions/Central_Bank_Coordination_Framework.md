## Thesis
The Central Bank Coordination Framework in Indonesia utilizes specialized task forces (TPI and TPID) to synchronize monetary and fiscal actions, specifically addressing supply-side and regional sources of inflation. [extracted]

## Definition
### 1. TPI (National Inflation Task Force)
National-level coordination between BI and various government ministries to manage administered prices and national supply chains. [extracted]

### 2. TPID (Regional Inflation Task Force)
Local-level coordination focused on food security, price stabilization of strategic commodities, and removing local infrastructure bottlenecks. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- **Supply-Side Focus:** Essential because BI instruments (interest rates) cannot fix crop failures or broken distribution roads. [extracted]
- **Integrated Strategy:** Necessary to manage the impact of large government projects and capital flow management. [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **Flexible ITF:** This coordination is a core pillar of the "Flexible ITF" strategy, acknowledging that the central bank cannot achieve price stability in isolation. [extracted]
