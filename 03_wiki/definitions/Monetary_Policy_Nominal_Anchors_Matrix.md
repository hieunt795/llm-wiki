## Thesis

The choice of an anchor determines the central bank's level of **Discretion** versus **Commitment**. A clear anchor reduces "Inflation Bias" caused by time inconsistency but may limit the bank's ability to respond to non-monetary shocks.

## The Regime Matrix

| Regime | Nominal Anchor | Primary Advantage | Major Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exchange Rate Targeting** | Foreign Currency (USD/EUR) | Simple, anchors import prices, rules-based. | Loss of independence (Trilemma), vulnerable to speculative attacks. |
| **Monetary Targeting** | Money Supply (M1/M2) | Independent policy, transparent signals. | Unstable velocity; relationship between money and output fades. |
| **Inflation Targeting (ITF)** | Numerical Inflation Target | Credible, focuses on domestic stability, transparent. | Inflation is hard to control directly; transmission lags. |
| **No Explicit Anchor** | Flexible/Forward-looking | Maximum flexibility to respond to various shocks. | Low transparency; public makes assumptions; low accountability. |
| **Nominal Income Targeting** | Growth + Inflation (PY) | Optimal balance during supply shocks. | Theoretically sound but never successfully practiced in reality. |

## Boundaries / Conditions

### The Hollowing of the Middle
Warjiyo notes a trend of "Bipolarization": countries are moving away from intermediate regimes (soft pegs/broad money) toward either "Hard Pegs" or "Full ITF with Floating Rates."
