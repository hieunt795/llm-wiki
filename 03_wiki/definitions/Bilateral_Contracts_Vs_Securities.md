## Thesis

Fixed income instruments are structurally bifurcated into **Bilateral Contracts** (private, non-transferable without consent) and **Securities** (liquid, freely transferable). This distinction determines the liquidity profile, legal framework, and trading venue for the instrument.

## Taxonomy (Table 1.1)

| Bilateral Contracts | Securities |
|---|---|
| **Deposits** | **Bonds** |
| **Loans** | **Commercial Paper (CP)** |
| **Swaps** | **Certificates of Deposit (CD)** |
| **Futures** | **Asset-Backed Securities (ABS)** |

*Note: In modern markets, the lines blur. For instance, syndicated loans have mechanisms for transfer, and central clearing (CCP) turns bilateral swaps into a security-like cleared structure.*

## Mechanical Drivers

### 1. Transferability
- **Bilateral:** Obligations are specific to the parties. Transferring the rights/obligations usually requires explicit consent from both the borrower and the lender.
- **Securities:** Designed for mass distribution. They are negotiable instruments that can be sold on secondary markets without the issuer's permission.

### 2. Market Venue
- **Bilateral:** Typically Over-the-Counter (OTC).
- **Securities:** Often Exchange-traded (Futures) or traded on organized interdealer platforms (Bonds).
