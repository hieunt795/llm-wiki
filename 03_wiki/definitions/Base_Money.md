# [[Base_Money]]

## Thesis
[WIKI/LLM] At the core of every modern monetary system lies **Base Money**, also known as high-powered money. It is the "Outside Money" that serves as the anchor for the vast volume of "Inside Money" created by the private banking sector. While central banks have significant control over the total volume of these liabilities, their power is subject to domestic demand fluctuations and international capital flows. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=50]]

## Definition
[RAW] Base money is defined purely from the perspective of the central bank's liability side. It consists of two primary components:
1. **Physical Banknotes:** The currency issued by the central bank for use by the general public.
2. **Current Account Holdings (Reserves):** The electronic balances held by commercial banks at the central bank for interbank settlement and reserve requirements. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch08.md#page=50]]

## Mechanism: Central Bank Operations
[RAW] A central bank manipulates its total short-term liabilities (Base Money) through three categories of actions:

### 1. Neutral Actions (Compositional Change)
[RAW] Actions that have no effect on the total size of liabilities, merely exchanging one form for another.
- *Example:* A commercial bank withdrawing physical banknotes in exchange for its electronic balance. Reserves decrease, physical currency increases, but the **Total Base Money** remains unchanged. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=50]]

### 2. Liquidity Providing Operations (Expansion)
[RAW] Actions that increase the total volume of base money.
- *Example:* The central bank purchasing an asset (bond or foreign currency) from the private sector. The central bank credits the selling bank's account with new reserves, expanding the balance sheet. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=50]]

### 3. Liquidity Absorbing Operations (Contraction)
[RAW] Actions that reduce the total volume of base money.
- *Example:* The central bank selling an asset or issuing its own financing bills. Payment is deducted from commercial bank reserves, shrinking the monetary base. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch08.md#page=50]]

## Boundaries / Conditions

### The Hierarchy of Quality
[WIKI] What makes base money unique is its position at the apex of the liquidity hierarchy. While commercial bank deposits (Inside Money) are promises to pay central bank money, base money *is* the final payment.
- **Legal Tender:** Base money typically enjoys legislative support as the ultimate settlement medium.
- **Credit Quality:** As a liability of the sovereign central bank, it carries the highest possible credit quality in domestic currency ("money of ultimate quality"). [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=37]]

### Control Limits
[RAW] The central bank's control over base money is limited by **Endogenous Demand**. In a crisis, the central bank may provide massive liquidity, but if the private sector hoards this base money (liquidity trap) rather than using it for credit creation, the central bank's influence on broader money supply (M3) and the price level breaks down. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=45]]
