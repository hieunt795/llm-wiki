## Thesis

The choice of a monetary regime is a choice of institutional discipline. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự tiến hóa: không có một "chiếc áo" chung cho mọi nền kinh tế. Sự thay đổi từ mỏ neo bên ngoài (Tỷ giá) sang mỏ neo bên trong (Cung tiền) và cuối cùng là mỏ neo dự báo (ITF) là hệ quả của việc các mối quan hệ kinh tế vĩ mô ngày càng trở nên phức tạp và khó dự đoán hơn. ## 1. Evolution of Nominal Anchors

Central banks have experimented with five major regimes to achieve price stability:

### A. Exchange Rate Targeting (The External Anchor)
- **Mechanism:** Pegging the domestic currency to a "hard" currency (USD, Gold) or a basket.
- **Pros:** Importer of credibility; anchors inflation expectations instantly; simple to understand.
- **Cons:** Loss of monetary independence (The [[Monetary_Policy_Trilemma|Trilemma]]); vulnerable to speculative attacks.
- **Best for:** Small, very open economies (Hong Kong, Singapore). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=168]]

### B. Monetary Targeting (The Intermediate Anchor)
- **Mechanism:** Controlling the growth of monetary aggregates (M1, M2).
- **Pros:** Allows independent monetary policy; completely transparent.
- **Cons:** Depends on a stable relationship between M and P. **Velocity Instability** due to financial innovation makes this regime unreliable in modern economies. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=168]]

### C. Inflation Targeting (The Direct Anchor)
- **Mechanism:** Committing to a specific medium-term inflation target.
- **Pros:** Focuses on domestic stability; transparent and accountable; allows flexible response to output shocks.
- **Cons:** Inflation is hard to control directly; transmission lags mean results are only seen in the long term. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=169]]

### D. No Explicit Anchor (The "Wait and See" Regime)
- **Mechanism:** Forward-looking monitoring without a pre-announced target.
- **Example:** US Fed during the 1990s.
- **Cons:** Lack of transparency can trigger market uncertainty and undermine accountability. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=169]]

### E. Nominal Income Targeting (The Theoretical Alternative)
- **Mechanism:** Targeting the sum of real GDP growth and inflation.
- **Pros:** Copes better with supply-side shocks; stabilizes both P and Y in tandem.
- **Status:** Historically hypothetical, but gaining academic traction. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=170]]

## Strategic Implications: The Pendulum Swing

Perry Warjiyo highlights that regimes change when their "Anchor" fails.
- **The Failure of M2:** When financial innovation blurred the definition of money, the link between money supply and inflation broke, forcing central banks toward **ITF**.
- **The Choice:** Today, the consensus leans toward **Flexible Inflation Targeting**, which combines the discipline of an inflation anchor with the flexibility to mitigate short-term output shocks. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=171]]
