## Thesis
Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) states that the exchange rate between two currencies should equal the ratio of the two countries' price levels, reflecting the law of one price for internationally traded goods. [extracted]

## Definition
PPP is based on the concept of flows where the demand for foreign exchange for imports equals the supply from exports. [extracted]

### Variants
- **Absolute PPP:** The prices of homogenous products in different countries are the same when calculated in a single currency:
  $$P = P^* + S$$
  (where $S$ is the log nominal exchange rate, $P$ and $P^*$ are log domestic and international prices).
- **Relative PPP:** Changes in exchange rates mirror differences in inflation between countries:
  $$\Delta S = \pi - \pi^*$$
- **Real Effective Exchange Rate (REER):** A weighted average of PPP across multiple trading partners:
  $$REER = \sum \omega_j \frac{\pi^*_j}{\pi}$$ [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- Law of One Price (LOOP) assumes perfect market mechanisms.
- No transaction costs, taxes, or trade barriers.
- Homogenous products.
- No uncertainty. [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **Cassel (1918):** Formulated the relative PPP theory. [extracted]
- **REER Index:** Used to measure currency misalignment from fundamental conditions and real exchange rate competitiveness. [extracted]
