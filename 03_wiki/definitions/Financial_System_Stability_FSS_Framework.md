## Thesis

The Global Financial Crisis (GFC) proved that the "Soundness of individual banks" (Microprudential focus) does not guarantee the "Soundness of the system." FSS shifts the focus from individual components to the **Macrofinancial Linkages** that can trigger a domino effect.

## Five Pillars of FSS Analysis

As identified by Warjiyo & Juhro (2019):
1. **Beyond Individual Soundness:** FSS is about the collective resilience of the system, not just one "Lehman Brothers."
2. **Management of Procyclicality:** Identifying and dampening the boom-bust cycles of credit and asset prices.
3. **Resilience to External Shocks:** Buffer mechanisms against "Sudden-stops" and capital reversals.
4. **Interconnectedness Management:** Monitoring the "Financial Network" (Interbank markets, Payment systems) to prevent rapid contagion.
5. **Behavioral Oversight:** Mitigating "Herding Behavior" and information contagion (Panic) among depositors and investors.

## Institutional Coordination
No single institution can manage FSS alone. It requires:
- **Central Bank:** Macroprudential policy and LoLR.
- **Supervisory Authority (OJK/SEC):** Microprudential supervision.
- **Deposit Insurance (LPS/FDIC):** Mitigating bank runs.
- **Treasury (Ministry of Finance):** Fiscal support for resolution.
