## Thesis

Perry Warjiyo divides Indonesia's pre-ITF history into three distinct eras, each teaching a lesson about the coordination between fiscal and monetary policy.

## The Three Eras

### 1. Stabilization and Rehabilitation (1968–1972)
- **Context:** Post-independence recovery.
- **Regime:** Rigid fiscal-monetary coordination via the Monetary Board.
- **Lessons:** Monetary policy must not finance the budget deficit. Discipline led to single-digit inflation and restored confidence.

### 2. Oil-based Growth and Repression (1973–1982)
- **Context:** Oil windfall.
- **Regime:** **Financial Repression**. Selective credit policies and "Net Asset Expansion Ceilings" to absorb excess liquidity from oil proceeds.
- **Constraint:** Interest rate caps and credit rationing discouraged private saving.

### 3. Deregulation and Liberalization (1983–1997)
- **Context:** Oil price slump forcing private-sector reliance.
- **Regime:** Shift to **Indirect Instruments**. OMOs (SBI auctions) became the primary tool.
- **Innovation:** The "Pakto 88" package reduced reserve requirements from 15% to 2%, sparking a banking boom.
- **The Failure:** Liberalization led to a decoupling of the financial and real sectors, unhedged foreign debt, and ultimately the 1997 Asian Crisis.
