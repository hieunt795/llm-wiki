## Thesis

Maintaining FSS is not a one-time act but a permanent, multi-layered defense. Perry Warjiyo argues that **Structural Policy** reduces the "Heat" and frequency of required **Cyclical** interventions, making the overall policy mix more efficient.

## The Two Dimensions

### 1. The Cyclical Dimension (The "Thermostat")
- **Focus:** Short-term fluctuations in credit and asset prices.
- **Tools:** Monetary policy, Capital Flow Management (CFM), and time-series macroprudential tools (e.g., CCyB).
- **Mechanism:** Triggered when FSS risk is deemed "excessive" (Figure 15.2).

### 2. The Structural Dimension (The "Foundation")
- **Focus:** Long-term resilience of the financial system architecture.
- **Tools:** Microprudential regulations, Basel III capital requirements (LCR, NSFR), and cross-section macroprudential tools (e.g., SIFI surcharges).
- **Mechanism:** Strengthening the "Plumbing" (Payment systems) and ensuring banks have permanent buffers to survive unanticipated events.

## Hierarchy of Response (Figure 15.2)
1. **First Option:** Targeted macroprudential tools (LTV, DTI) to address specific sector bubbles.
2. **Second Option:** Fiscal tightening to reduce aggregate demand.
3. **Third Option:** Monetary tightening ("Leaning against the wind") if inflation is also rising or if bubbles are systemic.
