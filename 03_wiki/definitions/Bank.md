## Thesis

The defining characteristic of a bank is the **unconditional promise of liquidity**. While banks perform many functions (custody, dealing, investment), their core institutional identity is built on the public's right to reclaim deposits in cash (Outside Money) at any time, regardless of whether that cash was originally placed there or created through credit.

## Functional Evolution

### 1. Pre-Institutional Banking
Banking functions existed before banks as we know them:
- **Knights Templar:** Operated an early traveler's cheque network.
- **Merchant Banking:** Emerged as traders chose to lend capital to others' ventures (earning interest) rather than risking it all in their own shipping ventures (earning equity profit).

### 2. Modern Core Definition
A bank is a depositary institution that:
1. **Accepts Deposits:** Differentiates between **Demand/Sight Deposits** (reclaimed at any time) and **Time Deposits** (fixed period).
2. **Creates Money:** Issues banknotes or digital deposits through the provision of credit.
3. **Infrastructure Provider:** Facilitates clearing and settlement for other financial institutions (e.g., CLS Bank, Custodians).
