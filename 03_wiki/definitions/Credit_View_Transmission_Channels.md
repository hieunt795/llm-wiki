## Thesis
The credit view of MPTM posits that financial frictions and asymmetric information create specific transmission channels (bank lending, balance sheet, risk-taking) that amplify or distort the impact of interest rate policy. [extracted]

## Definition
Unlike the "money view" (neoclassical), which assumes efficient markets, the credit view identifies three anomalies in transmission:
1. **Composition:** Policy affects variables (like housing) that should be insensitive to short-term rates.
2. **Propagation:** Impact persists long after a policy change.
3. **Amplification:** Small interest rate changes trigger disproportionately large output responses. [extracted]

### The Core Channels
- **Bank Lending Channel:** Focuses on the supply of credit from banks.
- **Balance Sheet Channel:** Focuses on the net worth and collateral of borrowers.
- **Risk-Taking Channel:** Focuses on how policy shifts risk tolerance. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- Most relevant in bank-dominated economies (e.g., EMEs).
- Asymmetric information (ex-ante adverse selection and ex-post moral hazard) is the foundational friction. [extracted]
