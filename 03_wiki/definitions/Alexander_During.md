## Thesis

Alexander Düring is a prominent financial analyst and author known for his comprehensive work on fixed income markets. His approach is characterized by a blend of technical mathematical rigor and a deep consideration of the historical, etymological, and moral contexts of financial instruments.

## Definition

In the context of this wiki, **Alexander Düring** is the primary source of truth and the author of the foundational text from which these nodes are extracted.

### Intellectual Stance
Düring explicitly states that his approach to fixed income is not neutral. He views the financial industry as a service sector that must fulfill a social role. This perspective influences his analysis of:
- **Ethics:** Probing the moral implications of debt and usury.
- **Redistribution:** Valuing social claims (like the NHS) as fixed income assets.
- **Critical Discussion:** Not just explaining tools, but highlighting their shortcomings and when not to trust them. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch01.md#page=7-8]]
