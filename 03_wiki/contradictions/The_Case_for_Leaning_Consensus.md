## Thesis

The "Clean" approach (Greenspan, 2002) was based on the belief that bubbles cannot be identified ex-ante and that raising rates might inadvertently trigger a crisis. The GFC proved that the cost of "Cleaning" (protracted depression, massive fiscal bailouts) is infinitely higher than the cost of "Leaning."

## Why the Paradigm Shifted

1. **Destructive Magnitude:** Systemic crises (like GFC) are not "mean-reverting." They cause permanent scarring to output and social welfare.
2. **Success of Leaning:** Evidence from Australia (2002-2003) showed that a central bank can successfully raise rates to dampen property bubbles even when inflation is on target.
3. **Availability of Surgical Tools:** The development of **Macroprudential** policy (LTV, CCyB) provided the central bank with "Scalpels" to lean against specific sectors without using the "Hammer" of interest rates.

## The New Global Consensus
Central banks now agree that:
- Price stability is not a guarantee of financial stability.
- "Stability is destabilizing" (Minsky hypothesis).
- Pre-emptive action is required to prevent **Macrofinancial Imbalances** from reaching a terminal point.
