## Thesis

The balance sheet channel focuses on the **Borrower Side** of credit. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự lan tỏa: chính sách tiền tệ không chỉ tác động đến chi phí vay vốn mà còn làm thay đổi trực tiếp **Năng lực tài chính** của người đi vay. Khi lãi suất tăng, giá trị tài sản đảm bảo giảm và dòng tiền bị bào mòn, khiến các doanh nghiệp—ngay cả những bên có dự án tốt—cũng bị ngân hàng từ chối cho vay do rủi ro đạo đức (moral hazard) tăng cao. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=132]]

## 1. The External Finance Premium (EFP)

Because of asymmetric information, lenders impose an EFP—a cost markup—to compensate for the risk of default and the cost of verifying the borrower's business feasibility.
- **The Logic:** As a borrower's **Net Worth** falls (due to higher interest expenses or lower asset prices), the EFP rises.
- **The Result:** The cost of external capital becomes significantly higher than internal funds, leading to a sharp drop in investment even for profitable firms. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=133]]

## 2. The Financial Accelerator (Figure 5.3)

This mechanism explains why small monetary shocks create large output fluctuations through a self-reinforcing loop.

### A. The Collateral Constraint (Kiyotaki-Moore)
The maximum loan ($L_1$) a firm can receive is bound by the value of its collateral ($W$):
$$L_1 \le \frac{q_1 W}{\alpha(1+r)}$$
- **$q_1$:** Market price of collateral assets.
- **$\alpha$:** Inverse of the Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio.

### B. The Propagation Loop
1.  **Monetary Easing:** $i \downarrow \to q_1 \uparrow$ (Asset prices rise).
2.  **Increased Capacity:** The collateral constraint loosens $\to L_1 \uparrow$ (Firm can borrow more).
3.  **Amplified Growth:** Higher investment $\to$ Higher output $\to$ Higher demand for assets $\to q_1$ rises further.
4.  **The Result:** Production ($y$) reaches its optimal level ($y^*$) faster than through the interest rate channel alone. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=134-135]]

---

## Strategic Implications: The Procyclical Trap

Perry Warjiyo highlights that this channel is highly **Procyclical**:
- **Boom Phase:** Rising asset prices hide underlying risks, as the collateral "veil" makes credit seem cheap and abundant.
- **Bust Phase:** Falling asset prices trigger a "Margin Call" on the entire economy. As collateral values collapse, credit is pulled back, forcing fire sales and deep recessions.
- **Inference:** Understanding this channel is vital for the **Macroprudential** role of central banks in "leaning against the wind" during asset bubbles. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=136-137]]
