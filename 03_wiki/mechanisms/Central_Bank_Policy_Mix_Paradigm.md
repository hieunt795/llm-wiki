# [[Central_Bank_Policy_Mix_Paradigm]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] In the post-GFC era, price stability is recognized as a necessary but insufficient condition for macroeconomic stability, requiring central banks to adopt a dual mandate of monetary and financial system stability.

## Mechanism
[RAW] Changing behavior in the financial system, which was linked to the growing role of risk perception and procyclicality, also exacerbated the complexity of monetary control and complicated decisionmaking. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=281]]

[RAW] The successes of ITF implementation must be supported by a macroprudential policy regulatory framework. This would simultaneously overcome one of the prerequisites to successful ITF implementation, namely a sound and efficient financial system. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=282]]

[LLM] This paradigm shift leads to a "Policy Mix" where the interest rate remains the primary tool for inflation, but is augmented by macroprudential tools (like reserve requirements and LTV ratios) to address financial imbalances and capital flow volatility.
