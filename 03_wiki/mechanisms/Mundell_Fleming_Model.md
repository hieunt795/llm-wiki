## Thesis
The Mundell-Fleming model extends the IS-LM framework to an open economy, analyzing the effectiveness of monetary and fiscal policy under different exchange rate regimes and capital mobility conditions. [extracted]

## Mechanism
The model consists of three curves:
1. **IS Curve (Goods Market):** $Y = C + I + G + NX$. Net exports ($NX$) are influenced by the real exchange rate $Q$.
2. **LM Curve (Money Market):** $M/P = L(Y, r)$.
3. **FE Curve (Foreign Exchange/BoP):** $BoP = CA + KA$. Capital account ($KA$) depends on the interest rate differential $r - r^* - E(\Delta S)$. [extracted]

### Policy Effectiveness
- **Flexible Exchange Rate + Perfect Capital Mobility:** Monetary policy is effective (affects output via exchange rate depreciation/exports). Fiscal policy is ineffective (crowded out by exchange rate appreciation).
- **Fixed Exchange Rate + Perfect Capital Control:** Fiscal policy is effective. Monetary policy is ineffective (central bank must intervene to maintain the peg, neutralizing money supply changes). [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- Underemployment (fixed prices).
- Low transportation costs (satisfies PPP).
- UIRP holds.
- Perfect or imperfect capital mobility (determines FE curve slope). [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **Mundell (1963) & Fleming (1962):** Original developers.
- **Policy Trilemma:** The model establishes the "Impossible Trinity." [extracted]
