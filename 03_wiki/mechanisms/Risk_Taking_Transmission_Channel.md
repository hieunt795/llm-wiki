## Thesis

The risk-taking channel explains how prolonged low interest rates breed financial instability. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự lan tỏa: khi lãi suất chính sách thấp hơn mức lợi nhuận mục tiêu của các định chế tài chính (như quỹ hưu trí, bảo hiểm), nó kích hoạt một đợt **Tìm kiếm lợi nhuận (Search for Yield)** mãnh liệt. Trong đợt sóng này, các chuẩn mực rủi ro bị nới lỏng, các sản phẩm phái sinh phức tạp bùng nổ, và hệ thống chuyển từ vai trò "giảm xóc" (shock absorber) sang "khuếch đại shock" (shock amplifier). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=137]]

## 1. The Search for Yield (Borio & Zhu, 2008)

Perry highlights three specific paths through which monetary policy shifts risk behavior:

### A. The Valuation & Cash Flow Path
Lower rates ($i \downarrow$) boost asset prices and collateral values. This improves the **Risk Perception** of banks, leading them to underestimate the probability of default and lower the external finance premium.

### B. The Target Yield Path (Rajan, 2005)
Financial managers often have fixed nominal return targets (e.g., 8%).
- **The Gap:** When the policy rate drops to 1-2%, managers cannot meet their targets using safe bonds.
- **The Shift:** They are forced to shift into higher-risk assets (junk bonds, subprime mortgages) or leverage their portfolios to "manufacture" the required return.

### C. The Transparency & Communication Path
**Forward Guidance** and central bank transparency can inadvertently anchor expectations so firmly that economic agents become complacent. Prudent players may engage in higher-risk activities, assuming the central bank will always provide a "safety net." [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=138-139]]

## 2. The Role of Liquidity (Figure 5.1)

Risk-taking is fueled by the interaction between two types of liquidity:
- **Funding Liquidity:** The ability of an investor to obtain cash (often using assets as collateral).
- **Market Liquidity:** The ability to trade assets without moving the price significantly.

### The Feedback Loop
During a boom, high funding liquidity supports risk-taking, which increases market liquidity. 
- **The Crash:** When risk perceptions tighten, investors scramble for cash (**Cash is King**). 
- **The Fire Sale:** Attempting to sell illiquid assets into a falling market triggers a **Liquidity Multiplier**, where falling prices force further sales to cover margin calls, culminating in systemic contagion (e.g., GFC). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=139-140]]

## Strategic Implications: The Minsky Moment

Perry Warjiyo highlights that this channel is the heart of the **Boom-Bust Cycle**:
- **Originate to Distribute:** Banks stop monitoring loans (moral hazard) and instead securitize them to offload risk, creating a "Ponzi" dynamic where credit growth outstrips fundamentals.
- **Policy Response:** This channel justifies the integration of **Macroprudential** policy into the central bank's toolkit to prevent "irrational exuberance" before it reaches a Minsky moment. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=138]]
