## Thesis

MV=PY is the fundamental accounting identity of macroeconomics. While simple in its static form, its **total differential** reveals the three control variables available to a central bank and the deep ideological split between Keynesians and Monetarists regarding which variable drives the other.

## The Differential Mechanism

Taking the total differential of $P = (MV)/Y$ gives:
$$dP = \frac{M}{Y} dV + \frac{V}{Y} dM - \frac{MV}{Y^2} dY$$

### Ideological Interpretations of the Terms

1. **The Velocity Term ($dV$):** Economists generally agree this is uncontrollable. Velocity reacts to liquidity preferences and investment climate. If $dV$ perfectly offsets $dM$, monetary policy becomes impotent (Liquidity Trap).
2. **The Keynesian View ($dY$):** Focuses on the output gap. Monetary policy works by influencing $dY$ through interest rates. In this view, $dM$ reacts passively to changes in real economic activity.
3. **The Monetarist View ($dM$):** Argues $dY$ is independent of the central bank in the long run. Therefore, the CB should focus exclusively on controlling $dM$ to achieve a desired inflation level ($dP$), accounting for stable trends in $dV$ and $dY$.

## Boundaries / Limits

### The Breakdown of V
If faith in the domestic currency collapses ($V \to 0$), the relationship breaks down. Price levels become independent of $dM$ as the economy resorts to barter or dollarization.
