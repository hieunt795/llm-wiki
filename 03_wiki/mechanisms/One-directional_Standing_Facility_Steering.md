# [[One-directional_Standing_Facility-based_Monetary_Policy_Implementation]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] This mechanism represents the simplest form of interest rate steering, where the central bank ensures the banking system is systematically reliant on a specific standing facility, forcing the interbank rate to converge to that facility's rate via arbitrage.

## Mechanism

### Variant 1: Structural Liquidity Deficit (Borrowing Facility)
[RAW] Under this approach, the central bank ensures through low or zero liquidity provision via outright open market operations (or liquidity-absorbing operations) that the banking system has a systematic liquidity deficit vis-à-vis the central bank. [[Bindseil_Monetary_Policy_Operations.md#page=51]]

[LLM] By starving the market of reserves, the central bank forces banks to use the borrowing facility (e.g., discount window) to meet reserve requirements or working balances. 

[RAW] Overnight interest rates cannot exceed the borrowing facility rate because it would always be cheaper for a bank to go to the borrowing facility than to the interbank market. Also, overnight rates cannot fall below the borrowing facility rate since at the margin the banking system needs to borrow at the latter rate, and hence no bank should be willing to lend at a lower rate. [[Bindseil_Monetary_Policy_Operations.md#page=51]]

### Variant 2: Structural Liquidity Surplus (Deposit Facility/Floor System)
[RAW] The central bank ensures through sufficiently high liquidity-injecting outright monetary policy operations that the banking system has a systematic liquidity surplus. The rate of the deposit facility (or remuneration of excess reserves) is set to the level of the intended policy target rate. [[Bindseil_Monetary_Policy_Operations.md#page=52]]

[LLM] This "floor system" has been utilized by the Federal Reserve and the Bank of England post-2009. In this setup, the abundance of reserves means banks have no need to borrow, and the deposit rate acts as a hard floor that gimbals the market rate, as no bank would lend to another bank for less than it can earn risk-free at the central bank.

## Worked Example: German Reichsbank (1900)
[RAW] Monetary policy operations were dominated by the discounting of bills. Banks took recourse to the Lombard facility, priced 100 basis points above the discount facility. The choice was determined by the availability of discountable bills; the discount rate determined market rates. [[Bindseil_Monetary_Policy_Operations.md#page=52]]
