## Thesis
Flexible ITF is an evolved monetary policy strategy that integrates inflation targeting with macroprudential policy and capital flow management to ensure both price and financial system stability. [extracted]

## Mechanism
Following the GFC (2008), central banks recognized that low inflation was not sufficient for macroeconomic stability. [extracted]

### Core Elements
1. **Multiple Targets:** Balancing the inflation target with output stabilization and financial system stability. [extracted]
2. **Policy Mix:** Integrating interest rate policy with **macroprudential tools** (e.g., LTV, CCyB) and capital flow management.
3. **Exchange Rate Role:** Using the exchange rate flexibly as a shock absorber while intervening to prevent misalignment. [extracted]
4. **Coordination:** Strong institutional coordination between the central bank and the government (e.g., TPI/TPID in Indonesia). [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- **Tail Risks:** Requires monitoring extreme risks that traditional models might miss.
- **Independence:** Integration must be managed carefully to avoid undermining central bank credibility through goal-overload. [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **Bank Indonesia Strategy:** Post-2010 BI strategy incorporates these five elements to manage external spillovers and domestic supply shocks. [extracted]
- **Svensson (1999):** Popularized the contrast between "Strict" and "Flexible" ITF. [extracted]
