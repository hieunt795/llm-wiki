## Thesis

The GFC proved that price stability is a necessary but not sufficient condition for macro-stability. IIT expands the traditional **Flexible ITF** by acknowledging that the central bank must look beyond the 2-year inflation horizon to manage the longer-term **Financial Cycle**.

## Mechanism: The Expanded Loss Function

In the IIT paradigm, the social loss function $L$ is modified to include a third term:
$$L = \frac{1}{2} [(\pi_t - \pi^*)^2 + \lambda x_t^2 + \gamma (Gap\_Fin_t)^2]$$
Where:
- $\pi_t - \pi^*$: Inflation gap.
- $x_t$: Output gap.
- $Gap\_Fin_t$: **Macrofinancial Imbalance** (usually measured by the **Credit Gap** or Asset Price Misalignment).
- $\gamma$: The weight the central bank places on financial stability.

## Strategic Shifts (Table 15.2)

Perry identifies three approaches to integrating FSS and Price Stability:

1. **Modification of Jackson Hole (Separation):** Monetary policy for prices, Macroprudential for FSS. Interaction is limited.
2. **Leaning Against the Wind:** Monetary policy explicitly reacts to credit booms even if inflation is low.
3. **Twin Targets (Full Integration):** FSS and Price Stability are treated as equal mandates with inseparable transmission channels.

## Operational Implications
IIT requires the central bank to use its interest rate tool pre-emptively to dampen credit bubbles (**Leaning**), recognizing that "Stability is destabilizing" (Minsky).
