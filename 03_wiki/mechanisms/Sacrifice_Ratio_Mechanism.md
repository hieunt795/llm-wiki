## Thesis

Disinflation is not free. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự đánh đổi: để hạ nhiệt lạm phát, NHTW thường phải thắt chặt tiền tệ, làm chậm tăng trưởng và tăng thất nghiệp trong ngắn hạn. **Tỷ lệ hy sinh (Sacrifice Ratio)** là thước đo "độ đau đớn" của quá trình này. Một NHTW có uy tín cao có thể giảm lạm phát với mức hy sinh thấp hơn nhiều so với một NHTW mất niềm tin. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=171]]

---

## 1. The Mathematical Definition

The ratio is derived from the slope of the **Short-run Phillips Curve**:
$$\text{Sacrifice Ratio} = \frac{\sum (y - y^*)}{\Delta \pi}$$
- **Numerator:** The cumulative sum of the negative output gap over the disinflation period.
- **Denominator:** The total percentage points of inflation reduced. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=172]]

---

## 2. Determinants of the Ratio

Perry identifies three factors that dictate the "pain" level:

### A. The Slope of the Phillips Curve
- **Flat Curve:** Means inflation is very stubborn (insensitive to output). It takes a *massive* recession to lower inflation even slightly (High Sacrifice Ratio).
- **Steep Curve:** Inflation responds quickly to cooling demand (Low Sacrifice Ratio). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=171]]

### B. Credibility and Expectations
- **The Lucas Critique Link:** If the central bank is credible, agents lower their inflation expectations ($\pi^e$) immediately. The Phillips curve shifts down, allowing inflation to fall **without** a major output drop.
- **The Anchor:** Anchored expectations are the only way to achieve a "costless" disinflation. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=172]]

### C. Hysteresis Effects (The Long-term Scar)
In some cases, short-term unemployment during disinflation becomes permanent (Hysteresis), lowering the country's **Potential Output** ($y^*$). This turns a temporary "sacrifice" into a permanent economic loss. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=172]]

---

## Strategic Implications: Gradualism vs. Cold Turkey

Central banks must choose the **Speed** of disinflation:
- **Cold Turkey (Shock):** Rapid rate hikes to break inflation quickly. Risks high immediate unemployment but earns credibility fast.
- **Gradualism:** Slowly lowering the target over several years. Reduces the annual "pain" but risks unanchoring expectations if the process takes too long. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=173]]
