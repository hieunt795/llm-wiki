## Thesis

Monetary policy affects more than just the "price of debt." Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của sự lan tỏa: sự thay đổi lãi suất chính sách kích hoạt một đợt **Tái cân bằng danh mục (Portfolio Rebalancing)** trên toàn hệ thống. Khi lãi suất giảm, nhà đầu tư rời bỏ tiền mặt để tìm kiếm lợi nhuận ở trái phiếu, cổ phiếu và bất động sản, từ đó đẩy giá các tài sản này lên và tạo ra những cú hích kép cho đầu tư và tiêu dùng. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=126]]

## 1. Transmission to Investment (Tobin's q)

Tobin's q is the ratio of the **Market Value of Firms** to the **Replacement Cost of Capital**.
$$q = \frac{\text{Market Value}}{\text{Replacement Cost}}$$

### The Mechanism
- **The Trigger:** A reduction in the policy rate ($i \downarrow$) makes stocks more attractive than bonds (higher discounted value of future dividends), causing stock prices ($P_s \uparrow$) to rise.
- **The Logic:** If $q > 1$, the firm's market value is higher than the cost of new equipment. The firm can issue a small amount of stock to buy a large amount of new capital.
- **Outcome:** Corporate investment ($I$) increases. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=126-127]]

## 2. Transmission to Consumption (Wealth Effect)

Based on the **Lifecycle Hypothesis**, consumption is driven by the total resources available to a consumer over their lifetime, including **Net Worth**.

### The Mechanism
- **The Trigger:** Lower interest rates increase the prices of property, stocks, and gold.
- **The Logic:** Even if income hasn't changed, the consumer *feels* wealthier because their assets are worth more (wealth effect).
- **The Discount Effect:** Lower rates mean a lower discount factor for future income flows, elevating the **Current Value** of expected future returns.
- **Outcome:** Household consumption ($C$) rises. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=127]]

## Strategic Implications: Portfolio Formation

Perry Warjiyo highlights that this channel depends on the **Markowitz Portfolio Model**.
$$\max U(C, A) \text{ subject to } \sum pC = W_0 + \sum \frac{r}{\sigma} A$$
- **The Substitution:** The central bank rate ($r_{CB}$) acts as the "risk-free" anchor. Any change in $r_{CB}$ forces a mathematical adjustment in the required yields of bonds ($r_B$) and stocks ($d_S$).
- **EME Sensitivity:** This channel is most potent in economies with deep financial markets or high home-ownership rates. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=126]]
