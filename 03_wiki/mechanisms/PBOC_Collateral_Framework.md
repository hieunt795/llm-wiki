## MLF Collateral Eligibility

The PBOC Medium-term Lending Facility (MLF) collateral structure is bifurcated into standard and structural tiers:

### Standard Collateral (High-Quality)
- **Sovereign Assets:** Treasury bonds, central bank bills, and local government bonds.
- **Policy Bank Bonds:** Issued by CDB, ADBC, and EXIM.
- **Corporate Credit Bonds:** Standard requirement is **AAA** domestic rating.

### Structural Collateral (Targeted Expansion - since 2018)
To support the "real economy," the PBOC expanded eligibility to lower-rated assets for specific sectors:
- **Eligible Ratings:** **AA+** and **AA** domestic ratings.
- **Eligible Sectors:**
  - Small and Micro-Enterprises (SMEs)
  - Green Finance / Green Projects
  - Agricultural / Rural Development
- **Asset Types:** Includes both corporate bonds and high-quality credit loans provided to these sectors.

## 3. Policy Transmission & Plumbing
- **Operational Shift:** Since July 2024, the **7-day Reverse Repo Rate** has replaced the MLF rate as the primary signal for policy stance.
- **Liquidity Tool:** While the OMO 7-day rate anchors the corridor, the MLF serves as the vehicle for long-term liquidity and structural credit support via the collateral framework described above.
- **Haircut Policy:** The PBOC applies variable haircuts based on credit quality and liquidity, effectively managing balance sheet risk while incentivizing commercial banks to hold the targeted structural assets (AA+/AA).

## 4. Evaluation & Risk Control Mechanism
The PBOC employs a multi-layered assessment architecture for non-AAA assets (AA+/AA) and credit loans to ensure balance sheet integrity:

### 4.1. The 3-in-1 Assessment System
- **Internal Rating (央行内部评级):** The PBOC does not rely solely on domestic Rating Agencies. It operates an independent internal rating model for enterprises and financial assets to re-verify credit quality before accepting them as collateral.
- **Green Finance Evaluation Scheme (银行业金融机构绿色金融评价方案):** A systematic evaluation of commercial banks based on:
    - **80% Quantitative:** Total volume, share, and growth of green finance business.
    - **20% Qualitative:** Adherence to national green finance policies.
    - Banks with high scores receive preferential access and liquidity terms when pledging green assets.
- **Dynamic Haircut Policy (折损率):** The PBOC uses a granular **Haircut (折损率)** system. Lower-rated (AA) or less liquid assets are subject to higher haircuts, effectively pricing in the credit risk differential compared to standard AAA government/policy bank bonds.

### 4.2. Verification Authorities
- **PBoC (Central Bank):** Final authority on haircut levels and eligibility. Utilizes the **PBC Credit Reference Center** to verify the debt repayment history of underlying SME/Green borrowers.
- **Third-Party Verifiers:** Mandatory for specialized facilities (e.g., CERF) to provide Second-Party Opinions (SPOs) on environmental impact.
- **Depositories (CCDC/SHCH):** Provide technical infrastructure for collateral management and real-time valuation.
