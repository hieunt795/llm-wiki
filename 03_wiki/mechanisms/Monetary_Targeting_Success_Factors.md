## Thesis

While the US and UK failed with monetary targeting due to unstable velocity, Germany (Bundesbank) and Switzerland (SNB) thrived. Their secret was treating the money supply target not as a "holy grail" but as a **Communication Anchor** to signal long-term inflation commitment.

## The Three Pillars of Success

1. **Simplicity over Technocracy:** Avoiding overly complex econometric definitions of money.
2. **Transparent Communication:** Focusing on the *rationale* behind targets. When the Bundesbank overshot its target, it clearly explained why (e.g., post-reunification expansion) and how it would return to the path.
3. **Implicit Flexibility:** While officially a monetary targeter, the Bundesbank behaved implicitly like an inflation targeter, allowing for "Constrained Discretion" to manage output and exchange rates.

## Lessons Learned
- Monetary targeting can work if the final inflation goal is clearly announced.
- Rigid rules are unnecessary; market perception of the central bank's **Commitment** matters more than the specific target accuracy.
