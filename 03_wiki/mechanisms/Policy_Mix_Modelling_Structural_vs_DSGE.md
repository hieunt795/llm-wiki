## Thesis

To implement a **Policy Mix**, the central bank's "Brain" (the modelling suite) must be upgraded. Simple IS-LM models are insufficient. The bank needs models that account for **Bank Capital**, **Credit Frictions**, and **Non-resident Portfolio Shifts**.

## Modelling Approaches

### 1. Integrated Inflation Targeting (IIF) Structural Models
- **Focus:** Practical, data-driven forecasting.
- **Innovation:** Adding a **Financial Block** to the traditional gap models (NKPC/IS).
- **Advantage:** Highly intuitive and useful for monthly policy meetings.

### 2. DSGE (Dynamic Stochastic General Equilibrium) Models
- **Focus:** Theoretical consistency and micro-foundations.
- **Innovation:** Modelling banks as **Active Intermediaries** (Smets-Wouters or Christiano-Eichenbaum-Evans extensions).
- **Advantage:** Allows for "Scenario Analysis" of extreme shocks (e.g., a 2008-style credit freeze) and avoids the Lucas Critique.

## The Model Consensus Strategy
Bank Indonesia uses both:
- **ARIMBI/SOFIE:** Structural models for short-term "Real-time" forecasting.
- **MODBI/DSGE:** For long-term strategic testing of macroprudential rule settings (e.g., setting the CCyB rate).
