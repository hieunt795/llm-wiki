## Thesis

Tobin's q provides a micro-foundation for the **Asset Price Channel**. It explains why firms issue new equity or take on debt to finance physical investment during bull markets: when $q > 1$, the market values the firm's existing capital more than the cost to reproduce it, signaling an incentive to expand.

## Mechanism

### 1. The Ratio
$$q = \frac{\text{Market Value of Assets}}{\text{Replacement Cost of Assets}}$$

### 2. Monetary Transmission Chain
- **Step 1:** Central Bank lowers the policy rate.
- **Step 2:** Stock market prices rise due to a lower discount rate and higher expected future earnings.
- **Step 3:** The firm's market value increases, while the replacement cost (physical price of machinery/buildings) remains relatively stable or rises slowly.
- **Step 4:** $q$ increases. The firm can issue a small amount of new equity to buy a large amount of new physical equipment.
- **Step 5:** Investment and aggregate demand ($AD$) increase.

## Research Insight: Connection to Cost of Capital
Perry Warjiyo notes that Tobin-q analysis is the asset-price equivalent of the **User Cost of Capital** approach used in the interest rate channel. While interest rates focus on the debt side, Tobin's q focuses on the equity and valuation side.
