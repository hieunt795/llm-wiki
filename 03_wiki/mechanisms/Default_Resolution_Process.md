## Thesis
Default proceedings have the conflicting aims of respecting contractual priorities and ensuring equitable treatment of creditors of equal rank. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=260]]

## Mechanism

### 1. Managed Default (Reorganization)
- **Stay:** A court decision that halts wind-down proceedings, allowing for negotiations on debt reduction or rescheduling. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=263]]
- **Managed Default:** A non-antagonistic resolution aiming for eventual debtor recovery. If it fails, the entity transitions to a wind-down. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=260]]
- **US Bankruptcy Code Examples:**
    - **Chapter 11:** Reorganization for private companies.
    - **Chapter 9:** Reorganization for municipalities.
    - **Chapter 7:** Liquidation for private enterprises. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=260]]

### 2. Wind-Down (Liquidation)
- **Cessation of Activity:** The final stage where assets are sold to pay out creditors. It is preferred when there is no realistic prospect of business recovery. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=263]]
- **Liquidation Discounts:** Assets sold in "firesales" incur substantial discounts to their carrying value due to specialization and urgency. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=260]]
- **Timeline:** Can take years, especially for banks with long-dated contractual assets (e.g., Herstatt Bank wind-down took 32 years). [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=263]]

### 3. Pre-Default Tools
- **Consent Solicitation:** Issuer asks investors to voluntarily accept changes to bond terms (e.g., covenant relaxation) to avoid default. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=262]]
- **Exchange Offer:** Issuer proposes swapping one bond for another, often with incentives. This is a game-theory scenario where participating investors may enforce terms on others via reduced liquidity for non-participating "hold-outs." [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=262]]

### Boundaries / Conditions
- **Jurisdictional Conflicts:** Creditors may face conflicting rulings from multiple courts if the debtor has assets or liabilities distributed across different legal systems. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=260]]
- **Debtor in Possession (DIP):** Lenders advancing funds during a managed default (to cover working capital) are granted special protection and higher seniority. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch25.md#page=263]]
