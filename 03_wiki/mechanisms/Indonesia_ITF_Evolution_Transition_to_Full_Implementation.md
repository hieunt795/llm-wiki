## Thesis
[RAW] ITF implementation in Indonesia faced multiple teething problems requiring systematic resolution during 2000-2005 transition period, reflecting broader challenges unique to post-crisis emerging markets undertaking institutional monetary policy reform.

## 1. Core Logic - Five Critical Constraints During Transition

[RAW] Perry Warjiyo (2002) and supporting literature identified five fundamental obstacles that had to be overcome for successful full ITF implementation:

### Constraint 1: Political Transition Undermining Policy Credibility
[RAW] Indonesian political democratization (1998 onward) created policy uncertainty:
- Ongoing political transition process weakening effectiveness of public policies including monetary policy
- Credible ITF implementation required improved public perception of government general policy climate
- Critical dependency: Government, People's Representative Council, and public support for inflation control commitment
- Amendment to BI Act (Act 3 of 2004) addressed this by government inflation target determination → enhanced public perception and commitment coordination

[LLM] This illustrates principal-agent theory application: Public (principal) must empower central bank (agent) through coordinated government-central bank inflation target framework for credibility to take hold.

### Constraint 2: Government Financial Sustainability Crisis
[RAW] Post-1997 financial crisis fiscal pressures created monetary policy transmission blockage:
- Indonesian government faced deepening debt crisis
- Domestic debt from bank recapitalization bonds ~50% of GDP
- **Critical problem**: Two-thirds of recapitalization bonds had variable interest rates tied to 3-month SBI rate
- **Transmission mechanism failure**: If inflationary pressures forced SBI rate hikes to achieve inflation target, government debt servicing burden would spike immediately in state budget
- **Fiscal dominance risk**: Central bank hesitation on monetary tightening due to fiscal sustainability concerns

[LLM] This illustrates fundamental ITF prerequisite: Requires "relatively strong fiscal position" (Schaechter et al., 2000) to avoid fiscal dominance override of monetary policy independence

### Constraint 3: Banking Sector Fragility and Credit Transmission Blockage
[RAW] Indonesian banking system recovery incomplete during transition period:
- **Credit crunch phenomenon** endemic to post-crisis banking system
- **Interest rate transmission lag**: Banking sector response to SBI rate changes slow and protracted
- **Dominance dependency**: Banking system dominates Indonesia's financial system, so monetary transmission through credit channel severely undermined
- **Cost of monetary control elevated**: Larger SBI rate movements required to achieve same real rate impact on credit and spending
- **Corporate restructuring decoupling**: Bank liquidity circulating in financial sector rather than real sector
- **Systemic exposure**: Financial-real sector decoupling exposed financial sector to risks, complicated monetary policy implementation

[LLM] This is a transmission mechanism failure distinct from monetary policy design—requires time for financial sector balance sheet repair and credit intermediation normalization.

### Constraint 4: Monetary Framework Technical Development Inadequacy
[RAW] Operational and technical gaps in BI's capacity to implement ITF:
- Inflation projection accuracy problems—economic models still under development
- **Limited understanding** of monetary policy transmission mechanism in post-crisis context (structural changes ongoing)
- **Operational target determination** challenges—which instrument best achieves inflation target?
- Economic and financial structural changes complicating modeling and forecasting
- Human resource capability constraints relative to advanced central banks

[LLM] This reflects institutional development constraint: ITF implementation requires modern central bank analytical capacity in modeling, data collection, and economic projection techniques.

### Constraint 5: IMF Program Constraints on Framework Transition
[RAW] IMF monetary program imposed operational target restrictions during transition:
- IMF program architecture based on money supply channel (quantity-based targeting)
- Operational target: Base money—linked to IMF demand for money in economy consistent with inflation target
- **Technical problems**: Base money behavior became harder to control due to:
  - Changing public demand for money during transition period
  - Seasonal factors (school holidays, Eid-ul-Fitr, Christmas, New Year)
  - Money multiplier instability from banking sector dynamics
- **Framework linkage gap**: Difficult to control base money and simultaneously link to ITF monetary policy framework
- **Implementation timing constraint**: Could not fully move to interest rate targeting while under IMF program requirements

## 2. Worked Example - Resolution Path: March 2004 Decision Point

[RAW] **BI Board of Governors Decision (March 4, 2004)**:
After adequate preparation with Act No. 3 of 2004 amendments and improving economic conditions, BI formally decided to implement full ITF with interest rate operational target.

**Supporting Developments:**
1. Act No. 3 of 2004 amendment addressing political uncertainty (inflation target government determination → enhanced public ownership)
2. Macroeconomic stabilization progress improving fiscal sustainability perception
3. Banking sector recovery accelerating
4. BI technical capacity maturation (models, projection systems, analytical capability)
5. IMF program conclusion/transition reducing base money targeting constraints

**Implementation Transition (July 1, 2005 onward):**
- Shift to BI Rate (overnight interbank rate) as operational target
- Published inflation targets with medium-long term horizons
- Transparent communication of inflation projections and policy rationale
- Accountability framework operational
- Structural organizational changes (new departments, enhanced capability)

## 3. Success Indicators Post-2005 Implementation

[RAW] Despite multiple challenges and occasional target misses (2005, 2008, 2013, 2014 due to fuel price shocks and commodity pressures), ITF implementation achieved:

1. **Institutional Maturation**: BI transformed from inward-focused to outward-focused organization
2. **Policy Signal Clarity**: BI Rate became key reference indicator for markets and business community
3. **Expectation Formation Shift**: Public expectations shifted from backward-looking to forward-looking
4. **Inflation Persistence Reduction**: Degree of inflation persistence declined measurably
5. **Credibility Improvement**: Slow but steady monetary policy credibility building despite external shocks
