## Thesis

The central bank is the **ultimate settlement agent**. While commercial banks create **Inside Money** (deposits), the final settlement of obligations between those banks must occur in **Central Bank Money** (Outside Money) to eliminate credit risk.

## Mechanism

### 1. The Core Process
- **Authorization:** Customer to Bank instruction.
- **Clearing:** Exchange of payment instructions between banks.
- **Settlement:** The final transfer of funds across the central bank's books.

### 2. Large Value Payment Systems (LVPS)
- **RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement):** Transactions are settled individually and immediately. This eliminates "settlement risk" but increases the demand for intraday liquidity.
- **Systemic Risk:** A failure in the LVPS (e.g., a major bank defaulting on a settlement) can trigger a "liquidity gridlock" where no other payments can move.

### 3. Retail Payment Innovation & FinTech
- **Innovation:** ATM, e-money, mobile banking.
- **Fragmentation Risk:** FinTech companies often operate outside the formal banking perimeter. If they become large without being integrated into the central bank's stability framework, they create "Knowledge Gaps" and systemic blind spots.

## The Central Bank as "Market Maker of First Resort"
During financial distress, when interbank markets freeze and clearing fails, the central bank must step in not just as LoLR to one bank, but as the operator of the entire system to ensure the "plumbing" of the economy keeps working.
