## Thesis
Market microstructure analysis shows that short-term exchange rate fluctuations are driven by order flows—the net of buy and sell orders—which transmit private information and reflect heterogeneous expectations among market players. [extracted]

## Mechanism
Order flows ($O_t$) act as the medium to transmit private information into exchange rate fluctuations ($\Delta S$). [extracted]

### The Model
The exchange rate return is modeled as:
$$\Delta S_{t+1} = \beta_r(r - r^*) + \beta_z O_t$$
where:
- $(r - r^*)$: Macroeconomic fundamental (interest rate differential).
- $O_t$: Order flow (signed volume of transactions).

### Characteristics
1. **Information Heterogeneity:** Market players have different understanding/perceptions of fundamentals.
2. **Private Information:** Large banks have excess information due to customer networks.
3. **Transaction Transmission:** News causes exchange rate fluctuations only when order flows transmit it. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- Short-term: Fundamental models (PPP, IRP) fail to predict short-term fluctuations (Exchange Rate Puzzle).
- Microstructure focuses on quote-driven segments (Customer-Dealer) and bid-ask segments (Inter-dealer). [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **Evans & Lyons (2002):** Found the model explains ~70% of exchange rate fluctuations. [extracted]
- **Surveys (Cheung & Chinn, 2001):** Dealers believe exchange rates move in response to trading flows rather than pure macro news. [extracted]
