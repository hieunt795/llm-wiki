## Thesis

NHTW in EMEs must monitor two separate "Weather Maps" to predict capital flows. High-frequency flows (PI/Hot Money) are almost entirely driven by the **Push** factors of the US economy, while long-term stability (FDI) depends on domestic **Pull** factors.

## The Determinant Matrix (Table 13.2)

| Factor Group | Variables | Impact on PI (Stocks/Bonds) | Impact on FDI |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PUSH** (Global) | **VIX / Risk Perception** | **Negative Strong** | Inconclusive |
| | **Advanced Interest Rates** | **Negative Strong** | Inconclusive |
| | **Advanced GDP Growth** | **Positive** (Probability only) | Inconclusive |
| **PULL** (Domestic) | **Domestic GDP Growth** | **Positive Strong** | **Positive Strong** |
| | **Domestic Asset Yields** | **Positive Strong** | Inconclusive |
| | **Sovereign Rating** | **Positive Strong** | **Positive Strong** |

## The "Flight-to-Safety" Mechanism
Calvo and Reinhart (1996) showed that during global distress, the **Push** factors become so dominant that they trigger **Contagion**. Even EMEs with "perfect" domestic fundamentals (strong Pull factors) suffer massive outflows as institutional investors hit their VaR limits and retreat to safe-haven assets (USD/Gold).

## Mathematical Formulation
$$F_t = \alpha + \beta X_t + \delta Y_t + \phi Z_t$$
Where:
- $X_t$: Push factor vector (External shocks).
- $Y_t$: Pull factor vector (Domestic fundamentals).
- $Z_t$: Institutional factors (FX regime, depth).
