## Thesis

Blinder (1998) famously said that central banks "manage expectations." In modern markets, the **Signalling Channel** is often more powerful than the physical OMO. If a central bank can convince the market of its future path, the long-term bond yields will adjust automatically without the bank spending a single dollar in the market.

## Stakeholder Matrix

As detailed by Blinder et al. (2001), communication must be tailored to four primary targets:

| Target | Primary Information Need | Communication Tool |
|---|---|---|
| **Financial Markets** | Future interest rate path, liquidity conditions. | Press releases, Minutes, Data releases. |
| **Govt & Parliament** | Compliance with the mandate, social costs. | Public testimony, Annual Reports. |
| **Mass Media / Public** | Rationale for price changes (CPI), cost of living. | Press conferences, simple brochures, social media. |
| **CB Observers / Academics** | Theoretical foundations, structural models. | Research papers, Seminars, Modeling workshops. |

## Rationale: Reducing Adjustment Costs
If the public fully understands the central bank's regime (e.g., ITF), they will anticipate the bank's response to shocks.
- **Effect:** When a shock occurs, prices and wages adjust *voluntarily* toward the target.
- **Benefit:** This reduces the **Inside Lag** (recognition) and the **Sacrifice Ratio**, making the transition to low inflation less painful.

## Boundaries / Conditions: The Risk of Over-communication
"More information is not always better." Excessive or contradictory communication can create noise and increase market volatility. The goal is **Accuracy and Clarity**, not pure volume.
