# [[Monetary_Policy_Credibility_Institutional]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] Policy credibility is built through the "consistency between what is said and what is done," reducing the inflation bias caused by political interference and allowing price stability to be achieved with minimal output loss.

## Definition
[RAW] Policy credibility can be defined as central bank commitment to comply with the rules or policy goals articulated in a transparent manner... Built through consistency between what is said and what is done. In essence... credibility is a function of the difference between actual inflation and the central bank’s inflation target. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=292]]

[RAW] Higher credibility at a central bank is generally believed to enhance the implementation effectiveness of monetary policy itself because if a central bank is credible and the public have rational expectations... achievement of price stability could be strived toward without sacrificing economic growth. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=292]]

[LLM] The institutionalization of central bank independence (CBI) is a primary tool to build this credibility, as it insulates monetary policy from short-term political pressures (inflation bias) and long-standing debates over debt monetization.
