## Thesis

The success of currency depreciation as a tool for economic stimulus depends on the **Marshall–Lerner Condition**. However, Alexander Düring bóc tách ranh giới của thanh khoản, trong khi Perry Warjiyo giải mã cơ chế tại sao **Thời gian là kẻ thù của sự điều chỉnh**. Hiệu ứng J-Curve chứng minh rằng trong ngắn hạn, phá giá đồng nội tệ thường làm trầm trọng thêm thâm hụt thương mại trước khi mang lại lợi ích thực sự. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=98]]

## 1. The Marshall–Lerner Condition
Depreciation improves the trade balance **only if** the sum of price elasticities of demand for exports and imports is greater than unity:
$$\epsilon_x + \epsilon_m > 1$$
- **EME Context:** This condition is typically met in countries where manufactured goods (which are price-sensitive) dominate exports. In commodity-heavy economies, this condition may fail in the short run. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=98]]

## 2. The J-Curve Dynamic

The path of the trade balance follows the shape of the letter 'J' over two distinct phases:

### Phase 1: The Contractual Lag (The Dip)
- **Mechanism:** Immediately after depreciation, the price of imports rises while export prices remain fixed in foreign currency terms.
- **The Catch:** Because trade contracts are signed months in advance (Nominal Rigidity), the **Volume** of goods cannot change instantly.
- **Outcome:** The country pays more for the same amount of imports, and the trade balance worsens. In Indonesia, this phase typically lasts **3 to 4 quarters** (9–12 months). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=98]] [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=100]]

### Phase 2: The Quantity Adjustment (The Rise)
- **Mechanism:** Over time, domestic consumers find substitutes for expensive imports, and foreign consumers switch to cheaper domestic exports.
- **The Shift:** Volume adjustments eventually outweigh the price effect.
- **Outcome:** The trade balance moves into surplus or improves significantly beyond its initial level. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=98]]

## Strategic Implications: Policy Patience

Perry Warjiyo highlights that the J-curve necessitates **Central Bank Patience**:
- **The Risk:** Policymakers may panic during Phase 1, seeing the trade balance worsen despite depreciation, leading to premature rate hikes.
- **The Evidence:** Empirical studies in Indonesia (Winarno, 2014) confirm that each 1% depreciation eventually raises the current account by 0.51%, but only after the 4-quarter J-curve lag is cleared. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=100]]
