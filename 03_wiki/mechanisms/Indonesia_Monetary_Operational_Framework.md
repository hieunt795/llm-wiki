# [[Monetary_Policy_Operational_Framework]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] An effective operational framework allows a central bank to translate its final goals (inflation/output stability) into daily actions. This involves choosing an operational target (e.g., O/N interbank rate or base money) that is controllable and has a stable transmission to the real economy.

## Conceptual Dimension: Instrument Selection

### Money Supply vs. Interest Rate (Poole Model)
[RAW] The selection of optimal monetary policy instruments is determined by the origin of the shock. If the demand function for currency is relatively stable compared to the demand for goods, then money supply is superior. If spending shocks are more volatile, the interest rate is preferred. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=194]]

[LLM] In modern economies with unstable money demand (velocity shocks), the interest rate has become the dominant instrument because it insulates the real economy from monetary sector volatility.

### Rules vs. Discretion
[RAW] A rule-based monetary policy response is more appropriate in response to current dynamics already taken into account in previous formulations. A discretion-based response is based on evaluations over time of ongoing dynamics, disregarding past developments. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=198]]

[LLM] Perry emphasizes that "rules are a prerequisite for sound monetary policy," but implementation often involves "constrained discretion"—using rules like the Taylor Rule or McCallum Rule as guides rather than rigid formulas.

## Application in EMEs and Indonesia

### Shift from Quantity to Price
[RAW] Developing countries (including Mexico, India, Thailand, and Indonesia) continued to apply monetary aggregates until the early 2000s. Nearly all central banks ultimately selected short-term interest rates as the operational target as financial systems established. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=205]]

[LLM] The transition in Indonesia (culminating in July 2005) was driven by the breakdown of the stable relationship between base money and inflation (unstable money multiplier).

### Indonesia's BI Rate Framework
[RAW] BI officially applies the interest rate as the operational target. The O/N interbank rate is a feasible operational target due to its measurability and controllability through signaling and liquidity adjustment mechanisms. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=219]]

[LLM] The BI Rate serves as the policy anchor, gimbaling expectations and steering the interbank market toward the desired stance.
