## Thesis

LTV is the "Surgical Knife" of the central bank. Unlike interest rates, which affect the entire economy, LTV can be targeted specifically at an overheating housing sector. It reduces both the **Demand** for speculative loans and the **Loss Given Default (LGD)** for banks.

## Mechanism: Dampening the Financial Accelerator

1. **In a Boom:** The central bank **lowers** the maximum LTV (e.g., from 90% to 70%). This requires borrowers to provide a larger down-payment, reducing credit demand even if interest rates are low.
2. **In a Bust:** The central bank **raises** the LTV to stimulate demand and support falling asset prices.

### The Buffer Function
By enforcing a gap (e.g., 30% equity), LTV ensures that even if property prices drop by 20%, the loan remains "over-collateralized," preventing bank insolvency and fire sales.

## Case Study: Indonesia
Bank Indonesia (BI) has used LTV aggressively since 2012 to manage property bubbles.
- **Key Innovation:** Tiered LTV based on the number of properties owned (e.g., stricter limits for a 2nd or 3rd house) to target speculators rather than first-time home buyers.
