# [[Demand_for_Central_Bank_Reserves]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] Reserve requirements (RR) act as a structural source of demand for central bank credit, forcing the banking system into a net "deficit" position that must be filled by monetary policy operations. [[Demand_for_Central_Bank_Reserves#page=114]]

## Mechanism
[RAW] Reserve requirements increase banks' funding needs vis-à-vis the central bank. If $RR = rD$, total central bank credit becomes $B + rD$. [[Demand_for_Central_Bank_Reserves#page=114]]

[RAW] Collateral constraints are reached earlier because required reserves held with the central bank are typically not counted as eligible collateral. [[Demand_for_Central_Bank_Reserves#page=114]]

[LLM] For example, if a bank's corporate loans have a haircut $h$, the collateral constraint becomes $(1-h)(D+B) \ge B + rD$. This implies that higher $RR$ reduces a bank's effective lending capacity by consuming its collateral buffer.

[RAW] Non-remunerated reserve requirements increase the cost of bank credit to the real economy by $r \cdot i_D / (D+B)$, where $i_D$ is the interest rate paid on deposits. [[Demand_for_Central_Bank_Reserves#page=114]]

[RAW] Reserve requirements with averaging provide a buffer against day-to-day autonomous factor shocks, allowing for less frequent open market operations. [[Demand_for_Central_Bank_Reserves#page=114]]
