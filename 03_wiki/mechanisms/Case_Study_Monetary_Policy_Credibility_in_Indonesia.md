# [[Case_Study_Monetary_Policy_Credibility_in_Indonesia]]

## Thesis
[WIKI/LLM] Empirical analysis of Bank Indonesia (BI) reveals that institutional independence and a single mandate for price stability are critical for overcoming the time inconsistency problem. The transition post-2000 eliminated asymmetric preferences toward the output gap, fostering greater credibility under the Inflation Targeting Framework (ITF).

## Mechanism

### 1. Pre-Independence Era (1990–1999): Time Inconsistency
[RAW] Prior to independence, BI operated under Act No. 13 of 1968 with a multiple mandate: maintaining rupiah stability and stimulating production/development to expand jobs. This "double mandate" created an **asymmetric preference** toward the output gap, as BI acted as an agent of development. Consequently, monetary policy was time-inconsistent, characterized by an overly loose stance during upswings and excessively tight stances during downturns. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=315]] [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=316]]

### 2. Post-Independence Era (2000–2009): Time Consistency
[RAW] Pursuant to Act No. 23 of 1999, BI became independent with a single mandate of rupiah stability. Empirical studies (Rahmahdian and Warjiyo, 2013) found that post-independence, BI’s preference toward the output gap became **symmetrical**. This shift reflected a commitment to low inflation and reduced discretion, successfully eliminating the influence of the output gap on inflation and improving the performance of the Inflation Targeting Framework (ITF). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=315]] [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=316]]

### 3. Implications for Credibility and Communication
[RAW] Despite improvements, BI faces "imperfect credibility" due to the relative novelty of the full ITF and high inflation persistence. Warjiyo emphasizes three strategic needs:
- **Consistency:** Reducing discretion and adhering to clear rules to restore public confidence.
- **Coordination:** Using mechanisms like the National Inflation Task Force (TPI) and Regional Inflation Task Forces (TPID) to manage non-monetary inflation (Administered Prices and Volatile Foods). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=317]]
- **Communication:** Shifting public expectations from backward-looking to forward-looking through transparent strategy to anchor expectations to the inflation target. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P4.md#page=317]]
