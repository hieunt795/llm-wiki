## Thesis

The Real Bills Doctrine was the operational "soul" of Episode 1 of central banking. Perry Warjiyo bóc tách ranh giới của một cơ chế tự điều chỉnh: bằng cách ghim chặt việc phát hành tiền vào dòng lưu chuyển của hàng hóa thực tế, NHTW tạo ra một hệ thống nơi **Cung tiền tự động co dãn theo nhu cầu kinh tế thực**. Đây là nỗ lực sớm nhất để ngăn chặn sự tách rời giữa khu vực tài chính và khu vực thực. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=23]]

## 1. Mechanism: The Self-Liquidating Loop

The doctrine operates on two inseparable pillars:

### A. Real Bills (The Productive Asset)
Banks only discount (buy) short-term commercial papers that represent **Goods-in-Process**.
- **Example:** A baker issues a bill to buy flour. Once the bread is sold, the loan is repaid. 
- **The Result:** The money created to fund the flour is destroyed when the bread is sold. The money supply expands only as long as the goods are in the pipeline. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=23]]

### B. Finance Bills (The Speculative Enemy)
NHTW quyết liệt từ chối chiết khấu các "Finance Bills"—những hóa đơn phát hành để mua tài sản tài chính (cổ phiếu, đất đai).
- **The Rationale:** Những hóa đơn này không tự thanh khoản qua sản xuất. Chúng tạo ra tiền để đẩy giá tài sản lên, tạo bong bóng mà không làm tăng sản lượng thực. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=23]]

---

## 2. Strategic Advantages: Automatic Stability

Under the Real Bills framework, two stability goals are achieved simultaneously:
1.  **Price Stability:** Total money circulating is always proportional to real trade/output ($MV = PY$ is held stable by the nature of the bills).
2.  **Financial Stability:** Credit and business cycles move in tandem. There is no "Excess Credit" to fund bubbles. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=23]]

---

## 3. The Failure: The Procyclical Trap

Perry Warjiyo highlights why this "perfect" doctrine collapsed during the Great Depression (1929):
- **The Flaw:** Real Bills is inherently **Procyclical**.
- **The Bust:** When the economy slows, the volume of "Real Bills" drops. According to the doctrine, the central bank **must** reduce the money supply.
- **The Result:** The Fed tightened policy in 1928-1929 because speculative "Finance Bills" were rising while Real Bills were stagnant. This turned a market correction into a catastrophic depression. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=24]]

---

## Strategic Implications: Lessons for ITF

Perry Warjiyo argues that modern **Inflation Targeting** faces the same problem. 
- **The Link:** While ITF manages the *price* of money (rates), it does not monitor the *quality* of credit.
- **Inference:** Central banks need a "Modern Real Bills" lens—**Macroprudential Policy**—to distinguish between productive credit and speculative bubbles that ITF rates often inadvertently fuel. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P2.md#page=24]]
