# [[Monetary_Policy_Transmission_Indonesia]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] The effectiveness of monetary policy in Indonesia is determined by multiple channels—interest rate, credit, exchange rate, and expectations—which are frequently impaired by bank disintermediation, risk perception, and procyclical financial behavior.

## Mechanism
[RAW] The latest comprehensive study conducted by BI showed various interesting changes, especially in the wake of the Asian Financial Crisis... First, the credit channel was shown to significantly influence monetary policy transmission because of the bank disintermediation problem. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=268]]

[RAW] The exchange rate channel when formulating monetary policy... exchange rate developments have a strong influence on inflation due to the direct impact of changing import prices (direct pass-through) and indirect impact through the output gap (indirect pass-through). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=269]]

[LLM] Transmission via the interest rate channel is often asymmetric; lending rates respond with a lag to policy rate cuts, especially during periods of high risk perception or financial sector procyclicality, such as the Global Financial Crisis.
