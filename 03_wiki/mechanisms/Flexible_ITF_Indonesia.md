# [[Flexible_ITF_Indonesia]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] Flexible ITF represents an evolution of standard inflation targeting that acknowledges the central role of financial stability and the "impossible trinity" by utilizing a multi-instrument policy mix rather than the policy rate alone.

## Mechanism
[RAW] ITF flexibility is reflected in the orientation to achieve the overriding goal, namely inflation, but while also paying due consideration to the dynamic performance of other macroeconomic variables in the short term, such as economic growth, exchange rates, and financial sector developments. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=279]]

[RAW] Five important factors are always considered when determining the policy rate... (1) two-year inflation forecast; (2) two-year economic growth forecast; (3) exchange rate forecast and affecting factors (including capital flows); (4) bank credit and interest rates; and (5) asset valuation in the financial sector. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=279]]

[LLM] The "Policy Mix" strategy involves four pillars: (1) interest rate policy (for inflation anchoring), (2) exchange rate policy (to manage volatility), (3) macroprudential policy (to manage liquidity and capital flows), and (4) policy coordination with the government.
