## Thesis

Initially established to manage the gold standard and consolidate currency (Episode 1), the central bank's modern role is a multidimensional mandate encompassing monetary policy, payment systems, and systemic stability. This evolution reflects the growing complexity of the global financial system.

## The Evolutionary Path

### 1. From Circulating Bank to "Banks' Bank"
Early central banks (like the Bank of England in the 17th century) began as private institutions rediscounting commercial paper. They eventually became the ultimate settlement agent, holding the accounts of commercial banks to facilitate clearing.

### 2. From LoLR to Macroprudential
- **Microprudential (Old Focus):** Ensuring the soundness of *individual* financial institutions.
- **Lender of Last Resort (LoLR):** Reactive provision of liquidity during a crisis (Bagehot's Rule).
- **Macroprudential (New Focus):** Managing *systemic risk* and the *financial cycle*. Tools like LTV (Loan-to-Value) and CCyB (Countercyclical Capital Buffers) are used to dampen procyclicality before a crisis occurs.

### 3. Payment System Modernization
The function has progressed from physical banknote printing to the regulation of RTGS (Real-Time Gross Settlement) systems, scripless securities settlement (e.g., BI-S4), and the oversight of FinTech business models.
