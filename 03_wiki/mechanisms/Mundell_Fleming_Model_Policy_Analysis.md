## Thesis

The model's most famous conclusion is that **policy effectiveness depends entirely on the exchange rate regime**. In a world of high capital mobility, monetary policy is powerful under floating rates but impotent under fixed rates, while the opposite is true for fiscal policy.

## Mechanism: Mathematical Formulation

The model's structure is based on the equilibrium of three markets:

1.  **Goods Market (IS Curve):**
    $$Y = C(Y, r) + I(Y, r) + G + NX(Y^*, Q)$$
    where $Q = E[P/S \cdot P^*]$ is the real exchange rate.
2.  **Money Market (LM Curve):**
    $$M^d/P = L(Y, r) = M^s/P$$
3.  **Foreign Exchange Market (FE/BP Curve):**
    $$BOP = CA(NX) + KA(r - r^* - E[\Delta S]) = 0$$

## Mechanism: Policy Effectiveness Matrix

| Policy | Fixed Exchange Rate | Floating Exchange Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Monetary Policy** | **Ineffective:** CB must use all reserves to defend the peg, neutralizing the original money supply change. | **Effective:** Rate cuts lead to depreciation, which boosts exports and further stimulates output ($Y$). |
| **Fiscal Policy** | **Effective:** Spending boosts $Y$ and rates; capital inflows force CB to expand money supply to defend the peg, adding further stimulus. | **Ineffective:** Spending boosts rates and attracts capital; the resulting appreciation crushes exports, cancelling out the original stimulus ("Crowding out"). |

## Analysis Framework: The FE Curve
The model adds the **Foreign Exchange (FE) curve** (also known as the BP curve) to the IS-LM diagram.
- **Slope:** Depends on the degree of cross-border capital mobility.
- **Perfect Capital Mobility:** FE curve is horizontal.
- **Zero Capital Mobility:** FE curve is vertical (common in systems with strict capital controls).

### The Policy Trilemma
The Mundell-Fleming model provides the mathematical proof for the **Monetary Policy Trilemma**: a country can only have two of (1) Capital Mobility, (2) Independent Monetary Policy, and (3) Fixed Exchange Rates.

## Empirical Evidence: ASEAN-5 Context
- **Marshall-Lerner Condition:** Proven for ASEAN-5, where exchange rate depreciation improves the trade balance after a temporary J-curve effect.
- **Growth Elasticity:** In Indonesia, the real exchange rate elasticity to GDP growth was identified at 0.14, while Singapore (0.88) showed the highest sensitivity.
- **J-Curve Duration:** For Indonesia, exchange rate depreciation reduces the real trade balance for approximately three quarters before the positive effects manifest.
