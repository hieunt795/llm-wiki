# [[Central_Bank_Reserves_and_Reserve_Requirements]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] The interbank money market is fundamentally the market for reserves (sight deposits) held at the central bank, where the demand side is determined by the sum of mandatory reserve requirements (RR) and voluntary excess reserves (XSR). [[Central_Bank_Reserves_and_Requirements#page=41]]

## Mechanism
[RAW] Banks have to maintain a non-negative reserve position. In the case of positive reserve requirements, banks must hold a specific level, denoted as $RR$. [[Central_Bank_Reserves_and_Requirements#page=41]]

[RAW] Often, banks also hold excess reserves ($XSR$), which are voluntary holdings distinct from liquidity injected by the central bank through outright purchase programs. [[Central_Bank_Reserves_and_Requirements#page=41]]

[LLM] The demand for reserves is thus expressed as $Demand = RR + XSR$. This demand interacts with autonomous factors (like banknotes and government deposits) to determine the net liquidity position of the banking system.

[RAW] Reserve requirements are often fulfilled on average over a maintenance period. This averaging mechanism allows banks to let liquidity flows affect reserve holdings temporarily, provided they meet the average target by the end of the period. [[Central_Bank_Reserves_and_Requirements#page=41]]

[RAW] This averaging mechanism can stabilize the overnight interbank rate and forms the basis for the martingale property of overnight rates within the maintenance period. [[Central_Bank_Reserves_and_Requirements#page=41]]
