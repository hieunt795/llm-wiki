## Thesis

Understanding MPTM is often described as opening the **"Black Box"** of economics. Because of behavioral changes, structural lags (6-8 quarters), and global spillovers, the central bank cannot simply "set" inflation; it can only influence it through a complex roadmap of interactions between the central bank, the financial sector, and economic agents.

## The Two Paradigms

As detailed in Chapter 5, the mechanism is bifurcated into:

### 1. The Money View (Neoclassical Channels)
Assumes efficient financial markets. Transmission occurs through changes in relative prices and asset valuations.
- **Interest Rate Channel:** Cost of capital and consumption smoothing.
- **Asset Price Channel:** Wealth effect and Tobin's q.
- **Exchange Rate Channel:** International trade and direct pass-through.
- **Expectations Channel:** Anchoring future forecasts.

### 2. The Credit View (Non-Neoclassical Channels)
Assumes market imperfections (asymmetric information, moral hazard). Transmission occurs through the availability of credit and bank behavior.
- **Bank Lending Channel:** Credit rationing and funding constraints.
- **Balance Sheet Channel:** Net worth and collateral constraints.
- **Bank Capital Channel:** Regulatory constraints (CAR/Basel).
- **Risk-taking Channel:** Search for yield and risk-tolerance shifts.

## Mechanical Factors (The Complexity)
- **Lag Profile:** Initial impact on financial markets (1-3 months) vs. terminal impact on CPI/GDP (18-24 months).
- **Leading Indicators:** M0, PUAB rates, credit growth, and exchange rate volatility.
- **Leading Target:** The policy rate (e.g., BI 7-day RR) as the starting point of the chain.
