## Thesis
The BI Rate is the primary policy rate used by Bank Indonesia to signal its monetary policy stance and anchor the short-term interest rate structure in the financial market. [extracted]

## Mechanism
1. **Signal Stance:** BI announces the rate monthly at the Board of Governors meeting to communicate the tightness or looseness of monetary policy. [extracted]
2. **Anchor:** Historically anchored the 1-month SBI rate; since 2008, it has focused on the **overnight interbank rate**. [extracted]
3. **Transmission:** Changes in the BI Rate are intended to transmit to term deposit rates, lending rates, and ultimately the real economy. [extracted]

### Criteria for Selection
The policy rate must be:
- Controllable by BI.
- Influential over the cost of funds in banks.
- Effective as a reference for economic agents. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- **Disintermediation:** Effectiveness is undermined if banks are slow to adjust lending rates due to high NPLs or weak competition. [extracted]
- **Liquidity:** During the 2008 crisis, the transmission sensitivity of lending rates to the BI Rate faded due to procyclical risk perception. [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **2005 Ingest:** Shifted from base money targeting to the BI Rate operational target on July 1, 2005. [extracted]
