## Thesis

Under the old paradigm, transmission stopped at CPI/GDP. In the **New Paradigm**, the central bank recognizes that its own policy actions change the "Structural Integrity" of the financial system, creating feedback loops that can either stabilize or destroy macro-stability.

## The Dual Link Mechanism (Table 15.1)

### Link 1: Interest Rate $\to$ Financial Instability
Lowering the policy rate ($r \downarrow$) improves stability in the short term but may create long-term risks:
- **Balance Sheet Channel:** Improves net worth and lowers default probability *now*.
- **Risk-Taking Channel:** Encourages banks to lower lending standards in search of yield.
- **Asset Price Channel:** Inflates collateral values, encouraging excessive leverage.

### Link 2: Financial Variables $\to$ Macroeconomic Shocks
As risks build (leverage $\uparrow$, bubbles $\uparrow$):
- The **Probability of a Crisis** increases.
- When a shock occurs, the **Financial Accelerator** amplifies the downturn, leading to a much higher **Sacrifice Ratio** than anticipated.

## Policy Implication: Synchronized Tools
To manage these dual links, the central bank must synchronize:
- **Monetary Policy:** To manage current demand.
- **Macroprudential Policy:** To "Mute" the risk-taking and leverage channels of the interest rate.
