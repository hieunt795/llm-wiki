## Thesis

Traditional supply-and-demand theory suggests that prices (interest rates) should move to clear the market. Alexander Düring bóc tách ranh giới của thanh khoản, trong khi Perry Warjiyo (trích dẫn Stiglitz & Weiss, 1981) giải mã cơ chế tại sao **Thị trường tín dụng không bao giờ tự làm sạch**. Vì rủi ro tín dụng tỷ lệ thuận với lãi suất, các ngân hàng hành động theo cách "định mức" (rationing) sản lượng thay vì tăng giá vô hạn. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=71]]

## Mechanism: The Bank-Optimal Rate

A bank's expected return is not a linear function of the interest rate it charges.

### 1. The Revenue-Risk Trade-off
As the lending rate ($r$) increases:
- **Revenue increases** from performing loans.
- **Risk increases** because safe borrowers drop out (Adverse Selection) and existing borrowers take higher risks (Moral Hazard). [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=72]]

### 2. The Peak (Bank-Optimal Rate)
There is a specific interest rate ($r^*$) where the bank's **expected return** is maximized.
- If the bank increases the rate beyond $r^*$, the losses from higher default rates exceed the extra interest income.
- **The Equilibrium:** The bank will set the rate at $r^*$ and refuse to lend to any additional borrowers, even if they are willing to pay more. This creates a state of **Excess Demand** ($Z$) that never disappears. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=73]]

## Strategic Implications: The Failure of Self-Adjustment

Under the Stiglitz paradigm, the economy can reach an equilibrium level of real output that is **below full employment** ($y < y^*$).
- **The Recession Trap:** During a recession, the demand for loans shifts left, but the supply (deposits) shifts left even faster.
- **The Interest Rate Spiral:** Attempting to use the interest rate to clear this gap only increases borrower risk, exacerbating the recession.
- **Policy Response:** This mechanism justifies the use of **Credit-directing** policies and macroprudential easing (like lowering LTV) to "unclog" the rationing bottleneck when the price mechanism fails. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=72-74]]
