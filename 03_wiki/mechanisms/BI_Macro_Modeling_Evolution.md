# [[BI_Macro_Modeling_Evolution]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] The evolution of BI's modeling infrastructure reflects the transition from money-targeting to interest-rate targeting (ITF) and finally to the multi-instrument Policy Mix, requiring increasingly sophisticated New Keynesian and DSGE frameworks.

## Mechanism
[RAW] BI has developed structural macroeconomic models... One of the large-scale structural macroeconomic models... is known as MODBI... used to analyze different macroeconomic variables and their projections in the medium-long run. [[perry_app_9_2.md#page=287]]

[RAW] BI adopted the FPAS approach recommended by the IMF in 2010. Consequently, BI developed a dynamic 'semi' general equilibrium macroeconomic model for policy simulations, namely the ARIMBI model (Aggregate Rational Inflation – Targeting Model for Bank Indonesia). [[perry_app_9_2.md#page=288]]

[LLM] The ARIMBI model allows for the endogenous behavior of risk variables and comprehensive simulation of the policy mix, integrating monetary (interest rates) and macroprudential instruments (Reserve Requirements and LTV ratios).
