## Thesis
The McCallum rule is a feedback-based monetary growth rule that adjusts the target growth rate of the monetary base in response to deviations of nominal output from its desired level and changes in base velocity. [extracted]

## Mechanism
The target growth rate for the monetary base ($\Delta b_t$) is calculated as:
$$\Delta b_t = \Delta x^* - \Delta va_t + \lambda (\Delta x^* - \Delta x_{t-1})$$
where:
- $\Delta x^*$: Constant target growth for nominal output (GDP).
- $\Delta va_t$: Average growth of base velocity over a recent period.
- $\lambda$: Sensitivity parameter for output deviations. [extracted]

### Objectives
- **Base Velocity Correction:** Automatically adjusts for technological or regulatory changes in the financial system that affect how money circulates. [extracted]
- **Countercyclical Response:** If nominal output growth falls below the target, the rule mandates an acceleration in monetary base growth. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- Uses the **Monetary Base** ($M0$) as the operational instrument. [extracted]
- Primarily used when nominal income targeting is the strategic goal.
- Effectiveness is hindered by instability in the money multiplier. [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **McCallum (1988):** Proposed the rule as a quantity-based alternative to interest rate rules. [extracted]
