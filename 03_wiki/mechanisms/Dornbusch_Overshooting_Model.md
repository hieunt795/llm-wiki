## Thesis

Traditional monetary models assume all prices are flexible. Alexander Düring bóc tách ranh giới của rủi ro, trong khi Perry Warjiyo (trích dẫn Dornbusch, 1976) giải mã cơ chế tại sao **Tỷ giá luôn biến động mạnh hơn các biến số cơ bản**. Đây là mô hình nền tảng để hiểu tại sao các cú shock cung tiền lại tạo ra những đợt sóng thần trên thị trường ngoại hối trước khi ổn định ở một mức cân bằng mới. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=88]]

## Mechanism: The Asymmetry of Speed

The core of the model lies in the **Sticky-Price** assumption.

### 1. The Initial Shock
Consider an expansionary monetary shock (increasing $M$). 
- In the Flexible model, all prices ($P$) and the exchange rate ($S$) would rise proportionally immediately.
- In the Dornbusch model, prices of non-traded goods ($P$) are **sticky**—they only adjust with a lag. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=88]]

### 2. The Asset Market Reaction
Because prices are slow to rise, the increase in nominal money supply leads to an increase in *real* money supply ($M/P$). 
- This drives domestic interest rates ($r$) down.
- According to **UIRP (Uncovered Interest Rate Parity)**, if domestic rates are lower than foreign rates, the currency must be expected to **appreciate** in the future. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=88]]

### 3. The Overshooting Result
To allow for a future appreciation while having a higher money supply, the exchange rate must first **depreciate beyond its long-term equilibrium level**.
- **The Leap:** $S$ jumps from $S_0$ to $S_{overshoot}$.
- **The Recovery:** As domestic prices ($P$) slowly catch up and interest rates return to normal, the exchange rate slowly appreciates back toward its long-term (PPP-consistent) level. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=88]]

## Strategic Implications: Central Bank Vigilance

Perry Warjiyo highlights that overshooting creates significant challenges for inflation targeting (ITF):
- **Artificial Volatility:** The jump in $S$ is purely technical but has real-world consequences for importers and exporters.
- **Pass-Through Risk:** The overshot exchange rate can "leak" into domestic inflation expectations through the trade channel before the fundamental equilibrium is reached.
- **Inference:** This mechanism justifies central bank intervention to "smooth" the overshooting path and maintain domestic price stability. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=88]]
