## Thesis

Because a bond represents a long-term lending relationship, the issuer's situation can change dramatically between purchase and maturity. To mitigate this risk, bond documentations (Prospectuses) include specialized legal clauses. Alexander Düring emphasizes that for private or international issues, these **Covenants** are the primary defense against "asset stripping" and "moral hazard." [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch17.md#page=148]]

## The Core Defensive Arsenal

### 1. Negative Pledge (Điều khoản không cầm cố định danh)
- **The Risk:** An issuer may strip its best assets to secure new loans, leaving existing bondholders with only un-pledged "scraps."
- **The Clause:** The issuer promises not to pledge any assets as security for future debt unless they provide the same security to existing bondholders. It ensures a level playing field for all unsecured creditors. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch17.md#page=150]]

### 2. Cross-Default (Vỡ nợ chéo)
- **The Risk:** An issuer defaults on a 2026 bond while your 2030 bond is technically still "current." Without this clause, you would have to wait until 2030 to sue, by which time the 2026 holders would have liquidated all assets.
- **The Clause:** A default on *any* major debt instrument is deemed a default on *all* debt, allowing all bondholders to accelerate their claims simultaneously. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch17.md#page=149]]

### 3. Collective Action Clause - CAC (Hành động tập thể)
- **The Challenge:** Historically, a few "holdout" investors could block a necessary debt restructuring by refusing to accept new terms.
- **The Clause:** Sets rules for a majority decision (e.g., 75% approval) that becomes legally binding on all bondholders, preventing a minority from sabotaging a restructuring. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch17.md#page=149]]

## Structural Arbitrage Clauses

### 1. Soft Bullet (Nợ đạn mềm)
- **Mechanism:** Allows the issuer to extend the bond's maturity date if they are unable to make the redemption payment (a "conditional extension").
- **The Arbitrage:** Used widely in [[Covered_Bonds]] to obtain higher credit ratings. By eliminating the "binary" default event at maturity, the issuer is seen as more stable, even though the investor's right to immediate repayment is sacrificed. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch17.md#page=150]]

### 2. Rating Triggers
- **Mechanism:** Coupons automatically increase if the issuer's credit rating falls below a certain threshold.
- **The Effect:** Protects investors against the price drop (spread widening) that accompanies a downgrade, but effectively subordinates other bondholders whose bonds lack this feature. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch17.md#page=150]]
