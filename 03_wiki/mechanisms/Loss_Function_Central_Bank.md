## Thesis
The central bank loss function is a mathematical representation of the social cost of economic instability, which the central bank seeks to minimize by balancing inflation and output gap deviations. [extracted]

## Mechanism
The standard quadratic loss function ($L$) is defined as:
$$L = E \sum_{t=0}^\infty \delta^t \left[ (\pi_t - \pi^*)^2 + \lambda x_t^2 \right]$$
where:
- $\pi_t - \pi^*$: Inflation deviation from target.
- $x_t$: Output gap.
- $\lambda$: Relative weight on output stabilization.
- $\delta$: Discount factor. [extracted]

### Policy Preferences
- **Strict Inflation Targeting ($\lambda = 0$):** The central bank focuses exclusively on the inflation target ("inflation nutters").
- **Flexible Inflation Targeting ($\lambda > 0$):** The central bank accepts short-term inflation variability to minimize output fluctuations. [extracted]

## Boundaries / Conditions
- **Symmetry:** Treats positive and negative deviations from the target as equally costly. [extracted]
- **Certainty Equivalence:** Under linear-quadratic frameworks, the optimal rule depends on the mean of the variables, not their variance. [extracted]

## Evidence / Source Anchors
- **Svensson (1997):** Formulated the disinflation path using the loss function approach. [extracted]
