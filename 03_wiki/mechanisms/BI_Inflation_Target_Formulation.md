# [[BI_Inflation_Target_Formulation]]

## Thesis
[WIKI] The formulation of inflation targets in Indonesia balances central bank technical expertise with government policy ownership, transitioning from "goal independence" to a "goal dependent" but coordinated framework to enhance policy credibility and alignment.

## Mechanism
[RAW] Act No. 23 of 1999... provides discretion to the Government and BI in terms of formulating the inflation target as the final goal of monetary policy... the announcement of the inflation target was changed from BI itself in pursuance of Act No. 23 of 1999 to the Government after consultation with BI in accordance with Act No. 3 of 2004. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=264]]

[RAW] Since 2002... the inflation target was set for all CPI components to facilitate greater public understanding... Currently, the government’s inflation target, determined after coordination with BI, is set at 4% ± 1% for 2016 and 2017, and 3.5% ± 1% for 2018. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=265]]

[LLM] While Headline CPI is the public target, Bank Indonesia utilizes core inflation (omitting administered prices and volatile supply shocks) as the primary indicator for monetary policy response to avoid excessive reactions to non-monetary shocks.
