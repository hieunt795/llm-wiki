## Thesis

In any fractional reserve banking system, the ability of banks to create money through lending is not infinite. It is structurally bounded by the **Reserve Ratio**—the required amount of foundational reserves (Specie or Central Bank Cash) held to satisfy potential withdrawal demands. Alexander Düring argues that in a metal-based system, this ratio creates a "straightjacket" that tethers economic expansion to the physical extraction rate of precious metals.

## Mechanism

The mechanism operates as a mathematical limit on the monetary multiplier.

### 1. The Redemption Requirement
To function as a reliable means of payment, bank notes or deposits (Inside Money) must be perceived as convertible into the underlying reserve (Outside Money). Banks must therefore hold a stock of reserves designed to:
- **Redemption:** Cover customer requests to withdraw physical cash.
- **Interbank Settlement:** Settle obligations with other banks who may not accept the issuing bank's private liabilities. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch02.md#page=15-16]]

### 2. The Ratio Calculation
The **Reserve Ratio** is defined as:
$$\text{Reserve Ratio} = \frac{\text{Reserves Held}}{\text{Total Bank Liabilities (Banknotes/Deposits) Outstanding}}$$

### 3. The Monetary Multiplier Effect
Because this ratio cannot easily be altered in a commodity-based system (due to the physical scarcity of metal), the amount of money a bank can create is limited by what the economy can source in terms of reserve metal. 
- **Fixed Supply:** If the stock of gold is fixed, and the reserve ratio is 10%, the total money supply is effectively capped at 10 times the gold stock.
- **Expansion Limit:** This creates a structural "straightjacket" where economic activity cannot expand faster than the mining of the reserve commodity. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch02.md#page=16]]

### Boundaries / Conditions

### The Transition to Fiat
In a fiat system, the central bank can alter the reserve ratio or increase the supply of reserves at will. This removes the physical straightjacket but introduces the risk of **Inflationary Policies**. sovereigns historically replaced metal-based currencies with fiat money often to raise funds at the expense of the population through deliberate debasement, particularly during wartime. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch02.md#page=16]]

### The International Friction
While domestic laws can regulate reserve ratios, international trade is governed by physical flows. A large trade deficit under a gold standard requires physical gold outflows, which forces a contraction of the domestic monetary base through this reserve mechanism, regardless of the desired domestic policy. [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch02.md#page=16]]
