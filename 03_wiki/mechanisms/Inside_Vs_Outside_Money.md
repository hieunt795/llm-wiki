## Thesis

The dual-layer architecture of modern money creates a fundamental tension between absolute settlement assets and credit-based claims. Alexander Düring bóc tách ranh giới kỹ thuật giữa người được ủy thác (trustee) và chủ ngân hàng (banker), trong khi Perry Warjiyo (trích dẫn Gurley & Shaw, 1960) vạch trần tại sao thay đổi tỷ trọng giữa hai loại tiền này lại có thể phá vỡ tính trung lập của tiền. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch02.md#page=14]] [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=51]]

---

## 1. Outside Money (The Anchor)
**Outside Money** consists of assets that are not the liability of any private domestic entity. 
- **Specie:** In a commodity system, physical gold or silver bullions are the primary outside money. They have "intrinsic" value and exist independent of any contract.
- **Sovereign Cash:** In a fiat system, the central bank's physical notes and reserve balances function as outside money. They represent a claim on the state but are treated as the "base" asset of the private economy. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch02.md#page=14]]

## 2. Inside Money (The Credit Claim)
**Inside Money** consists of the liabilities of the private banking sector.
- **Creation:** When a bank issues a loan, it creates a deposit on its own ledger. This deposit is "Inside Money"—it is a debt certificate payable on demand.
- **The Banking Transition:** The moment a "trustee" (holding 100% of an asset) begins to lend a portion of that asset to a third party while the original owner still treats their claim as liquid, the trustee becomes a **Banker**, and the asset transforms into Inside Money. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch02.md#page=14]]

---

## 3. Macro-Economic Dynamics (Gurley & Shaw)

Perry Warjiyo highlights that the distinction is not merely academic but central to monetary transmission.
- **The Assets/Liabilities Ratio:** If the public holds both types of money, a change in the stock of one type (e.g., central bank printing Outside Money) without a corresponding change in the other alters the net worth and portfolio ratios of the private sector.
- **Breaking Neutrality:** This shift triggers changes in the relative prices of assets and, therefore, real output. Changes in the *composition* of money matter as much as the *quantity*. [[Perry_Central_Bank_Policy_P3.md#page=51]]

---

## Boundaries / Conditions: The Fractional Collapse
Because Inside Money is created through lending, its total volume typically dwarfs the supply of Outside Money. This creates the structural vulnerability known as the **Bank Run**:
1.  Holders of Inside Money simultaneously attempt to convert their claims into Outside Money.
2.  The fixed supply of Outside Money is insufficient to satisfy the demand.
3.  Interchangeability at Par fails, leading to a systemic liquidity freeze. [[During_Fixed_Income_Ch02.md#page=14]]
