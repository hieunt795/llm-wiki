# Bank Treasury Glossary

## Thesis
A specialized dictionary of terminology used in dealing rooms and treasury operations, providing the semantic foundation for understanding FX, money markets, and risk management. / Một từ điển chuyên biệt về thuật ngữ được sử dụng trong phòng giao dịch và hoạt động nguồn vốn, cung cấp nền tảng ngữ nghĩa để hiểu về FX, thị trường tiền tệ và quản trị rủi ro.


This glossary contains essential terminology and dealing jargon used in the treasury environment, primarily based on the ACI Dealing Certificate curriculum.

## A
*   **Ante-spot**: Value dates in the short dates before the spot date (e.g., Today and Tomorrow). Rules for calculating outright exchange rates are reversed to achieve the desired ante-spot rate.
*   **Appreciation**: Describes a currency strengthening in response to market demand.
*   **Arbitrage**: Simultaneous buying and selling of a currency or instrument in different markets to take advantage of temporary rate discrepancies.
*   **Around par**: Used in forward quotations when the two currencies' interest rates are equal (considering the bid-offer spread), meaning the forward price is quoted either side of parity with the spot rate.
*   **(At) your risk**: Quoted rates by a bank or broker are subject to change.

## B
*   **Bankers’ acceptance**: A commercial bill of exchange or draft that becomes a money market instrument when accepted (endorsed and guaranteed) by a bank.
*   **Base currency**: The reference currency in an exchange rate quotation (e.g., in GBP/USD, GBP is the base currency).
*   **Base rate**: The benchmark rate used by commercial banks to calculate rates for depositors and borrowers.
*   **Basis**: In financial futures, the cash price minus the futures price.
*   **Basis point (bps)**: 0.01 per cent.
*   **Bear**: A speculator who sells short expecting a devaluation or depreciation.
*   **Bear squeeze**: Official action making it costly or difficult to maintain short positions.
*   **Best order**: A firm order to deal left with a counterparty without a stipulated rate limit.
*   **Bid (FX)**: The rate at which the market is prepared to buy the base currency.
*   **Bid (Money Market)**: The rate at which a bank is prepared to borrow funds.
*   **Big figure**: The first few digits of an exchange rate quotation (e.g., in 1.4500, "1.45" is the big figure).
*   **Broken date**: A value date in the future that is not a standard fixed period (forward) date.
*   **Broker**: Intermediary who negotiates deals between banks but does not act as a principal.

## C
*   **Cable**: Dealers' jargon for the GBP/USD exchange rate.
*   **Call option**: A contract giving the right (but not obligation) to buy a currency.
*   **Cap**: A sequence of interest rate options providing protection against an increase in interest rates (fixing a maximum rate).
*   **Capital adequacy**: Minimum levels of capital required to support risk assets (Basel standards).
*   **CD (Certificate of Deposit)**: A negotiable receipt for a deposit payable to the bearer.
*   **CHAPS**: Clearing House Automated Payments System (UK sterling).
*   **CHIPS**: Clearing House Interbank Payments System (US dollar, New York).
*   **Classic repo**: A sale and repurchase agreement governed by standard documentation (TBMA/ISMA).
*   **Clean price**: The market quoted price of a bond excluding accrued interest.
*   **Close-out**: Canceling a remaining balance of an option or position by executing an equal and opposite trade.
*   **CLS (Continuous Linked Settlement)**: A multi-lateral netting process for settling FX transactions to mitigate settlement risk.
*   **Collar**: Combination of a cap and a floor to limit interest rate exposure within a range.
*   **Continental (International) terms**: Quoting forward exchange prices in terms of the quoted currency's premium/discount (vs. London terms).
*   **Correspondent bank**: A bank in another center with which a bank maintains an account relationship (Nostro/Vostro).
*   **Cross rate**: The exchange rate between two foreign currencies not involving the US dollar or sterling (in the traditional sense).

## D
*   **Deal date**: The date the transaction is agreed.
*   **Dealer (Trader)**: Specialist authorized to effect transactions and take speculative positions within limits.
*   **Delivery risk**: The risk that a bank pays out a currency sold without receiving the currency purchased.
*   **Delta**: The ratio measuring the change in an option's price relative to the underlying instrument's price.
*   **Depreciation**: Currency losing value due to market forces.
*   **Direct quotation**: Quoting variable amounts of domestic currency against a fixed amount of foreign currency.
*   **Dirty price**: Clean price plus accrued interest.
*   **Discount**: When a currency is cheaper to purchase forward than for spot delivery.

## E
*   **Either-way price (Choice price)**: A quote where the bid and offer are the same.
*   **Eligible bills**: Bills of exchange eligible for re-discount at the Central Bank (e.g., Bank of England).
*   **End/end rule**: If a spot deal falls on the last working day of a month, the forward deal matures on the last working day of the future month.
*   **EURIBOR**: Euro Interbank Offered Rate (benchmark for EUR).
*   **Exchange Position**: The risk taken due to changes in currency values (vs. Cash Position).

## F
*   **Fed funds**: Short-term funds market in the US for banks with excess reserves.
*   **Financial futures**: Legal agreement to buy/sell a standard quantity of an instrument at a future date on an exchange.
*   **Firm**: A committed price for a specific time and amount.
*   **Fixed date**: Standard periods for FX and money market operations (e.g., 1, 3, 6 months).
*   **Fixing date (FRA)**: The date the contract rate is compared to the benchmark (e.g., LIBOR) to determine settlement.
*   **Floor**: Interest rate option fixing a minimum interest rate receivable.
*   **Forward**: Exchange deals over 7 days from spot.
*   **Forward/Forward**: An FX swap or money market operation where both value dates are in the future.
*   **FRA (Forward Rate Agreement)**: Contract to protect against future interest rate moves over a notional period.
*   **Front Office**: The dealing room and its immediate support.

## G
*   **Gamma**: Measure of the change in delta relative to the underlying price.
*   **Gap structure**: The maturity profile of mismatches in a book.
*   **GC (General Collateral)**: Repo trade aimed at borrowing/lending money secured against high-quality government bonds.

## H
*   **Haircut (Initial Margin)**: The excess value of collateral over the loan amount in a repo.
*   **Hedging**: Offsetting risk positions to protect against adverse market movements.
*   **Hot money**: Short-term capital flows driven by interest rates and exchange rate expectations.

## I
*   **Indirect quotation**: Quoting variable amounts of foreign currency against a fixed unit of domestic currency.
*   **Interest arbitrage**: Linking FX and money market transactions to create synthetic assets/liabilities.
*   **IRS (Interest Rate Swap)**: Exchange of interest flows (fixed vs. floating) on a notional principal.
*   **Intervention**: Central bank entering the market to support or influence a currency's value.
*   **Intra-day position**: Exposure limits allowed during the trading day.

## L
*   **Leads and Lags**: Timing of payments/receipts by corporates to benefit from expected currency moves.
*   **Leverage**: Gaining exposure to a large position with a small initial investment (e.g., futures).
*   **LIBOR**: London Interbank Offered Rate (benchmark).
*   **Limit**: Maximum authorized dealing amount to control credit and trading risk.
*   **Liquidity (Market)**: Availability of constant prices and counterparties.
*   **Long**: Excess of purchases over sales (overbought).

## M
*   **Mark to Market (MtM)**: Revaluing positions using current market prices to assess profit/loss.
*   **Market Maker**: Institution prepared to make two-way prices.
*   **Mine**: Jargon for "I buy" or "I take" the offer.
*   **Mismatch**: Offsetting positions that do not correspond by maturity.

## N
*   **NOP (Net Open Position)**: The difference between total assets and total nợ (liabilities) in a currency.
*   **Nostro**: "Our" account held with another bank in their local currency.
*   **Novation**: Legal method of transferring ownership/obligations by creating a new contract.

## O
*   **Offered (FX)**: The rate at which the market is prepared to sell the base currency.
*   **Open Interest**: Number of outstanding futures/options contracts.
*   **Outright**: A forward deal not forming part of a swap.
*   **OTC (Over the Counter)**: Instruments traded directly between parties rather than on an exchange.
*   **Overnight (O/N)**: Value today against tomorrow.

## P
*   **Point (Pip)**: The smallest unit of price movement (usually 4th decimal place).
*   **Premium**:
    1.  Price paid for an option.
    2.  When a currency is more expensive forward than spot.
*   **Put option**: Right to sell a currency.

## Q
*   **Quoted currency**: The currency used to value the base currency.

## R
*   **RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement)**: System requiring immediate settlement of payments to reduce systemic risk.
*   **Repo**: Sale and repurchase agreement.
*   **Reverse Repo**: Purchase and resale agreement (from the lender's perspective).
*   **Roll-over**: Extending the maturity of a position or fixing a new interest rate.

## S
*   **Secondary Market**: Market for instruments that have already been issued.
*   **Settlement date**: The date cash changes hands.
*   **Short**: Excess of sales over purchases (oversold).
*   **Spot**: Cash settlement usually two business days from deal date.
*   **Spread**: Difference between the bid and offer prices.
*   **Square**: When purchases and sales are equal (zero net position).
*   **STIR**: Short-term interest rate futures.
*   **Stop-loss order**: Instruction to close a position if the market moves beyond a certain point to limit losses.
*   **Swap (FX)**: Simultaneous purchase and sale of identical amounts of a currency for different value dates.

## T
*   **TARGET**: Trans-European Automated Real-time Gross settlement Express Transfer system (Euro payments).
*   **Tom/Next (T/N)**: Tomorrow against the next day (Spot).
*   **Two-way price**: Quote showing both bid and offer rates.

## V
*   **Value date**: The contracted date for delivery or effectiveness of a deal.
*   **Vega**: Sensitivity of an option's price to changes in volatility.
*   **Vostro**: "Your" account held by a correspondent bank with "us" in our local currency.

## Y
*   **Yard**: Dealer slang for one billion (1,000,000,000) units.
*   **Yours**: Jargon for "I sell" or "I give" at your bid.

## Z
*   **Zero coupon**: A bond with no coupons, issued at a deep discount.
