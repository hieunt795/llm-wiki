## Thesis

The success of the "Operational Framework" depends on managing these lags. If the central bank waits until it sees inflation to act, it is already too late.

## The Lag Structure

### 1. Inside Lag (NHTW-centric)
- **Recognition Lag:** Time taken to realize a shock has happened (due to data delays).
- **Decision Lag:** Time for the Board of Governors to meet and agree on a response.
- *Note:* Rule-based policy (Taylor Rule) drastically reduces the Decision Lag.

### 2. Outside Lag (Economy-centric)
- **Transmission Lag:** Time for interest rate changes to move from interbank rates to lending/deposit rates.
- **Impact Lag:** Time for households and firms to change behavior (12-18 months).

## Research Insight: Informational Requirements
Perry cites Blake (2000), arguing that central banks should adopt **generic policy rules** with error-correction mechanisms to minimize the impact of these lags during periods of high uncertainty.
