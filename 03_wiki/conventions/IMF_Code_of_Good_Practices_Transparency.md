## Thesis

Following the crises of the late 1990s, the IMF recognized that **Secrecy** contributed to systemic collapse. The 1999 Code provides a checklist for member states to ensure their central banks operate in a way that minimizes asymmetric information and maximizes public trust.

## Core Pillars of the Code

1. **Clarity of Roles:** Explicitly defining the legal mandate, jurisdiction, and relationship between the central bank and other financial authorities (e.g., Treasury, OJK).
2. **Open Process:** Disclosure of the policy formulation and reporting processes (why and how decisions are made).
3. **Information Availability:** Providing high-frequency economic and financial data to the public in a standardized format.
4. **Accountability and Integrity:** Clear procedures for public testimony, audited financial statements, and a statutory **Code of Ethics** for employees.

## Implementation: The "Understandable, Accessible, and Timely" Rule
Transparency is defined by Sundarajan et al. (2003) as an environment where information is:
- **Understandable:** Avoids overly technical jargon for the public.
- **Accessible:** Available via multiple channels (web, media, library).
- **Timely:** Published immediately after decisions or as data is realized.
