## Thesis

The purpose of OTC trading conventions is to reduce the incidence of misunderstandings through **deliberate redundancy**. By using standard language for bids, offers, and confirmations, counterparties ensure that both sides are clear about what has taken place, even in fast-moving markets or via informal channels like chat or phone. ## Convention

### 1. Acceptance Terms
- **"Mine":** Used by the customer to accept an offer (buy the security). [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=90]]
- **"Yours":** Used by the customer to accept a bid (sell the security). [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=90]]

### 2. Confirmation Protocol
- **"Done" / "OK, done":** The final confirmation by the dealer that the trade is concluded. Until this is stated, no trade exists. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=90-91]]
- **"Off that" / "Off":** Signals that a previously provided quote is no longer valid. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=90]]

### 3. Quote Status
- **"Subject" / "Your Risk":** Indicates that the dealer is no longer bound by the previously provided quote, usually due to market volatility. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=89]]

### 4. Post-Trade Information (In Comp)
Following a competitive quote ("in comp"), dealers are given standard non-monetary rewards: [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=97]]
- **"Cover":** The winner is told the "cover price" (next best bid/offer) to help them judge their risk. The runner-up is told they "were cover".
- **"Traded Away" / "Even":** Losing dealers are told the trade was done elsewhere, often with minimal detail to protect the winner.

### Variants

- **Voice/Chat vs. Electronic:** Electronic platforms use color schemes (e.g., red for sell, blue for buy) to replicate the redundancy of verbal conventions. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=90]]
- **Yield Quoting:** Some markets (e.g., US Treasuries) quote in yields or yield changes rather than prices, which changes the specific vernacular but keeps the "Mine/Yours" logic. [extracted] [[Fixed_Income_Alexander_During_Ch12.md#page=92]]
