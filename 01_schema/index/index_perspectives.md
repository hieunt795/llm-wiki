- [[Fear_Of_Floating]] — **Thesis:** The 'Fear of Floating' describes a systemic and institutional reluctance to accept market-driven volatility, manifesting as aggressive FX interventions by central banks (Macro) and a preference for fixed-rate over floating-rate liabilities by debt issuers (Micro).

- [[Japanese_Castle_Wall_Analogy]] — **Thesis:** The Japanese castle wall analogy illustrates the fundamental trade-off in monetary policy: maintaining an economy perfectly at potential (a 'high' wall) can paradoxically lead to more catastrophic collapses when a shock finally exceeds the central bank's capacity.
- [[Just_Do_It_Monetary_Strategy]] — **Thesis:** The 'Just-Do-It' strategy is a monetary policy approach without an explicit nominal anchor, relying instead on pre-emptive actions and the personal credibility of central bank leadership to maintain low inflation.
- [[Keynesian_Post_Keynesian_Crisis_Theory]] — **Thesis:** Capitalism's inherent uncertainty and speculative volatility in long-term expectations, capital valuation, and credit availability make full employment unstable; crises arise from endogenous swings in animal spirits rather than exogenous shocks.
- [[Macrostability_Without_Financial_Stability_Paradox]] — **Thesis:** The central paradox identified by the GFC is that 'there is no macrostability without financial stability'; long periods of price stability and low interest rates can paradoxically foster systemic instability by encouraging excessive leverage and asset bubbles.

- [[Marxist_Crisis_Theory]] — **Thesis:** Capitalism's internal contradictions—anarchy of production, falling profit rates, and limits on demand—make crises inevitable; crises emerge endogenously as systemic pathologies, not exogenous shocks.

- [[Monetary_Base_Constraint_Specie]] — **Thesis:** The requirement for physical commodity reserves (specie) acts as a structural 'straightjacket' that limits money creation and international trade volumes to the rate of physical commodity discovery, creating unsustainable imbalances in a globalized economy.
- [[Monetary_Policy_Pendulum]] — **Thesis:** The 'Monetary Policy Pendulum' describes the shifting institutional focus of central banks between the pursuit of price stability and the maintenance of financial system stability, driven by historical crises and evolving academic consensus.
- [[Monetary_Policy_Transparency_Future_Paradigm]] — **Thesis:** Monetary policy transparency has become inevitable and central to modern central banking, driven by central bank independence expansion, ITF implementation, and sophisticated financial markets—requiring ongoing refinement of best practices and empirical evaluation of effectiveness.
