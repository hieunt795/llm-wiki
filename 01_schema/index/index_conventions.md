# WIKI INDEX: Conventions

- [[Bond_Pricing_And_Accrued_Interest_Conventions]] — **Thesis:** Bond pricing conventions bifurcate between the quoted 'Clean Price' and the settlement 'Dirty Price', utilizing specialized rounding and fractional rules (like US thirty-seconds) to standardize global trading.
- [[Coupon_Washing_Tax_Strategies]] — **Thesis:** Coupon washing is a tax arbitrage technique using repurchase agreements (Repo) to transfer bond ownership to a tax-exempt entity just before coupon payment, converting a taxable gross coupon into a non-taxable manufactured dividend.
- [[Danish_Balance_Principle_And_Mortgage_Delivery]] — **Thesis:** The Danish covered bond market utilizes a strict 'Balance Principle' to link individual mortgages 1-to-1 with funding bonds, granting retail borrowers a unique 'Delivery Option' to cancel debt by purchasing their own bonds at market discounts.
- [[Daycount_Conventions]] — **Thesis:** Daycount conventions are standardized rules for calculating the number of days in an interest period and a year, originally designed to simplify manual calculation but resulting in systematic discrepancies in effective interest rates.
- [[FX_Forward_Quoting_Conventions]] — **Thesis:** FX Forward rates are typically quoted as forward points (pips) relative to the spot rate, rather than outright rates, reflecting the interest rate differential between two currencies.
- [[IMF_Code_of_Good_Practices_Transparency]] — **Thesis:** The IMF Code of Good Practices on Transparency in Monetary and Financial Policies (1999) establishes international standards for the clarity, accessibility, and accountability of central bank operations.
- [[Inflation_Target_Formulation]] — **Thesis:** Formulation của inflation target là convention kỹ thuật gồm 3 lựa chọn — chỉ số giá nào (Headline vs Core), hình thức số (Point vs Range), và horizon mục tiêu — cân bằng giữa controllability và credibility. New Zealand tiên phong với range + escape clauses; EMEs như Indonesia tiến hóa từ Core sang Headline CPI để tăng tính minh bạch công chúng.
- [[ISIN_And_CUSIP_Identification_Architecture]] — **Thesis:** Securities identification systems like ISIN and CUSIP utilize standardized alphanumeric codes with built-in check-digit algorithms to enable automated settlement and eliminate human error in global trading.
- [[Islamic_Finance_Constraints]] — **Thesis:** Islamic Finance (Shar'iah) fundamentally prohibits interest-based lending (riba), excessive uncertainty (gharar), and gambling/speculation (maysir), necessitating the development of equity-like or asset-backed structures for fixed income participation.
- [[Joint_And_Several_Liability_Sovereign_Bonds]] — **Thesis:** When multiple entities combine to issue a unified 'Joint Bond', the systemic credit risk depends entirely on the liability structure chosen: while 'Several Liability' isolates default risk back to individual members, 'Joint and Several Liability' establishes absolute cross-mutualization, a mechanism currently explicitly outlawed in the European Union under constitutional sovereignty constraints.
- [[LIBOR_And_Term_Benchmark_Fragility]] — **Thesis:** The global transition from LIBOR to overnight benchmarks represents a fundamental shift from opinion-based term rates to transaction-based compounded rates, necessitated by the collapse of the unsecured interbank term lending market.
- [[Mathematical_Conventions_Of_Interest_Accrual]] — **Thesis:** The conversion of an annualized interest rate quote into actual physical cash requires the rigid application of pre-electronic computing conventions (Daycount Fractions) and the explicit mathematical choice between 'Simple Interest' division and 'Discount Margin' subtraction, fundamentally altering the terminal payout value of the asset.
- [[Monetary_Policy_Implementation_Lags]] — **Thesis:** Monetary policy lags are divided into the 'Inside Lag' (time to recognize and take action) and the 'Outside Lag' (time for the action to affect the economy), which together determine the required lead time for pre-emptive policy.
- [[Monetary_Policy_Lags_Profile]] — **Thesis:** Monetary policy lags are the time delays between the implementation of a policy action and its impact on terminal economic variables (CPI/GDP), typically categorized into a short financial market lag and a long real economy lag.
- [[OTC_Trading_Conventions]] — **Thesis:** OTC trading relies on a terse, highly standardized vernacular—such as 'Mine', 'Yours', and 'Done'—to ensure clarity and redundancy in agreement protocols.
- [[Preferred_Habitat_Market_Distortions]] — **Thesis:** Preferred habitat distortions occur when institutional investors (pension funds, insurance, ETFs) operate under structural or regulatory mandates that force non-diversifiable demand for specific maturity sectors, overriding pure economic risk/return considerations.
- [[Prepayment_Risk_Tranching_And_PACs]] — **Thesis:** Prepayment risk tranching is a structural methodology that partitions the timing uncertainty of cashflows into distinct bond classes, utilizing Planned Amortisation Certificates (PACs) to provide duration certainty to senior investors by diverting cashflow volatility to support tranches.
- [[Wash_Sale_Rules_Tactics]] — **Thesis:** Financial engineers use synthetic instruments (like combinations of options) to circumvent wash-sale rules, allowing investors to realize capital losses for tax purposes while maintaining their market exposure.
